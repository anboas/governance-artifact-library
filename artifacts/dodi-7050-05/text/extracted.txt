Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5106.01 and pursuant to Chapter 4 of
Title 5, United States Code (U.S.C), (also known and referred to in this issuance as the “Inspector
General Act of 1978, as amended,”) this issuance:
• Establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for the coordination of
possible criminal, civil, administrative, and contractual remedies to be pursued in response to evidence
of fraud or corruption stemming from criminal, civil, and administrative investigations related to DoD
procurement activities.
• Implements the requirement to report appropriate information about defective products and product
substitution to the Government-Industry Data Exchange Program in accordance with DoD Instruction
(DoDI) 5200.49.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to:

    a. OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff
and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department
of Defense (IG DoD), the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

    b. Criminal and civil investigations conducted by Defense Criminal Investigative
Organizations (DCIOs) or other criminal investigative organizations, which is defined in the
Glossary. Criminal and civil investigations may be supported by administrative investigation
activities.

   c. Administrative investigations conducted by DoD Components with activities that do not
have law enforcement authority (e.g., a DoD Component inspector general or inquiry officer).


1.2. POLICY.

    a. DoD Components maintain awareness of all investigations of fraud or corruption related
to procurement activities affecting their organization.

       (1) The awareness must ensure that all possible criminal, civil, contractual, and
administrative remedies are identified to cognizant procurement and command officials and to
Department of Justice (DOJ) officials, as appropriate, and that appropriate remedies are pursued
expeditiously. This must include coordination with all other affected DoD Components.

         (2) The awareness efforts will not infringe on the independence of the IG DoD or an
inspector general of a Defense Intelligence Component and will not interfere in the criminal,
civil, or administrative investigation.

    b. When a DCIO notifies a DoD Component of an investigation of fraud or corruption
related to procurement activities, the centralized organization for the affected DoD Component
will coordinate with appropriate agencies so the affected DoD Component can determine and
pursue the appropriate contractual and administrative remedies to recover funds lost through
fraud or corruption and to ensure the integrity of DoD programs and operations.

    c. Appropriate criminal, civil, contractual, and administrative remedies are to be pursued as
early as possible to support successful remedies, as specified in Section 4.

   d. Notice of all significant allegations of fraud or corruption involving programs, persons,
and organizations affiliated with the DoD are provided to the centralized organization for the

   e. All confirmed cases of counterfeit materiel are investigated and appropriate remedies are

                            SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. IG DOD.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraphs 2.4. and 2.5., the IG DoD:

   a. Oversees and evaluates, as the IG DoD considers appropriate, DoD Component
compliance with this issuance and specifically monitors Defense Criminal Investigative Service
compliance.

    b. Requests that the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment
(USD(A&S)) designate one of the affected DoD Components (identified as having interest in the
matter) to be the lead DoD Component regarding significant investigations of fraud or
corruption, when necessary and as described in Paragraphs 2.2. and 5.3.

   c. Manages the DoD Contractor Disclosure Program in accordance with DoDI 5505.15.

     d. Ensures the DoD Component heads are notified of fraud affecting their Component, even
if the fraud does not meet the threshold for significant investigations of fraud or corruption.


2.2. USD(A&S).

The USD(A&S):

    a. Designates one of the affected DoD Components as the lead DoD Component when more
than one DoD Component is affected by alleged fraud or corruption and when requested by the
IG DoD. In such cases, the USD(A&S) directs the other affected DoD Component(s) to support
the designated lead DoD Component, as described in Paragraph 5.3.

   b. Acts as the principal point of contact for all matters relating to the prevention, detection,
reporting, and disposition of counterfeit materiel, including counterfeit risk mitigation, in
accordance with DoDIs 4140.01 and 4140.67.

    c. Develops training materials on fraud and corruption in the procurement process and that
all procurement and procurement-related training includes instruction on fraud and corruption
appropriate for the duration and nature of the training.


2.3. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR INTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY
(USD(I&S)).

Other than for the Defense Criminal Investigative Service, which operates under the independent
direction and control of the IG DoD, the USD(I&S), as the designated Principal Staff Assistant
for law enforcement:

   a. May monitor DoD Component execution of this issuance.

2.4. DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The DoD Component heads:

   a. Ensure compliance with this issuance and prescribe additional DoD Component
procedures as necessary to implement this policy.

   b. Ensure all occurrences of suspected or confirmed counterfeit materiel are reported to the
appropriate DCIO or other criminal investigative organization at the earliest opportunity, in
accordance with DoDI 4140.67.

    c. Designate a centralized organization to maintain awareness of each significant
investigation of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities affecting their organization
and take necessary action to ensure the coordination of criminal, civil, contractual, and
administrative remedies. The awareness efforts will not infringe on the independence of the IG
DoD or an inspector general of a Defense Intelligence Component and will not interfere in any
criminal, civil, or administrative investigation.

    d. Direct centralized organization(s) that were affected by alleged fraud or corruption to
fully support the centralized organization of the designated lead DoD Component, in all cases
involving allegations affecting more than one DoD Component, in accordance with Paragraph
2.2.a.

    e. Establish procedures for coordination of criminal, civil, contractual, and administrative
remedies in connection with investigations of fraud or corruption related to procurement
activities, in accordance with Paragraph 3.6. and Section 4.

    f. Establish procedures enabling the centralized organization(s) to ensure that safety and
readiness issues are examined and appropriately addressed for all cases in which a notice is
required in accordance with Paragraph 5.2.a. Paragraph 5.2. outlines the minimum procedures to
be followed by the centralized organization(s) in all cases involving allegations of
non-conforming product(s), defective product(s), product substitution, or counterfeit materiel.

    g. Direct that the appropriate authorized official of an agency within the DoD Component be
provided sufficient information about alleged fraud or corruption (not including grand jury
information subject to Rule 6(e) of the Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure or contained in court
sealed documents or under court orders) to evaluate risk, preserve evidence, determine if further
inquiry is warranted on their part to prevent reoccurrence or detect other possible fraud within
their activity, and otherwise take actions to protect the U.S. Government’s interests.

2.5. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND THE IG DOD.

2.6. INSPECTORS GENERAL OF DEFENSE INTELLIGENCE COMPONENTS.

In accordance with the authority in the Inspector General Act of 1978, as amended, and in
addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the inspectors general of the Defense
Intelligence Components will ensure their respective staff:

    a. Conducts investigations of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities within
their respective DoD Component.

    b. Notifies the appropriate DCIO(s) or other criminal investigative organization(s) when a
potential criminal or civil offense is identified. Provides or coordinates the DoD Component
response to victim impact statements when requested by the DCIOs or other criminal
investigative organizations.

    c. Notifies the respective DoD Component when a fraud investigation is initiated, referred to
another organization, or completed in coordination with the respective DCIO for criminal and
civil investigation matters. The notifications may be delayed in exceptional circumstances, such
as when notification could adversely affect an investigation.

    d. Reports to the U.S. Attorney General, or other appropriate official within the DOJ, when
the respective inspector general of a Defense Intelligence Component has reasonable grounds to
believe there has been a violation of Federal criminal law as required by Chapter 4, Section
404(d), of Title 5, U.S.C. When a criminal investigative organization is involved in the matter,
that organization will be the lead for making reports to the U.S. Attorney General or other
appropriate DOJ official.

    e. Notifies the IG DoD of all credible allegations of violations of criminal or civil statutes
involving DoD personnel, property, activities, or contracts affiliated with the Defense
Intelligence Components involving fraud, theft, or violations of Sections 3729 through 3733 of
Title 31, U.S.C. (also known as the False Claims Act), and kickbacks, bribery, statutory conflicts
of interest, defective products, and product substitution.

    f. Provides the DoD Component head information necessary for the DoD Component to
determine and implement remedial or disciplinary action.

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. CENTRALIZED ORGANIZATION NOTIFICATION.

In support of all significant investigations of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities
affecting its organization, the DCIO or other criminal investigative organization, as
independently aligned under the IG DoD or a Military Department, will:

   a. Immediately notify the centralized organization(s) in writing of the start of all significant
investigations of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities, except as specified in
Paragraph 3.1.c. The initial notification must include:

       (1) Case title (e.g., name of the DoD contractor or subcontractor and corresponding
unique entity identifier (UEI) number).

       (2) Case control number.

       (3) Investigative agency.

        (4) Office of primary responsibility, including the name and telephone number of the
investigating official.

       (5) Date opened.

       (6) Factual basis for the investigation.

       (7) Suspected offense(s).

    b. Immediately notify the Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency of any
investigation that develops information that could impact DoD-cleared industrial facilities or
personnel.

   c. Delay notification of an investigation when such notification would reveal sensitive law
enforcement sources or methods used or when the DOJ determines that a notification will
compromise an ongoing criminal or civil investigation.

         (1) The decision to delay the notification of an investigation applies to the investigating
DCIO or other criminal investigative organizations, audit organizations, and other DoD
Component administrative investigative activities that become involved in supporting a criminal
or civil investigation.

       (2) When notification of an investigation is delayed, the notification will be provided
promptly to the centralized organization by the investigative organization as soon as possible
when the notification will no longer compromise or negatively impact the investigation.

        (3) The intent of the requirement to notify the centralized organization of significant
investigations of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities is to enable information

3.2. U.S. ATTORNEY AND DOJ PRESENTATION.

The investigating DCIO or other criminal investigative organization will notify the criminal and
civil sections of the respective U.S. Attorney’s office or the DOJ of all fraud and corruption
investigations when appropriate.


3.3. GRAND JURY.

For all significant investigations of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities, the
investigating DCIO or other criminal investigative organization will immediately share all
available non-grand jury investigative information with the centralized organization(s).

   a. Non-grand jury investigative techniques are encouraged whenever possible so that non-
grand jury investigative information may be shared with the centralized organization(s), to
enable the centralized organization(s) to use such information in civil, administrative, and
contractual remedies.

   b. The investigating DCIO or other criminal investigative organization should only use
grand jury investigative techniques, such as using grand jury testimony and grand jury
subpoenas, when other investigative techniques are unsuccessful or are deemed inappropriate
based on investigative circumstances.


3.4. CENTRALIZED ORGANIZATION UPDATES.

The investigating DCIO or other criminal investigative organization will provide the centralized
organization(s):

    a. Routine updates about the status of significant investigations of fraud or corruption related
to procurement activities and coordination between investigators and the prosecution authority.
The routine updates may be withheld when the information would reveal sensitive law
enforcement sources or methods used or when the information may compromise an ongoing
criminal or civil investigation.

   b. Any completed reports of investigation in accordance with the DCIO’s or other criminal
investigative organization’s established reporting requirements and procedures.

3.5. OTHER DCIO AND CRIMINAL INVESTIGATIVE ORGANIZATION
COORDINATION AND ACTION.

The investigating DCIO or other criminal investigative organization will:

     a. With concurrence of the prosecutor involved, provide appropriate procurement officials,
commanders, and suspension and debarment officials, along with their respective centralized
organization(s), with any court records, documents, or other evidence of fraud or corruption
related to procurement activities when such information is needed to enable consideration of
potential applicable remedies. Such information will be provided expeditiously, at the
appropriate time in the investigation, to enable the suspension and debarment official to consider
suspension and debarment action in a timely manner, in accordance with the applicable remedies
listed in Section 4.

    b. Provide the prosecution authority with information about any adverse impact on a DoD
mission gathered in accordance with Paragraph 3.5.c. at the earliest opportunity. Such
information can also be used in preparing a victim impact statement for use in sentencing
proceedings.

    c. At the earliest practical point in the investigation, and whenever possible without reliance
on grand jury subpoenas or other court-sealed documents, gather relevant information on:

       (1) Individual(s) potentially involved in the matter under investigation, including
compiling any civil, contractual, or administrative remedies that may be pursued.

         (2) Organizational structure, finances, and contract history of DoD contractors or
subcontractors potentially involved in the matter under investigation; including compiling any
civil, contractual, or administrative remedies that may be pursued.

   d. Provide timely notice to other cognizant DCIO(s) or other criminal investigative
organization(s) of evidence of fraud and corruption affecting other DoD Components.

    e. Ascertain the impact upon any ongoing investigation or prosecution on any contractual or
administrative remedies being considered and advise the appropriate centralized organization(s)
of any adverse impact.

    f. Obtain “Contract Data Reports” located at https://sam.gov/content/contract-data, on every
investigation into non-conforming products, defective products, product substitution, and
counterfeit materiel. Timely notify the centralized organization(s) identified as having contract
actions or performing contract management services with the subject of the investigation.
However, such notification will occur at an appropriate time when it will not adversely impede
or compromise investigative activities.

    g. Coordinate criminal investigations with the appropriate investigative and enforcement
offices in Federal and State departments, agencies, and commissions as appropriate.

3.6. DOD COMPONENT HEAD PROCEDURES.

The DoD Component head’s established procedures for the coordination of criminal, civil,
contractual, and administrative remedies in connection with investigations of fraud or corruption
related to procurement activities must ensure:

    a. The centralized organization regularly discusses matters, such as the status of significant
investigations of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities, with the assigned DCIO(s)
or other criminal investigative organization(s).

    b. That all proper and effective administrative and contractual remedies available to the DoD
are considered and promptly pursued by the necessary DoD officials (e.g., commanders, program
officials, and contracting officers), when found applicable and appropriate.

       (1) Any suspension or debarment action should commence as soon as practicable.

        (2) The centralized organization will ensure that the DCIO(s) or other criminal
investigative organization(s) and the DOJ criminal and civil fraud divisions (previously
identified to the centralized organization as having interest in the matter) are notified in advance
of all proposed remedies to identify and avoid detrimental impact to ongoing investigations.

    c. A case-specific comprehensive remedies plan is developed for each significant
investigation of fraud or corruption related to procurement activities. The remedies plan will
involve the appropriate DCIO(s) or other criminal investigative organization(s) and centralized
organization(s) and possibly DOJ criminal and civil fraud offices, audit agencies, and offices of
inspectors general. The specific comprehensive remedies plan should include information about
possible contractual recoveries and appropriate timing of recovery actions. Any proposed action
by the centralized organization(s) should be discussed with the organizations involved in the
remedies plan.

   d. That the centralized organization(s) identify and document adverse impact(s) on DoD
missions for use in the development of the remedies plan required in Paragraph 3.6.c. when such
adverse impact(s) is determined in significant investigations of fraud or corruption related to
procurement activities.

        (1) This adverse impact information will be furnished to the DCIO or other criminal
investigative organization responsible for the investigation and used by the centralized
organization(s) in the development and preparation of victim impact statement(s) for use in
sentencing proceedings at the request of the DOJ and as provided for in Rule 32(i)(4)(B) of the
Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure and Section 3771 of Title 18, U.S.C.

       (2) Some examples of adverse impact on a DoD mission are endangerment of personnel
or property, monetary loss, denigration of program or personnel integrity, compromise of the
procurement process, counterfeit materiel, and reduction or loss of mission readiness.

    e. That an incident report is submitted to the appropriate adjudication facility for an
assessment and determination of continuing security clearance eligibility for any findings that
cleared personnel perpetrated procurement-related fraud or corruption.

          SECTION 4: REMEDIES IN RESPONSE TO EVIDENCE OF
                       PROCUREMENT FRAUD
Appropriate criminal activity, civil, contractual, and administrative remedies must be pursued as
early as possible to support successful remedies.

4.1. During an investigation and before prosecution or litigation, and when based in whole or in
part on evidence developed during the investigation, contractual and administrative remedies will
be pursued only after advance notice to the responsible DCIO or other criminal investigative
organization and the appropriate legal counsel in the DoD and the DOJ.

4.2. Contractual or administrative remedies may be pursued before final resolution of the
criminal or civil case only after coordination with the responsible DCIO or other criminal
investigative organization and the DOJ have been provided an opportunity to identify potential
adverse impact(s) to the ongoing criminal or civil case.

 SECTION 5: ACTIONS TO TAKE IN NON-CONFORMING PRODUCT,
DEFECTIVE PRODUCT, PRODUCT SUBSTITUTION, AND COUNTERFEIT
                MATERIEL INVESTIGATIONS

5.1. GENERAL.

The actions to take in non-conforming product, defective product, product substitution, and
counterfeit materiel investigations are in support of and in accordance with DoDI 4140.01 and
the Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure.


5.2. DCIO.

In all cases involving allegations of non-conforming product(s), defective product(s), product
substitution, or counterfeit materiel (i.e., suspect product(s)), the DCIOs will:

   a. Immediately notify the appropriate centralized organization(s) of the initiation of the
investigation.

    b. Continue to provide to the centralized organization(s) any information developed during
the investigation that indicates a suspect product(s) has been, or might be, provided to the DoD.

   c. Require that any request for testing of one or more suspect products is provided to the
centralized organization(s).


5.3. CENTRALIZED ORGANIZATIONS.

In all cases involving allegations of non-conforming product(s), defective product(s), product
substitution, or counterfeit materiel, the centralized organization(s) will:

    a. Review the notice of the investigation immediately after receiving it from the DCIO to
determine any potential safety or readiness issues indicated by the suspected fraud in accordance
with Paragraph 3.6.b.

   b. Notify all appropriate safety, procurement, and program officials of the existence of the
investigation.

    c. Ensure that the affected DoD Component(s) provides the DCIO with full testing support
to completely identify the non-conforming, defective, substitute, or counterfeit nature of the
suspect product(s) (the appropriate procurement program will be responsible for costs associated
with the testing).

   d. Upon request, prepare a comprehensive victim impact statement describing the adverse
impact of the fraud on DoD programs for use in any criminal, civil, contractual, or administrative
remedies related to the matter in accordance with Paragraph 3.6.d.

5.4. DESIGNATED LEAD DOD COMPONENT.

In all cases involving allegations of non-conforming product(s), defective product(s), product
substitution(s), or counterfeit materiel that affect more than one DoD Component, the designated
lead DoD Component will identify a lead centralized organization. The designated lead DoD
Component could serve as lead agency for any resulting suspension or debarment action
following coordination in accordance with applicable regulation and DoD guidance.

    a. If the lead DoD Component fails to identify a lead centralized organization, the DCIO
responsible for the investigation will notify the IG DoD. The IG DoD will request that the
USD(A&S) appoint a lead centralized organization. The appointed lead centralized organization
will coordinate to ensure the proper agency (agencies) address any resulting suspension and
debarment matters.

   b. The DoD Component(s) affected by the alleged non-conforming product(s), defective
product(s), product substitution(s), or counterfeit materiel will provide the centralized
organization of the designated lead DoD Component with all the information needed and
requested to support the investigation.

    c. The lead centralized organization will ensure that information on the alleged non-
conforming product(s), defective product(s), product substitution(s), or counterfeit materiel is
provided to all other affected centralized organization(s) and determine whether non-DoD
government organization(s) are also affected, taking the lead for the DoD in coordinating with
those other government organization(s).

    d. The lead centralized organization will ensure compliance with the requirements of
Paragraph 5.3. and assume responsibility for preparing a comprehensive victim impact statement,
as requested. Other centralized organization(s) will cooperate with and support the lead
centralized organization.

   e. At the conclusion of the covert portion of the investigation, and in coordination with the
DCIO, the lead centralized organization will release appropriate information about counterfeit or
non-conforming item(s) to the Government-Industry Data Exchange Program in accordance with
DoDI 5200.49.

TERM                   DEFINITION

counterfeit materiel   An item that is an unauthorized copy or substitute that has been
                       identified, marked, or altered by a source other than the item’s legally
                       authorized source and has been misrepresented to be an authorized
                       item of the legally authorized source. This includes:

                          Both used and refurbished items being sold as new and items
                       being sold under an intentional misrepresentation of the item’s
                       material and capability.

                           Items that have been modified to add malicious or other
                       unapproved hardware or software, or modifications that would void
                       the manufacturer’s warranty.

covert portion         A part of an investigation that has not been openly declared.

criminal               Defined in DoDI 5505.16.
investigations

DCIO                   The Defense Criminal Investigative Service, the Department of the
                       Army Criminal Investigation Division, the Naval Criminal
                       Investigative Service, and the Air Force Office of Special
                       Investigations.

defective product      A product or the component of a product that fails to function or
                       operate in accordance with the terms of a contract, its specifications
                       or drawings, or with applicable Federal or military specifications.

Defense Intelligence   The Defense Intelligence Agency, the National Geospatial-
Components             Intelligence Agency, the National Reconnaissance Office, and the
                       National Security Agency.

kickbacks              Any money, fee, commission, credit, gift, gratuity, thing of value, or
                       compensation of any kind that is provided to a prime contractor,
                       prime contractor employee, subcontractor, or subcontractor employee

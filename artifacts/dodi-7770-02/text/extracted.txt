               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to:

    a. OSD, the Military Departments (including the United States Coast Guard (USCG) at all
times, including when it is a Service in the Department of Homeland Security by agreement with
that Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the
Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the
Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD.

    b. The Commissioned Corps of the United States Public Health Service (USPHS), under
agreement with the Department of Health and Human Services, and the Commissioned Officer
Corps of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), under agreement with
the Department of Commerce.


1.2. POLICY.

DoD will:

    a. Maintain a collection of pay file databases of the pays, allowances, deductions, and
collections of the uniformed services used for:

       (1) Information reports, statistical tabulations, and demographic information of the pays,
allowances, deductions, and collections of the uniformed services for use throughout the DoD,
by other government agencies, and throughout the branches of the Federal Government.

      (2) Policy and program analysis, personnel planning, budget analysis, and evaluation of
DoD programs and policies.

   b. Share uniformed services pay data suitable for research and analysis with the federally
funded research and development centers in accordance with DoD Instruction (DoDI) 5000.77
and DoD research partners for use in DoD-sponsored studies and analysis.

   c. Share uniformed services pay data with the DoD Office of the Actuary for calculations
and valuations for the:

      (1) DoD Military Retirement Fund established in Chapter 74 of Title 10, United States
Code (U.S.C.).

       (2) DoD Education Benefits Fund established in Section 2006 of Title 10, U.S.C.

   d. Share certain uniformed services pay data with the Defense Enrollment Eligibility
Reporting System in accordance with DoDI 1341.02.

   e. Use computer matching programs with other Federal and State agencies, in accordance
with Section 552a of Title 5, U.S.C. (also known as the “Privacy Act of 1974,” as amended,);
DoDI 5400.11; and DoD 5400.11-R.

   f. Use and maintain Social Security numbers (SSNs) in accordance with DoDI 1000.30.

                          SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS
(USD(P&R)).

The USD(P&R) develops and establishes policy and overall guidance for reporting of uniformed
services pay data.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R), the Assistant Secretary of Defense
for Manpower and Reserve Affairs:

   a. Provides policy guidance, procedures, and standards for the administration and
management of DoD human resource policies, plans, and programs for the Military Services.

    b. Develops, publishes, and maintains data reporting requirements; conducts analyses; and
provides guidance and procedures for the collection, integration, and management of the pays,
allowances, deductions, and collections of uniformed services pay data.

   c. Develops and provides clarifying guidance on new and emerging pay reporting
requirements by publishing guidance, procedures, and reporting requirements as necessary to
implement this issuance.

programs and applications, and makes all pay data available to the Beacon Analytic Environment
(BAE).

    c. Provides pay data for use by the federally funded research and development centers and
other government sponsored research partners within the BAE.

    d. Provides query and report capabilities for the pay data. Develops, produces, and
distributes pay-based administrative reports to requesting customers from the BAE.

               SECTION 3: PAY FILE REPORTING PROCEDURES

3.1. UNIFORMED SERVICES DATA.

    a. This section establishes requirements and provides guidance for the uniformed services
pay sites to report the data items or derived data items that are available on existing authoritative
uniformed services automated information pay processing systems.

   b. DMDC processing will retain an original unchanged version of the uniformed services
pay data, after processing any specified reject and edit conditions mentioned in this issuance.


3.2. REPORT INSTRUCTIONS.

Each pay site will enter an ICD with the DMDC to specify reporting requirements, data
specifications, file naming conventions, and submission requirements. Additionally, the DMDC
will specify processing guidelines and management reports to summarize the submission for
each pay site.

   a. Report Requirements.

   Each pay site will:

       (1) Submit data to the DMDC via secure electronic transmission in compliance with the
data submission requirements of this issuance.

       (2) Submit pay data by uniformed service component associations within 5 calendar days
of each month’s end close out date.

        (3) Report one pay record for each member who is on its payroll or has left the payroll
since the previous month’s close-out date.

   b. Data Specification.

   Each pay site will report pay data with specifications and formats referenced in Table 3 as:

       (1) Reporting a numeric data item requiring a format of “numeric” as right justified,
unsigned, without decimal equivalents, with leading zeros.

        (2) Reporting a character data item requiring a format of “character” as left-justified with
trailing blanks, except as noted in the record field coding and remarks.

        (3) Reporting a data item whose content is not available, not required, or not applicable
as a blank field.

    (4) Reporting a numeric data item that is a valid calendar date in the format of
YYYYMMDD. Report an indefinite dated calendar date as “29991231”.



SECTION 3: PAY FILE REPORTING PROCEDURES                                                            9
                                                                 DoDI 7770.02, December 4, 2025


        (5) Reporting a numeric data item requiring a format of “signed numeric, implied
decimal” with 2 implied decimals and leading zeros. Enter a minus (-) sign in the left-most
position for negative values and a blank in the left-most position for zero or positive values.

       (6) Reporting a numeric data item requiring a format of “signed numeric” without an
implied decimal and leading zeros. Enter a minus (-) sign in the left-most position for negative
values and a blank in the left-most position for zero or positive values.

      (7) Basing all year to date data items and calculations for allowances, allotments, tax
amounts, deductions, and contributions on a calendar year period; each calendar year contains 12
monthly reporting periods.

        (8) Basing all year to date data items and calculations for leave on a fiscal year period;
each fiscal year contains 12 monthly reporting periods.

   c. File Naming Conventions.

   Data set names, when needed, will be specified in the respective ICD.

   d. Electronic Secure Submission.

       (1) Each uniformed services component will maintain a capability for electronically
submitting data to the DMDC. The respective ICD will address additional system specifications,
including security protocols appropriate to protect data from being compromised and consistent
with the standards established in DoDI 8500.01.

        (2) The confidentiality level of data sent to the DMDC, is sensitive in accordance with
the guidance contained in DoDI 8500.01. Encrypt sensitive information sent via the Non-
classified Internet Protocol Router Network before transmission, as specified in the respective
ICD.

   e. United States Space Force (USSF) Processing.

    In accordance with Section 20108 of Title 10, U.S.C., reporting USSF pay data will conform
certain members of the USSF to an equivalent status in a Regular Component or Reserve
Component (RC) for providing pay and benefits, as shown in Figure 1.

        (1) Military training, duty, or other service performed by a member of the USSF in a
space force active status not on sustained duty (SFAS-NSD) will be considered military training
duty, or other service, as a member of an RC.

       (2) Sustained duty performed by a member of the USSF in a space force active status on
sustained duty (SFAS-SD) will be considered active duty (AD) as a member of a Regular
Component.

     (3) AD performed by a member of the USSF in SFAS-NSD will be considered AD as a
member of an RC.

                                    SECTION 4: PAY FILE

4.1. APPLICABILITY.

   a. The uniformed services pay sites will report pay records for the current month for:

        (1) All members of the Regular Components of the Military Services.

        (2) All members of the RCs of the Military Services.

        (3) All members of the USSF in a space force active status.

        (4) All members of the Commissioned Officer Corps of the NOAA.

     (5) All members of the Regular Corps of the USPHS and Ready Reserve Corps of the
USPHS Commissioned Corps.

       (6) All cadets and midshipmen of the Military Service academies, except for the United
States Merchant Marine Academy.

        (7) All members on retired lists of the uniformed services serving on AD.

    b. For each reported pay record of a member referenced in Table 1, the reporting pay site
will establish the uniformed service and component association using the:

        (1) Uniformed service organizational branch code (record field 1, Table 3).

        (2) Uniformed service organization component code (record field 2, Table 3).

         (3) The USSF has an assigned uniformed services organization component code (record
field 2, Table 3) of “Z”, reflecting no uniformed service organization component, in accordance
with Section 20001 of Title 10, U.S.C.

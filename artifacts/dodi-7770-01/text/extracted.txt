               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

   a. This issuance applies to:

       (1) OSD, the Military Departments (including the Coast Guard at all times, including
when it is a Service in the Department of Homeland Security by agreement with that
Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the
Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the
Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD.

        (2) All retired uniformed Service members, including enlisted members, warrant
officers, and commissioned officers, as well as their survivors who are entitled (receiving pay) or
are eligible (not receiving pay) to receive retirement payments or annuities during each reporting
period.

   b. This issuance does not apply to reservists who have completed the service required for
non-regular retirement but have not yet reached the statutory age to receive retirement payments.


1.2. POLICY.

It is DoD policy that:

    a. The DoD will maintain a centralized database for the timely reporting of RT and RTA
personnel pay data for interagency reporting, research, actuarial purposes, and evaluation of DoD
programs and policies.

    b. The requirements and procedures prescribed by DoD 5400.11-R and DoD Instruction
(DoDI) 5015.02 must be followed to safeguard the personnel data comprised of personally
identifiable information in this database. Individuals who have access to this database may be
held personally responsible and punished for making unauthorized disclosures.

    c. The database is subject to computer matching programs, as prescribed by DoD 5400.11-R.
Computer matching is conducted with other Federal and non-Federal agencies to verify
eligibility for benefit programs and determine continued compliance with benefit program
requirements. Matching is also conducted to recover improper payments made to, or delinquent
debts, owed by Federal personnel to include current and former military members.


1.3. INFORMATION COLLECTIONS.

The RT and RTA pay files database, referred to in Paragraph 1.2.a. of this issuance, does not
require licensing with a report control symbol in accordance with DoDI 8910.01.

2.1. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness (USD(P&R)), the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve Affairs
will provide policy guidance for reporting RT and RTA military pay records within the DoD.


2.2. DIRECTOR, DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE HUMAN RESOURCES ACTIVITY.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, the Director, Department of Defense Human Resources Activity, provides support to
ASD(M&RA), Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department
of Defense, Secretaries of the Military Departments and Commandant of the U.S. Coast Guard,
as appropriate, in execution of duties and responsibilities assigned under this Instruction:

   a. Serves as custodian of automated extracts of military pay records and provides data
quality control analysis and reporting, inquiry capabilities, and administrative and computer
support.

    b. Determines if uniform service reporting may be accomplished through secure electronic
data transfer.

   c. Establishes, for electronic submissions, an account number and password for reports and
provides additional system specifications.

   d. Develops, produces, and distributes all periodic and ad hoc RT and RTA reports.

    e. Provides record-level data, programming, reports, and analytical support, as requested, to
the USD(P&R) for special studies requiring use of the RT and RTA data.

    f. Reviews DHRA submission requirements and report instructions provided in Section 3 of
this issuance at least annually and develops changes, as required.

The USD(C)/CFO:

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. GENERAL GUIDANCE.

This section provides guidance on collecting and reporting pay data pertaining to those data
elements that are available on existing uniformed services automated information processing
systems.


3.2. DHRA SUBMISSION AND REPORT INSTRUCTIONS.

   a. Data Media.

   Each pay site will submit data to the DHRA via secure electronic transmission in compliance
with the data submission requirements of this issuance found in Paragraphs 3.2.b, 3.2.c, and 3.2.d.

   b. Reporting Requirements.

       (1) Each pay site will submit its data files by uniformed service within 5 calendar days of
the end of each month (“as-of” date of the file), except as noted in Paragraph 3.2.b.5.

        (2) Each uniformed service file will contain one record for each uniformed service
member or uniformed service member annuitant who is on its RT or RTA payroll as of the
reporting date.

       (3) Each pay site will follow the data field sequence in accordance with the Master File
coding instructions specified in Tables 1 and 2 of this issuance when reporting.

       (4) Each pay site will use data elements standardized in accordance with DoDI 8910.01
and DoDI 8320.02. Pay sites failing to comply with the coding instructions in this issuance will
be responsible for the conversion costs in accomplishing data interchange.

         (5) Each fiscal year contains 12 monthly reporting periods. The first report will be for
the first full month following the effective date of this issuance and will be submitted by the 15th
of the following month. Subsequent reports will be submitted within 5 days of the close of the
month reported.

        (6) Each report will be accompanied by an e-mail that identifies the files transmitted and
states the number of records in each file.

   c. Data Specifications.

        (1) Order each file by Person Social Security Number (SSN) (Record Field 2 of Tables 1
and 2), in ascending order.

         (2) Numeric data that are not dollar amounts will be right-justified with leading zeroes
(i.e., Format: 050).

       (3) Alphabetical data will be left-justified with trailing blanks.

        (4) All dates will be reported in YYYYMMDD format unless otherwise instructed in the
data element description.

       (5) All dollar amount fields, such as pay and premium amounts, will be assigned dollars
and cents, with implied decimal, unless otherwise specified.

   d. Electronic Secure Submission.

       (1) Data Submission.

        To electronically submit data to DHRA, the pay site will contact DHRA’s RT and RTA
pay file manager, who will ensure the establishment of an account number and password
exclusively for these submissions and provide additional system specifications, including
security protocols appropriate to protect the data from compromise and consistent with standards
established by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (see
http://www.nist.gov/index.html).

       (2) Transmission of Sensitive Information.

        The confidentiality level of data being sent to DHRA, as specified in Sections 4 and 5,
are considered Sensitive in accordance with the guidance contained in DoDI 8500.01. Sensitive
information may be sent via the SECRET Internet Protocol Router Network or Non-secure
Internet Protocol Router Network. Sensitive information sent via the Non-secure Internet
Protocol Router Network must be encrypted before transmission. The submitting organization is
responsible for the accuracy and completeness of each electronic data transfer.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

annuitant. A person receiving an annuity.

annuity. A monthly payment made to a person as a result of a specific survivorship plan.

ANSI. The ANSI established alphabetic and numeric codes for each State and outlying areas in
ANSI standard INCITS 38-2009[R2019]. ANSI standard INCITS 38-2009[R2019] replaced the
Federal Information Processing Standard (FIPS) standards FIPS 5-2, FIPS 6-4, and FIPS 10-4.
The ANSI alphabetic State code is the same as the USPS State code except for U.S. Minor
Outlying Islands, which have an ANSI code “UM” but no USPS code—and U.S. Military Mail
locations, which have USPS codes (“AA”, “AE”, “AP”) but no ANSI code.

basic pay. The active duty pay rates prescribed for an officer or enlisted member according to
pay grade and years of service.

BRS. Starting January 1, 2018, all new entrants to the military received this retirement system.
The “blending” in BRS comes from the blending of two major sources of retirement income: the
existing retired pay laws providing for a retirement at a fixed amount for those who attain the
requisite years of service (generally 20 years) plus the Thrift Savings Plan, a tax-deferred
retirement savings and investment plan that offers Federal employees the same type of savings
and tax benefits that many private corporations offer their employees under 401(k) plans
including government contributions and matching. Service members who joined the Service
before January 1, 2018, could opt-in into new the blended retirement system if they had less than
12 years of service (or had accumulated fewer than 4,320 retirement points) before January 1,
2018. If they did not opt in, the remained in the previousl defined benefit plan. For most active

before January 1, 2018.

cost of living adjustment. Percentage change applied to retired pay and SBP annuities based on
laws using the Consumer Price Index, as published by the Bureau of Labor Statistics, to
determine the amount of income.

CRDP. This program provides a 10-year phase-out (with certain exceptions) of the offset of
military retired pay for retired members who receive VA disability compensation, have a
combined VA disability rating of 50 percent or greater, and are active or Reserve members
receiving retired pay for longevity or disability retirees with more than 20 years of service in
accordance with Section 1414 of Title 10, U.S.C.

CRSC. This program provides a special monthly payment that is equivalent to the portion of
military retired pay offset for receipt of VA disability compensation determined to be combat-
related. Qualified individuals include any military retired members whose retired pay is offset
for VA compensation based on a combat-related disability in accordance with Section 1413a of
Title 10, U.S.C.

CSB. A program provided by the 1986 Military Reform Act by which a member agrees to
certain active service requirements in return for bonus pay in accordance with Section 354 of
Title 37, U.S.C.

DFAS. Utilizing the Defense Retiree and Annuitant System, provides services for Army,
Navy, Marine Corps, and Air Force retirees and their annuitants.

DIC. Compensation paid by the VA to the widow or widower of a member who dies after
December 31, 1956, from a service connected or compensable disability in accordance
with Chapter 13 of Title 38, U.S.C.

FR/FMCR. A Component of the Regular Service (Navy or Marine Corps) to which
members may be transferred and released from active duty after obtaining 20 or more years
of active Federal service.

GENC. The U.S. Government profile of International Organization for Standardization
(ISO) 3166 Codes for the representation of names of countries and their subdivisions. It
specifies an authoritative set of country codes and names for use by the Federal Government
for information exchange, using ISO 3166 (Parts 1 and 2) names and code elements
wherever possible, with modifications only where necessary to comply with U.S. law and
U.S. Government recognition policy. Information regarding events leading to the
development of the GENC Standard is documented at
https://nsgreg.nga.mil/geopoliticalCode.jsp.

ISO 3166 Codes. The International Standard for country codes and codes for their subdivisions.
The purpose of ISO 3166 is to define internationally recognized codes of letters and/or numbers
that we can use when we refer to countries and subdivisions.

pay sites. Activities that provide finance and accounting services for Military retirees and

retired pay (includes FR and FMCR retainer pay). The gross entitlement for a member

Title 10, U.S.C.

date in accordance with Section 1401a(f) of Title 10, U.S.C.

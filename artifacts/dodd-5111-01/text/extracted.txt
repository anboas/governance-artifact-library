Purpose: This issuance updates the responsibilities and functions, relationships, and authorities of the
USD(P) and authorizes the USD(P), as a Principal Staff Assistant (PSA) reporting directly to the
Secretary of Defense, to establish DoD policy in DoD issuances within the responsibilities and functions
and authorities assigned in this issuance, and to reissue chartering DoD Directives (DoDDs) for OSD
officials who were appointed by the President with the advice and consent of the Senate and who are
under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P), in accordance with DoD Instruction
(DoDI) 5025.01, pursuant to the authority vested in the Secretary of Defense by Sections 113 and 134 of
Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.).

              SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.


This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

               SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES AND FUNCTIONS

The USD(P) is the PSA and advisor to the Secretary of Defense for all matters regarding the
formulation of national security and defense strategy and policy and the integration and oversight
of DoD policy, strategy, plans, execution, and capabilities to achieve national security objectives.
In the exercise of assigned responsibilities, the USD(P):

    a. Represents DoD in matters involving the National Security Council; the Department of
State; and other Federal departments, agencies, and interagency groups with responsibility for
national security policy.

   b. Serves as a member of the National Security Council Deputies’ Committee and the
Deputies’ Committee for Crisis Management; advises the Secretary of Defense on crisis
prevention and management, including planning for major areas of concern.

   c. Serves as a statutory member of the Nuclear Weapons Council and the Council on
Oversight of the National Leadership Command, Control, and Communications System.

    d. Establishes DoD policy for defense-related international agreements and represents DoD
in negotiating such agreements unless authority is otherwise delegated.

  e. Serves as an advisor to the Joint Requirements Oversight Council on matters within the
USD(P)’s authority and expertise.

   f. Oversees:

        (1) Development, implementation, and integration across DoD of the National Defense
Strategy (as described by Section 113 of Title 10, U.S.C.) and strategic policy guidance for DoD
activities across all geographic regions and military functions and domains.

       (2) Integration of DoD activities into the National Security Strategy of the United States.

   g. Oversees the preparation of policy guidance for the development of the global force
posture.

    h. In coordination with the General Counsel of the Department of Defense (GC DoD) and in
accordance with Section 112b of Title 1, U.S.C., Part 181 of Title 22, Code of Federal
Regulations, and DoDI 5530.03, authorizes the negotiation and conclusion of categories of
international agreements and delegates authority to the DoD Component heads to approve
negotiation and conclusion of categories of international agreements.

   i. Establishes DoD policy and positions for international negotiations and consultations on
arms control implementation and DoD policy for compliance issues.

    j. Establishes DoD policy on the conduct of alliances, partnerships, and other defense
relationships with foreign governments and their military establishments and international
organizations; integrates plans and programs undertaken with those alliances, partnerships, and

                               SECTION 4: AUTHORITIES

Pursuant to the authority vested in the Secretary of Defense, and subject to his or her authority,
direction, and control, and in accordance with DoD policies and issuances, the USD(P) is hereby
delegated authority to exercise, within assigned responsibilities and functions, all authority of the
Secretary of Defense derived from statute, Executive order (E.O.), or interagency agreement,
except where specifically limited by statute or E.O. to the Secretary of Defense. The USD(P) is
specifically delegated authority to:

    a. Establish DoD policy through DoDIs, Directive-type Memorandums (DTMs), and rules
published in the Federal Register, within the authorities and responsibilities assigned in this
issuance and in accordance with DoDI 5025.01 or Administrative Instruction (AI) 102.

        (1) In those documents, assign responsibilities related to the authorities and
responsibilities in this issuance to other OSD PSAs and the DoD Component heads. Assignment
of responsibilities in DoD issuances or rules published in the Federal Register to the Military
Departments must be made through the Secretaries of those Departments. DoD issuances or
rules published in the Federal Register assigning responsibilities to the Combatant Commands
must be coordinated with the CJCS.

       (2) DoDIs and DTMs must be fully coordinated in accordance with DoDI 5025.01.
Rules published in the Federal Register must be fully coordinated with affected OSD and DoD
Component heads and be consistent with AI 102.

       (3) This authority may not be re-delegated.

   b. Approve other DoDIs, DoDMs, and DTMs in areas of assigned responsibilities and
functions that implement policy already established by a DoDD, DoDI, DTM, or policy
memorandum issued by the Secretary or Deputy Secretary.

        (1) In these documents, assign responsibilities related to the authorities and
responsibilities in this issuance to other OSD PSAs and the DoD Component heads. Assignment

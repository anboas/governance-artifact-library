                             DOD INSTRUCTION 5505.19
      MILITARY CRIMINAL INVESTIGATIVE ORGANIZATIONS’
       SPECIAL VICTIM INVESTIGATION AND PROSECUTION
                        CAPABILITY

Originating Component:        Office of Inspector General of the Department of Defense

Effective:                    October 8, 2024

Releasability:                Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                              at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Reissues and Cancels:         DoD Instruction 5505.19, “Establishment of Special Victim Investigation
                              and Prosecution (SVIP) Capability within the Military Criminal
                              Investigative Organizations (MCIOs),” February 3, 2015, as amended

Approved by:                  Robert P. Storch, Inspector General of the Department of Defense


Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive (DoDD) 5106.01, this issuance:
• Establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and provides procedures for the military criminal
investigative organizations (MCIOs) to establish a special victim capability in accordance with the
authority in Chapter 4 of Title 5, United States Code (U.S.C.) (also known as the “Inspector General Act
of 1978,” as amended).
• Implements the investigative portion of Section 573 of Public Law (PL) 112-239 (accompanied as a
note with Section 1561 of Title 10, U.S.C.) that requires each Military Department to establish a special
victim capability comprised of specially trained MCIO investigators, judge advocates, victim witness
assistance personnel, and administrative paralegal support personnel to support victims of covered
special victim offenses. To avoid confusion with special victims’ counsel (SVC) programs, this
capability is referred to as the special victim investigation and prosecution (SVIP) capability.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense (IG DoD), the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

    a. MCIO investigators must provide a timely, effective, and worldwide SVIP capability for
victims of covered special victim offenses, as described in Paragraph 3.2., that occur within the
MCIOs’ investigative jurisdiction and responsibility.

     b. Specially trained and designated MCIO criminal investigators are SVIP members who
must collaborate with other specially trained SVIP members, as listed in Paragraph 3.3., during
all stages of the investigative and military justice process for covered special victim offenses.

    c. Nothing in this issuance is intended to change or limit an MCIO’s existing authority to
investigate any offense; however, only covered special victim offenses will be designated as
SVIP incidents.

   d. MCIO investigators must document investigation information and actions in their
organization’s automated investigation management system and be able to retrieve SVIP
investigation information from the system.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. IG DOD.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., the IG DoD oversees and evaluates, as the IG
DoD considers appropriate, DoD Component compliance with this issuance.


2.2. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR INTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY.

As the designated Principal Staff Assistant for law enforcement, the Under Secretary of Defense
for Intelligence and Security:

   a. May monitor DoD Component execution of this issuance.

    b. Establishes, reviews, and coordinates on training and training requirements related to this
issuance.

    c. Monitors resourcing to implement this issuance and advocates for any changes to that
resourcing.


2.3. DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The DoD Component heads:

   a. Require their Component to comply with this issuance and prescribe additional
procedures, as necessary, to implement its policy.

    b. Ensure records and information established and created in accordance with this issuance
are retained in accordance with DoD Instruction (DoDI) 5015.02 and DoD Component records
management disposition schedules.


2.4. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., the Secretaries of the Military Departments
establish SVIP capabilities for their respective Department in accordance with this issuance.

                         SECTION 3: GENERAL PROVISIONS

3.1. SVIP PROCESS.

The MCIOs must assign a specially trained SVIP criminal investigator to all covered special
victim offenses that occur within the MCIOs’ investigative jurisdiction and responsibility at the
onset of an investigation or as soon as practical after an investigation finds that a covered special
victim offense may have occurred. The criminal investigator will collaborate with other
specially trained SVIP members as specified in Paragraphs 1.2.b. and 3.3.


3.2. COVERED SPECIAL VICTIM OFFENSES.

Covered special victim offenses include:

   a. Adult sexual assault.

   b. Domestic violence involving sexual assault or aggravated assault with grievous bodily
harm.

   c. Child abuse involving child sexual assault or aggravated assault with grievous bodily
harm.


3.3. SVIP MEMBERS.

In accordance with Section 573 of PL 112-239, the SVIP members include specially trained and
designated:

   a. MCIO criminal investigators.

   b. Judge advocates, including those assigned to the Office of Special Trial Counsel and SVC
when appropriate for the offense.

   c. Victim witness assistance personnel.

   d. Paralegal or administrative legal support personnel.


3.4. TRAINING.

The MCIO lead criminal investigator assigned to a covered special victim offense investigation
must be specially trained by MCIO or other personnel to respond to and investigate such
offenses. Selecting and certifying MCIO lead criminal investigators for the SVIP capability is
contingent on them completing basic criminal investigator training at their respective law
enforcement training institution (including military training schools) and SVIP specialized
training. The MCIOs are permitted to provide the SVIP specialized training during the basic
criminal investigator training course and can document that the criminal investigators are fully

trained for SVIP investigations. The MCIO lead criminal investigator for a covered special
victim offense must receive SVIP specialized training that at least includes:

   a. Legal jurisdiction for conducting criminal investigations.

    b. The elements of proof for criminal offenses associated with all covered special victim
offenses.

   c. Crime scene management.

   d. DNA and serology evidence collection.

   e. Identifying, preserving, obtaining, and transporting forensic evidence.

   f. Crime victims’ rights and available victim witness assistance, support, legal assistance,
and counseling services in accordance with DoDI 1030.02 and Section 806b of Title 10, U.S.C.

   g. Sensitivities and risk factors associated with child abuse, including, but not limited to:

       (1) Various interviewing techniques used by the law enforcement community to help
victims recall specific details about a crime.

       (2) Working with a family advocacy program (FAP) manager in accordance with DoDIs
6400.01 and 6400.06.

        (3) Working with a domestic abuse victim advocate (DAVA) to ensure the victim and
their family receives support and required services, in accordance with DoDIs 1030.02 and
6400.06.

    h. Sensitivities and risk factors associated with domestic violence and sexual assault victims,
including, but not limited to:

       (1) Various interviewing techniques used by the law enforcement community to help
victims recall specific details about the crime.

       (2) Determining the impact and involvement of alcohol and drugs.

        (3) Understanding the requirements associated with obtaining and executing protection
orders in accordance with Section 1561a of Title 10, U.S.C. for civilian protection orders and
DoDI 6400.06 for military protection orders.

       (4) Understanding aspects of restricted reporting for domestic violence in accordance
with DoDI 6400.06.

        (5) Recognizing the dynamics of domestic violence, including behaviors used by abusers
to control or dominate their victims.

       (6) Working with a DAVA.

3.5. CRITERIA AND CERTIFICATION.

The MCIOs will select and certify those investigators that will serve as lead criminal
investigators for covered special victim offenses after they complete all required training, as
specified in Paragraph 3.4.


3.6. MCIO LEAD CRIMINAL INVESTIGATOR OR SUPERVISORY SPECIAL
AGENT.

The designated MCIO lead criminal investigator or supervisory special agent must:

    a. Notify or ensure each SVIP member and victim advocacy personnel are notified, as
appropriate for the reported offense, within 24 hours (regardless of the day of the week) after
determining that the reported offense meets the special victim covered offense criteria. The
notification will be completed via personal meeting, telephone conference call, electronic mail,
or video teleconference (VTC). Nothing in Paragraph 3.6. is intended to change how
investigators collaborate with judge advocates in early investigative processes. When covered
offense determination is unclear, the criminal investigator or supervisory special agent will
coordinate with the supporting judge advocate to determine whether the reported incident
includes a covered offense.

    b. Complete initial collaboration with all SVIP members and victim advocacy personnel, as
appropriate for the reported offense, within 2 business days after determining that an allegation
meets the criteria of a special victim covered offense. The collaboration will occur via personal
meeting, telephone conference call, electronic mail, or VTC. This initial collaboration can be
completed at the same time the notification specified in Paragraph 3.6.a. is made, if sufficient
information about the reported incident is available to discuss.

   c. Collaborate monthly with respective SVIP members and victim advocacy personnel, as
appropriate for the reported offense, to assess progress in the investigation or prosecution of a
covered special victim offense and to ensure all aspects of the victim’s needs are being met. The
monthly collaboration:

        (1) Will occur via personal meetings, telephone conference calls, electronic mail, or
VTCs.

       (2) Can be completed during other monthly installation meetings if the other SVIP
members are in attendance and only those with a need to know about the offense(s) to be
discussed are present.

        (3) Must be completed during the active investigation and continue until action against
the subject(s) identified in the investigation has been taken and the investigation is closed.

    d. Document the respective MCIO’s SVIP member notifications and collaboration in the
corresponding investigation report or file. The documentation will include the name, position,
and organization of the SVIP members involved in the consultation and the date of the
consultation.

    e. Collaborate as necessary and appropriate for the investigated offense with sexual assault
response coordinators, sexual assault prevention and response victim advocates, FAP managers,
and DAVAs.

   f. Investigate unrestricted reports of adult sexual assaults in accordance with DoDIs 5505.03
and 5505.18 and Volume 1 of DoDI 6495.02.

   g. Investigate unrestricted reports of domestic violence involving sexual assault or
aggravated assault with grievous bodily harm in accordance with DoDIs 5505.03, 5505.18,
6400.01, and 6400.06.

   h. Investigate allegations of child abuse involving child sexual assault or aggravated assault
with grievous bodily harm in accordance with DoDIs 5505.03, 6400.01, and 6400.06.

   i. Provide releasable applicable investigation information to SVIP members except when it
may hinder or compromise the criminal investigation.


3.7. ASSESSMENT.

In accordance with Section 573 of PL 112-239 (accompanied as a note with Section 1561 of
Title 10, U.S.C.), the Military Departments will collect data and report on the effectiveness and
impact of the investigative portion of the SVIP to the Secretary of Defense as needed. These
reports will assess the performance and effectiveness of Military Service programs, policies,
training, and services. The Military Departments will, at a minimum, collect data regarding
compliance with the following requirements:

   a. The appropriate SVIP members were notified within 24 hours when a reported offense
meets the criteria of a covered special victim offense.

    b. The appropriate SVIP members collaborated within 2 business days when a reported
offense meets the criteria of a covered special victim offense.

    c. The MCIO criminal investigators identified to conduct investigations of covered special
victim offenses were trained and certified in accordance with this issuance.

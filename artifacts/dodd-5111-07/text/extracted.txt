               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

               SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES AND FUNCTIONS
The ASD(ISA), under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P), is the principal civilian
advisor to the Secretary of Defense, Deputy Secretary of Defense, and the USD(P) on all matters
relating to international security strategy and policy issues related to nations and international
organizations in Europe, including the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) and the
European Union; Russia, Ukraine, and Eurasia; the Middle East; and Africa. Section 5 identifies
the regions and countries of focus for the ASD(ISA). In fulfillment of the National Defense
Strategy, the ASD(ISA):

    a. Conducts and manages day-to-day, multilateral, regional, and bilateral defense relations in
assigned areas of responsibility.

    b. Develops, country-specific and regional security and defense strategy and policy, advises
senior DoD leaders, issues guidance to translate and incorporate national and DoD policies into
these strategies and policies, and oversees their implementation in coordination with the
appropriate DoD officials.

   c. For assigned areas of responsibility, develops, coordinates, and oversees the
implementation of:

       (1) Defense strategy and policy and management of defense and military relations.

       (2) Policy, plans, and activities, and use of DoD resources engaged in:

           (a) Encouraging the development of military capabilities.

           (b) Addressing strategic competition below the level of armed conflict.

            (c) Encouraging constitutional democracy, respect for human rights, and civilian
control of the military.

         (d) Institutionalizing an appropriate role for the military in a constitutional
democracy.

          (e) Encouraging the development of standards of military professionalism that
promote respect for elected civilian authorities and human rights.

        (3) DoD regional and country-specific policy and recommendations concerning security
cooperation (SC) programs and organizations, including foreign military sales, security
assistance, international military education and training, and other missions pertaining to SC, in
coordination with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Strategy, Plans, and Capabilities, who
has policy oversight for individual SC programs.

    d. Represents the Secretary of Defense and the USD(P) in interagency policy deliberations
and international negotiations relating to assigned areas of responsibility. In coordination with
the General Counsel of the Department of Defense, oversees the development, review,

responsibility.

   e. Develops, coordinates, and oversees the implementation of defense policy related to
NATO; the Euro-Atlantic Partnership Council; the Partnership for Peace; and other institutions
with a security dimension, including the European Union, the Organization for Security and
Co-operation in Europe, the Gulf Cooperation Council, the African Union, and African regional
economic communities.

       (1) On behalf of the USD(P), provides policy guidance and oversight to the Secretary of
Defense Representative in Europe, who concurrently serves as the Defense Advisor, U.S.
Mission to NATO and Defense Policy Advisor, U.S. Mission to the European Union.

       (2) In coordination with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Space Policy, promotes
coordination and cooperation on NATO nuclear policy and strategy with NATO allies and
supports the NATO Nuclear Planning Group and its subordinate body, the High Level Group.

   f. In coordination with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Special Operations and
Low-Intensity Conflict:

        (1) Develops and oversees defense policy related to violent extremist organizations
within the assigned areas of responsibility.

       (2) Develops and oversees messaging guidance and oversees operations in the
information environment related to the assigned areas of responsibility.

    g. Addresses strategic competition with the People’s Republic of China in the assigned areas
of responsibility, in coordination with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Indo-Pacific
Affairs.

    h. Participates in Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution process activities that
relate to assigned areas of responsibility.

   i. When possible, uses existing systems, facilities, and services of the DoD and other Federal
departments and agencies to avoid duplication and achieve maximum efficiency and economy.

   j. Performs such other duties as the Secretary of Defense and the USD(P) may prescribe.

                             SECTION 3: RELATIONSHIPS

3.1. ASD(ISA).

In performing assigned functions and responsibilities, the ASD(ISA):

   a. Reports directly to the USD(P), who exercises authority, direction, and control.

   b. Coordinates and exchanges information with other OSD officials; the DoD Component
heads; and Federal, State, or local officials having collateral or related responsibilities and
functions.


3.2. PRINCIPAL STAFF ASSISTANTS (PSAS) AND DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The PSAs and DoD Component heads coordinate with the ASD(ISA) on matters under their
purview related to the responsibilities, functions, and authorities assigned in this issuance.

                             SECTION 4: AUTHORITIES
In accordance with DoD policies and issuances, the ASD(ISA) is delegated the authority to:

    a. Approve DoD manuals and directive-type memorandums in areas of assigned
responsibilities that implement policy already established by a DoDD, DoD instruction (DoDI),
directive-type memorandum, or policy memorandum issued by the Secretary of Defense or
Deputy Secretary of Defense.

       (1) In these documents, assign responsibilities related to the authorities and
responsibilities in this issuance to the PSAs and DoD Component heads. Assignment of
responsibilities in these DoD issuances to Military Department officials must be made through
the Secretaries of those Departments. DoD issuances assigning responsibilities to the Combatant
Commands must be coordinated with the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff.

       (2) Such documents must be fully coordinated in accordance with DoDI 5025.01.

       (3) This authority may not be redelegated.

    b. Communicate directly with the DoD Component heads as necessary, to carry out assigned
responsibilities and functions, including transmitting requests for advice and assistance.
Communications to Military Department officials must be transmitted through the Secretaries of
the Military Departments or as otherwise provided in law or as directed by the Secretary of
Defense in other DoD issuances. Communications to the Combatant Commanders must be in
accordance with DoDD 5100.01.

    c. Communicate with other government officials, members of the public, and representatives
of foreign governments, as appropriate, in carrying out assigned responsibilities and functions.
Communications with representatives and members of the Legislative Branch must be conducted
through the Office of the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Legislative Affairs, except for
communications with the Committee on Appropriations of the Senate and House of
Representatives, which must be coordinated with the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense
(Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of Defense.

   d. Communicate with agencies of the Executive Branch and Congress on special access
programs. For all special access program matters, the ASD(ISA) must conduct communications
with agencies of the Executive Branch and Congress pursuant to DoDD 5205.07.

    e. Obtain reports and information, in accordance with DoDI 8910.01, as necessary, to carry
out assigned responsibilities and functions.

   f. Establish arrangements for DoD participation in non-DoD governmental programs for
which the ASD(ISA) has been assigned primary DoD responsibility.

    g. Negotiate and conclude international agreements in areas of assigned responsibility, when
authorized by and in accordance with DoDI 5530.03. This authority is subject to coordination
with the General Counsel of the Department of Defense.

   SECTION 5: REGIONAL AND COUNTRY FOCUS OF THE ASD(ISA)
The alignment of individual countries under the focus of the ASD(ISA) and the other Assistant
Secretaries of Defense with regional responsibilities is subject to adjustment by the USD(P).
Such adjustments will be accomplished by administrative changes pursuant to the
November 30, 2006 Deputy Secretary of Defense Memorandum.

   a. Europe.

    Includes Albania, Andorra, Austria, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia,
Cyprus, Czechia, Denmark (including Greenland and the Faroe Islands), Estonia, Finland,
France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Kosovo, Latvia, Liechtenstein,
Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Montenegro, the Netherlands, North Macedonia,
Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, San Marino, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden,
Switzerland, Turkey, the United Kingdom (including its overseas territories and crown
dependencies), and Vatican City.

   b. Russia, Ukraine, and Eurasia.

    Includes Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Moldova, Russia,
Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, and Uzbekistan.

   c. Middle East.

    Includes Bahrain, Egypt, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Oman, Palestinian
territories, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Syria, the United Arab Emirates, and Yemen.

   d. Africa.

    Includes Algeria, Angola, Benin, Botswana, Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cabo Verde, Cameroon,
the Central African Republic, Chad, Comoros, Côte d’Ivoire, the Democratic Republic of the
Congo, Djibouti, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Eswatini, Ethiopia, Gabon, Gambia, Ghana, Guinea,
Guinea-Bissau, Kenya, Lesotho, Libya, Liberia, Madagascar, Malawi, Mali, Mauritania,
Mauritius, Morocco, Mozambique, Namibia, Niger, Nigeria, Republic of the Congo, Rwanda,
São Tomé and Príncipe, Senegal, Seychelles, Sierra Leone, Somalia, South Africa, South Sudan,
Sudan, Tanzania, Togo, Tunisia, Uganda, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.

                             DOD DIRECTIVE 5160.05E
       ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES ASSOCIATED WITH THE
        CHEMICAL AND BIOLOGICAL DEFENSE PROGRAM

Originating Component:       Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment

Effective:                   November 12, 2024

Releasability:               Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                             at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Reissues and Cancels:        DoD Directive 5160.05E, “Roles and Responsibilities Associated with the
                             Chemical and Biological Defense Program (CBDP),” September 8, 2017,
                             as amended
Cancels:                     Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition, Technology, and Logistics
                             Memorandum, “Implementation Plan for the Management of the Chemical
                             Biological Defense Program,” April 22, 2003, as amended

Approved by:                 Kathleen H. Hicks, Deputy Secretary of Defense


Purpose: This issuance:
• Establishes policy and assigns responsibilities for the Chemical and Biological Defense Program’s
(CBDP’s) research, development, and acquisition (RDA) of chemical and biological (CB) defense
capabilities (medical and physical) required to support DoD operations in CB-impacted environments
pursuant to Section 1522 of Title 50, United States Code (U.S.C.).
• Designates the Secretary of the Army as the DoD Executive Agent (EA) for the CBDP pursuant to
Section 1522(c)(1) of Title 50, U.S.C., and in accordance with DoD Directive (DoDD) 5101.01.

                SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

The DoD will:

   a. Carry out a coordinated CBDP that:

        (1) Is under the oversight of the Defense Acquisition Executive who will leverage and
chair a special interest review process for the CBDP.

      (2) Maintains close and continuous coordination between CB physical defense programs
and CB medical defense programs.

        (3) Develops and acquires integrated and interoperable CB defense capabilities,
informed by risk management processes and current and future threats, that assure DoD executes
its missions in CB-impacted environments.

        (4) Integrates operations security requirements into CB defense plans, programs, and
activities to protect critical information and indicators that could allow adversaries and potential
adversaries to plan, prepare, and conduct chemical or biological operations against the United
States.

       (5) Is composed of a portfolio of individual efforts that are managed as separate
programs, with acquisition category designations, as appropriate, based upon the anticipated
expenditures of each program or level of senior interest.

   b. Submit the DoD CBDP funding requests in the budget of the DoD for each fiscal year as a
separate account with a single program element for each category of research, development, test,
and evaluation (RDT&E); procurement; and military construction in accordance with
Section 1522(d)(2) of Title 50, U.S.C.

    c. Carry out the CBDP in accordance with applicable international obligations of the United
States, including the Convention on the Prohibition of the Development, Production, and
Stockpiling of Bacteriological (Biological) and Toxin Weapons and on their Destruction, and the
Convention on the Prohibition of the Development, Production, Stockpiling, and use of
Chemical Weapons and on their Destruction; and with associated implementation requirements
in Parts 710-721 and Part 742.2 of Title 15, Code of Federal Regulations; Parts 120-130 of
Title 22, Code of Federal Regulations; and DoD Instruction 2040.02. Address issues related to
questions of compliance with these treaties in accordance with DoDD 2060.01.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION AND SUSTAINMENT
(USD(A&S)).

The USD(A&S):

   a. Oversees:

        (1) DoD CBDP RDA programs to ensure they support the National Defense Strategy, the
National Biodefense Strategy, the DoD Strategy for Countering Weapons of Mass Destruction
(CWMD), and other national and departmental guidance, as applicable, and enable the DoD to
anticipate and address current and future CB threats through delivery of CB defense capabilities
to the Military Departments.

       (2) The DoD EA activities for the CBDP pursuant to DoDD 5101.01.

       (3) Resourcing, in coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief
Financial Officer, Department of Defense, to enable the CBDP to execute the RDA requirements
for developing and fielding priority DoD CB defense capabilities that address readiness and
modernization of the Military Services to counter CB threats.

       (4) The prioritization of mission-focused, risk-informed RDA of CB defense capabilities
through a governance framework.

    b. Reviews and approves all CBDP program objective memorandum (POM)
recommendations.

   c. Coordinates intelligence and counterintelligence support for the RDA of CB defense
programs and activities with the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security in
accordance with DoDD 5143.01.

    d. In coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense for Research and Engineering
(USD(R&E)) and in accordance with DoDD 5137.02, formulates CB defense mission
engineering policy, guidance, practices, and tools for analysis (including modeling and
simulation) of operational warfighting concepts, functions, systems, and technologies in an end-
to-end mission context.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR NUCLEAR, CHEMICAL, AND
BIOLOGICAL DEFENSE PROGRAMS (ASD(NCB)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(A&S), the ASD(NCB):

   a. Is the principal advisor to the Secretary of Defense, Deputy Secretary of Defense, and
USD(A&S) on CB defense, including matters related to the RDA of medical and physical
materiel and DoD compliance with existing and prospective CB arms control treaties and

agreements pursuant to Section 138(b)(4) of Title 10, U.S.C.; DoDD 2060.01; DoDD 5000.01;
and DoDD 5134.08.

   b. Oversees and integrates RDA of CB defense capabilities to:

        (1) Support the objectives of national and departmental strategies to reduce incentives
for adversaries and actors of concern and their networks from pursuing, possessing, and
employing CB weapons; increase barriers to the acquisition, proliferation, and use of CB warfare
agents; manage risks, including terrorism risks; and deny the effects of current and future CB
threats through integrated, layered defenses.

        (2) Enable joint force resilience while anticipating and addressing current and future CB
threats to achieve DoD-wide integrated and layered CB defense.

   c. Is responsible for the overall coordination, oversight, and integration of the CBDP and
may delegate this responsibility to a subordinate official within their authority, direction, and
control.

  d. Oversees the DoD EA for the CBDP, on behalf of the USD(A&S), pursuant to
DoDD 5101.01 and DoDD 5134.08.

    e. Coordinates and integrates activities with the DoD EA for the CBDP, the CJCS, the
USD(R&E), the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness (USD(P&R)), the
Assistant Secretary of Defense for Space Policy, and the Secretaries of the Military Departments
to direct CB defense RDA to address current and future CB threats in accordance with the
National Defense Strategy, National Biodefense Strategy, and other national and departmental
guidance as applicable.

    f. Develops and provides risk-informed strategic guidance, priorities, goals, and objectives
for CBDP RDA activities that:

       (1) Align CB defense investments with national and departmental guidance.

        (2) Balance acquisition investments to mitigate risks to the joint force and DoD missions
and integrate with the planning, programming, budgeting, and execution process.

        (3) Promote balanced doctrine, organization, training, materiel, leadership and education,
personnel, facilities, and policy solutions for CB defense capability requirements across the joint
force and Military Services.

      (4) Deliver relevant CB defense capabilities that enable the joint force to fulfill critical
DoD strategic missions.

   g. Develops and makes milestone decision authority recommendations to the Defense
Acquisition Executive for CBDP program(s) of record, as required.

   h. Leads, endorses, and submits the CBDP POM recommendation to the USD(A&S) as
required.

   i. Designates CBDP defense-wide account funds management responsibilities to an
appropriate office or agency pursuant to DoD 7000.14-R.

    j. In coordination with the DoD Component heads, integrates risk-management and mission
assurance-related processes, outputs, and concepts to inform CB defense capability RDA.

   k. In coordination with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Health Affairs (ASD(HA))

       (1) Coordinates and collaborates on DoD biosurveillance-related CB defense activities

Director, Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA); the Secretaries of the Military
Departments; and the DoD EA for the CBDP, integrates and oversees DoD infrastructure
required to perform CBDP-related RDT&E.


2.3. USD(R&E).

In coordination with the USD(A&S), the USD(R&E):

    a. Establishes policy for laboratories critical to RDA of CB defense capabilities, including
infrastructure.

   b. Reviews and assesses science and technology related to CB defense capabilities.

    c. Oversees, develops, and implements policy and guidance for conducting (including
planning, execution, and reporting) developmental test and evaluation related to the DoD CBDP.

    d. Develops CB defense mission engineering policy, guidance, practices, and tools for
analyzing (including modeling and simulation) operational warfighting concepts, functions,
systems, and technologies in an end-to-end mission context.


2.4. DIRECTOR, TEST RESOURCE MANAGEMENT CENTER.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(R&E), and in accordance with
DoDD 5105.71, the Director, Test Resource Management Center, coordinates with the
ASD(NCB) and the Secretaries of the Military Departments on the infrastructure and personnel
required to accomplish the test-and-evaluation mission as it relates to the RDA of CB defense
capabilities.

2.5. DARPA.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(R&E) and in accordance with
Sections 1522(c)(2) and (d)(3) of Title 50, U.S.C., the Director, DARPA, may conduct a
program of basic and applied research and advanced technology development of CB defense
technologies and systems. If such a program is conducted, the Director, DARPA:

   a. Coordinates with the ASD(NCB) and the Military Departments to avoid unnecessary
duplication of the CB defense RDA activities.

   b. Establishes a separate program element in the DARPA budget for these efforts.


2.6. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR POLICY.

The Under Secretary of Defense for Policy:

    a. Establishes, coordinates, and oversees the implementation and integration of DoD CWMD
and CB defense policy in accordance with the DoD Strategy for CWMD, DoD policy for
preparedness and response to domestic CB incidents, and any national or departmental guidance
related to CB defense preparedness and response.

   b. Coordinates with the ASD(NCB), or their designee, on CB defense planning and
programming priorities for the RDA and use of CB defense capabilities.


2.7. USD(P&R).

The USD(P&R):

   a. In accordance with DoDD 5124.02, establishes policies, plans, and programs for health
and medical affairs, including providing and maintaining readiness.

   b. Pursuant to DoDD 6420.02, serves as the office of primary responsibility for the DoD
Biosurveillance Program activities and coordinates with the USD(A&S) on developing
capabilities to execute the DoD Biosurveillance Program.


2.8. ASD(HA).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R) and in accordance with
DoDD 5136.01, the ASD(HA):

   a. Serves as the principal advisor to the Secretary of Defense, the Deputy Secretary of
Defense, and the USD(P&R) for:

       (1) Health care and health surveillance aspects of CB medical defense programs.

       (2) Deployment matters as they pertain to force health protection and readiness.

   b. Coordinates with the ASD(NCB) and the Secretaries of the Military Departments on:

        (1) CB defense medical materiel RDA activities and developing CB medical defense
policy for using preventative and therapeutic countermeasures for DoD personnel.

       (2) Medical laboratory infrastructure, personnel, and facility utilization related to CB
defense RDT&E.

       (3) Medical readiness of joint forces to respond to CB threats regardless of threat origin.

       (4) Preparing and submitting the DoD Unified Medical Program Budget to the Under
Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of Defense, for CB
defense-related activities, including the operational sustainment of biological defense vaccines
and other CB medical materiel, in coordination with the Secretaries of the Military Departments
and the Director, Defense Health Agency.

        (5) Biosurveillance-related activities for CB defense in accordance with this issuance and
overall situational awareness of health threats to the joint force in accordance with
DoDD 6420.02.


2.9. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR INTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY.

The Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security:

    a. Through the Director, Defense Intelligence Agency, and in accordance with
DoDD 5143.01 and DoD Instruction 5000.02, provides all required intelligence support for
validating and prioritizing CB threats to DoD personnel, systems, equipment, and facilities.

    b. Advises and assists the USD(A&S), and the heads of other Federal departments and
agencies as appropriate, concerning CB defense acquisition programs that significantly affect
defense intelligence, counterintelligence, and security programs or organizations pursuant to
DoDD 5143.01.

   c. In coordination with the USD(A&S) and in accordance with DoDD 5143.01, identifies
opportunities to use advances in technology to enhance CB-related intelligence,
counterintelligence, and security capabilities of the DoD.

   d. Oversees RDT&E of national intelligence program-funded CWMD programs in
coordination with the Director of National Intelligence, the USD(A&S), the USD(R&E), and
other OSD Components, as appropriate.

2.10. DIRECTOR OF OPERATIONAL TEST AND EVALUATION (DOT&E).

The DOT&E:

    a. Serves as principal adviser to the Secretary of Defense , the Deputy Secretary of Defense,
the USD(A&S), and the USD(R&E) on operational test and evaluation in the DoD and the
principal operational test and evaluation official within the senior management of the DoD
pursuant to Section 139 of Title 10, U.S.C., and in accordance with DoDD 5141.02.

    b. Prescribes policies, guidance and procedures for operational test and evaluation, including
for CBDP acquisition programs designated for DOT&E oversight, and approves operational test
and evaluation plans for programs under DOT&E oversight.

   c. Reviews and make recommendations on all budgetary and financial matters relating to
operational test and evaluation, including operational test facilities and equipment supporting the
CBDP.


2.11. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR SPECIAL OPERATIONS AND
LOW-INTENSITY CONFLICT.

The Assistant Secretary of Defense for Special Operations and Low-Intensity Conflict, in
accordance with DoDD 5111.10 and pursuant to Sections 138(b)(2) and 167 of Title 10, U.S.C.:

    a. Serves as a Principal Staff Assistant to the Secretary of Defense in exercising authority,
direction, and control of all special operations-peculiar administrative matters relating to the
organization, training, and equipping of special operations forces.

   b. Ensures budgets account for operating and sustaining special operations forces-peculiar
CB defense equipment and materiel.

   c. Provides special operations forces-peculiar CB defense-specific POM recommendations
and submits for consideration to the ASD(NCB).

   d. Supports the CBDP and the DoD EA for the CBDP to conduct joint development,
acquisition, and sustainment.


2.12. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments:

   a. Organize, train, equip, and otherwise prepare their respective active and reserve
component forces to operate successfully in CB environments.

   b. Develop and integrate CB defense into risk-informed concepts and capability requirement
documents for joint and Military Service-specific missions.

   c. Budget for operating and sustaining CB defense equipment and materiel.

   d. Develop and provide to the ASD(NCB) CB defense-specific POM recommendations.

   e. Support the CBDP and the DoD EA for the CBDP to conduct joint development,
acquisition, and sustainment.

   f. Manage and sustain infrastructure required to perform CBDP-related RDT&E.


2.13. SECRETARY OF THE ARMY.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.12., the Secretary of the Army, pursuant to
Sections 1522(c)(1) and (d)(4) of Title 50, U.S.C., and in accordance with DoDD 5101.01, in
their capacity as the DoD EA for the CBDP:

   a. Coordinates and integrates RDT&E and acquisition requirements of the Military
Departments for the CBDP.

   b. Reviews all funding requirements for the CBDP.


2.14. CJCS.

The CJCS:

    a. Pursuant to Sections 163 and 181 of Title 10, U.S.C., advises the Secretary of Defense by
identifying, assessing, approving, and prioritizing joint force CB defense gaps to meet the
National Defense Strategy.

    b. Advises the Secretary of Defense on CB defense risk management to support the
responsibilities in Sections 153 and 163 of Title 10, U.S.C.

   c. In coordination with the other DoD Component heads, and pursuant to DoDD 5105.77 for
National Guard forces, validates and prioritizes critical deficiencies and strengths in joint force
capabilities related to CB defense and CB-related threats against DoD personnel, equipment, and
weapon systems.

   d. Integrates CB defense requirements through the Joint Capabilities and Integration
Development System to support the CBDP planning, programming, budgeting, and execution
process and the Defense Acquisition System.

   e. Ensures alignment with CB defense-related biosurveillance activities to support enhanced
coordination between DoD global public health reporting and biodefense in accordance with
DoDD 6420.02.


2.15. CHIEF, NATIONAL GUARD BUREAU.

In accordance with DoDD 5105.77 and pursuant to Section 10502 of Title 10, U.S.C., the Chief,
National Guard Bureau:

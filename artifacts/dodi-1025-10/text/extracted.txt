               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY. This issuance:

    a. Applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs
of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector General of the
Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

    b. Applies only to professional military education and training, or civilian institution degree
programs specifically concerned with resource allocation and management functions provided by
the CFO Academy.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. The Department of Defense will operate and maintain a CFO Academy within the
National Defense University’s (NDU) College of Information and Cyberspace (CIC) to foster
financial management excellence.

   b. The CFO Academy provides degree programs specifically concerned with resource
management allocation functions.

   c. Nothing in this issuance restricts the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff or NDU
President in exercising their authority, direction, and control over NDU.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE (COMPTROLLER)/CHIEF FINANCIAL
OFFICER, DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE (USD(C)/CFO). The USD(C)/CFO:

    a. Monitors the CFO Academy, advocates for its resources, and advises the NDU President
regarding its curriculum and other activities.

   b. Chairs the CFO Academy Advisory Board (CFOA AB).

   c. Solicits members of the CFOA AB to provide nominations for two visiting chairs: one
from DoD and one from either the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) or other federal
agencies as coordinated with the OMB, on a continuous basis. The visiting chair assignment
may qualify as a senior level financial management developmental assignment experience in
accordance with the DoD Financial Management Certification Program, in accordance with
DoDI 1300.26.

   d. Provides assistance and collaboration to the CFOA AB for research and analysis.

   e. Invites OMB to participate on the CFOA AB and in the curriculum review.


2.2. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS. As it
relates to the CFO Academy and workforce professional development, in accordance with DoD
Directive 5124.02 and in addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., the Under Secretary of
Defense for Personnel and Readiness supports the USD(C)/CFO with advice, assistance, and
collaboration on resource management education to ensure compliance with applicable
manpower personnel management statues, regulations, and instruction polices.


2.3. OSD AND DOD COMPONENT HEADS. The OSD and DoD Component heads:

   a. Nominate students to attend the CFO Academy.

   b. Nominate candidates for the DoD visiting chair.

    c. Participate in the curriculum review process when requested (the DoD Inspector General
is not required to comply with this responsibility).


2.4. SECRETARY OF THE ARMY. In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., and
in accordance with the CJCSI 1801.01 Series, the Secretary of the Army provides base
operations and facility support to the CFO Academy on Fort McNair, Washington D.C. through
Joint Base Myer-Henderson Hall and logistic support through the Logistic Readiness Center.

2.5. CHAIRMAN OF THE JOINT CHIEFS OF STAFF. The Chairman of the Joint Chiefs
of Staff exercises authority, direction, and control over the CIC. In addition to the
responsibilities in Paragraph 2.2., and as implemented in the CJCSI 1800.01 and 1801.01 series,
the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff provides support for NDU students and manpower as
required within available resources and while considering other Joint Staff priorities.


2.6. PRESIDENT, NDU. Under the authority, direction, and control of Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff, the President, NDU (or designated representative):

   a. Serves on and coordinates meetings of the CFOA AB.

   b. Is responsible for planning and resourcing of the CFO Academy, in accordance with
CJCSI 1801.01 Series.

   c. Includes the CFO Academy in the annual NDU CIC budget and financial plan for finances
and expenses incident to the operation of the CFO Academy, except for:

        (1) The pay, allowances, and permanent-change-of-station travel expenses of military or
civilian personnel detailed to assist in the management and operation of the CFO Academy,
including instructors. These expenses are paid for by the DoD Components to which the
personnel are assigned.

       (2) The pay, allowances, and temporary duty travel and per diem costs of military and
DoD civilian personnel attending the CFO Academy as students, which are paid for by the
sponsoring or parent organization.

        (3) Courses for non-DoD personnel, which will be provided under resource constraints
on a reimbursable basis. Tuition fees will be assessed for other federal agencies, in accordance
with DoDI 4000.19. For courses for non-federal organizations provided pursuant to applicable
law and regulation, tuition fees will be assessed in accordance with Chapter 4 of Volume 11A of
DoD 7000.14-R. Student costs such as travel, per diem, and subsistence are paid for by the
sponsoring organization or parent organization.

       (4) The pay and allowances, including housing allowance, for the visiting chairs. These
expenses will be paid for by the parent agency of the visiting chair.

       (5) Facilities and their maintenance, which is the responsibility of the Secretary of the
Army, in accordance with Paragraph 2.4.

   d. Provides administrative and logistical support to the CFO Academy.

   e. Facilitates coordination with DoD financial management components.

    f. Validates final selection for visiting chairs and concurs with the appointment and duration
of their assignments.

                             SECTION 3: CFO ACADEMY

3.1. CFO ACADEMY PURPOSE. The CFO Academy provides integrated, professional
education to selected military and civilian personnel involved in resource allocation and
management functions subject to Volume 410 of DoDI 1400.25. The CFO Academy:

    a. Conducts accredited graduate level education in the leadership of resource management
that enables DoD’s efficient and effective use of financial and other resources that supports
DoD’s mission under the CIC, in accordance with NDU Regulation 5100.01. The CFO
Academy achieves this by educating military and civilian personnel in DoD's financial
management competencies in support of the DoD Financial Management Certification Program,
in accordance with DoDI 1300.26.

   b. Provides such courses for foreign countries, international agencies and non-federal
organizations as may be requested in accordance with applicable laws and regulations, and as
may be justified on the basis of benefit to the U.S. Government, subject to the approval of the
Under Secretary of Defense for Policy.

    c. Develops understanding and improves competence in analysis and decision-making
techniques, as they apply to the allocation and use of resources.

    d. Develops knowledge and understanding of the concepts, applications, and techniques of
systems for managing defense resources.


3.2. CFO ACADEMY STRUCTURE. The CFO Academy is made up of:

   a. Faculty with the proper minimum qualifications and other subordinate positions and
elements as established by the Chancellor of the CIC, within authorized resources.

   b. Civilian students selected from DoD Components and federal agencies.

   c. Military students selected and nominated by their respective service.

   d. International students selected and nominated by their respective nations.

  e. A visiting chair, from either the OMB or other federal agencies as coordinated with the
OMB and by the Office of the USD(C)/CFO, and designated by the Chancellor of the CIC.

    f. A visiting chair, provided by DoD, coordinated by the Office of USD(C)/CFO, and
designated by the Chancellor of the CIC.


3.3. CFOA AB. The CFO Academy is supported by the CFOA AB, which is established
pursuant to DoD Instruction 5105.18

    a. CFOA AB Composition. This board contains these officials or their designated
representative(s):

       (1) USD(C)/CFO, who serves as the Chair of the CFOA AB.

       (2) Under Secretary of Defense Personnel for Personnel and Readiness.

       (3) Chancellor of the CIC.

       (4) Joint Staff Comptroller.

       (5) Assistant Secretary of the Army, Financial Management and Comptroller.

       (6) Assistant Secretary of the Navy, Financial Management and Comptroller.

       (7) Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Financial Management

       (8) Director, Defense Finance and Accounting Service.

       (9) Director, Defense Contract Audit Agency.

       (10) Deputy Comptroller, OMB.

      (11) Financial Management OSD Functional Community Manager, serving as CFOA
AB Secretary.

   b. CFOA AB Actions. The CFOA AB:

      (1) Provides curricula, research, and consultation guidance for the operation of the CFO
Academy.

       (2) Oversees annual review of the CFO Academy curricular content.

      (3) Provides stakeholder perspective on course priorities and required student throughput
demographics.

       (4) Convenes twice per year with representatives from its respective member
organizations.

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all

The changes to this issuance are administrative and update language to comply with the
March 17, 2025 Secretary of Defense Memorandum.

               SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES AND FUNCTIONS
Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P), pursuant to DoD Directive
(DoDD) 5111.01, the ASD(HD&HA) is the principal civilian advisor to the Secretary of Defense
and the USD(P) for all matters regarding the formulation of national security and defense
strategy related to homeland defense (HD) activities; defense support of civil authorities
(DSCA); engagement with national security forces of partner countries on select transboundary
threats; defense relationships with countries in the Western Hemisphere; and issues pertaining to
the Arctic region. The ASD(HD&HA)’s responsibilities also include the development and
oversight of DoD strategy, policy, plans, and capabilities for DoD HD activities; DSCA; defense
continuity; mission assurance (MA); Arctic and global resilience; Western Hemisphere affairs;
critical infrastructure security and resilience; combating terrorism; and general matters related to
the overall supervision of HD activities. The ASD(HD&HA) also serves as the DoD Agency
Coordination Lead (ACL) for Anomalous Health Incidents (AHI) in accordance with the April
11, 2022, Secretary of Defense Memorandum. In the exercise of assigned responsibilities, and in
fulfillment of the National Defense Strategy, the ASD(HD&HA):

   a. HD and Related Matters.

       (1) Provides overall supervision of HD activities of the Department of Defense.

          (a) Develops, coordinates, and oversees implementation of DoD policy for HD and
the commitment of forces and other DoD resources for HD activities.

           (b) Coordinates on matters regarding the readiness posture of forces to conduct HD.

        (2) Serves as the DoD focal point for participation in the interagency development of
integrated national preparedness frameworks and associated plans, including the implementation
of Presidential Policy Directive (PPD)-8 as appropriate.

       (3) Consistent with DoDD 5030.19, develops, coordinates, and oversees the
implementation of DoD policy for operational use of the air domain in the United States,
including use of uncrewed aircraft systems in accordance with the October 31, 2023, Secretary of
Defense Memorandum, and use of counter-small uncrewed aircraft systems.

   b. DSCA.

        (1) Pursuant to DoDD 3025.18, develops, coordinates, and oversees implementation of
DoD policy for DSCA plans, preparedness, and activities, including DSCA related to incident
response, civilian law enforcement, special events, public health emergencies and medical
services, civil disturbances, and domestic chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear, and high-
yield explosives response matters.

       (2) Coordinates on matters related to the preparedness and posture of forces to conduct
DSCA and the impact of DSCA on the ability of the Department of Defense to perform its
primary missions.

   c. Defense Continuity.

    Develops and oversees DoD policy for continuity of operations (COOP), continuity of
government (COG), enduring constitutional government (ECG), and defense crisis management
in accordance with PPD-40, the Federal Mission Resilience Strategy, DoDD 3020.44, and
DoDI 3020.26. In this capacity, the ASD(HD&HA):

       (1) Establishes cooperative relationships with partners on COOP and COG. Reviews
and evaluates plans, programs, and requirements for such forces, systems, and capabilities to
ensure consistency with strategy and policy.

       (2) Supports the USD(P) in the USD(P)’s role as the DoD Continuity Coordinator,
serving as the single point of contact for other Federal departments and agencies for continuity
matters in coordination with the CJCS and the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and
Security, in accordance with DoDI 3020.39, for the Defense Intelligence Enterprise. In this
capacity, the ASD(HD&HA):

           (a) In coordination with the DoD Chief Information Officer (DoD CIO), develops
and distributes requirements for continuity communications capabilities supporting DoD and
national missions in accordance with applicable Federal and Secretary of Defense guidance and
direction.

          (b) Pursuant to the National Defense Strategy, and in coordination with the DoD
CIO, provides overarching policy guidance, priorities, and goals for National Leadership
Command Capability in support of COOP, COG, and ECG.

         (c) Advises the Secretary of Defense, through the USD(P), on all aspects of COOP,
COG, and ECG, including execution of mission and operational requirements.

           (d) Ensures the Department of Defense plans, maintains, tests, and exercises
comprehensive and effective continuity capabilities for the uninterrupted execution of DoD
mission-essential functions in accordance with DoDI 3020.47.

           (e) In coordination with the CJCS, develops and executes at least one annual
continuity exercise.

            (f) Oversees risk management of continuity programs and plans in accordance with
DoDI 3020.45 to ensure that operational readiness decisions appropriately consider mission
distributed postures and the probability of an attack or event and its consequences.

   d. Defense Crisis Management.

    Serves as the DoD Domestic Crisis Manager, pursuant to DoDD 3020.44. In this capacity,
and in coordination with the Secretaries of the Military Departments, the CJCS, other DoD
Component heads, and Principal Staff Assistants (PSAs), as appropriate, the ASD(HD&HA)
develops, coordinates, and oversees the implementation of DoD-wide policy for domestic crisis
management. These efforts include oversight of appropriate programs, processes, information
sharing capabilities and procedures, and supporting infrastructure, together with information
technology, to implement crisis management within the Department of Defense.

   e. Critical Infrastructure Security and Resilience.

    Serves as the lead within the Office of the USD(P) for DoD defense critical infrastructure
(DCI) and broader infrastructure security and resilience efforts. Leads DoD implementation of
the roles and responsibilities specified in National Security Memorandum (NSM)-22 required to
strengthen and maintain secure, functioning, and resilient critical infrastructure against both
physical and cyber threats. For cyber threats, performs a supporting role for cybersecurity issues
relating to the defense industrial base, consistent with the responsibilities of the coordinating
authority for cybersecurity issues relating to the defense industrial base prescribed by
Section 1724 of Public Law 116-283 (also known as the “William M. (Mac) Thornberry
National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) for Fiscal Year (FY) 2021”) and referred to in this
issuance as the NDAA for FY 2021. Develops and oversees DoD policy for MA in accordance
with DoDD 3020.40. Advocates for improved resilience across the Department of Defense and
the U.S. Government in support of Federal Mission Resilience Strategy goals. In this capacity,
the ASD(HD&HA):

     (1) Develops DoD policy for the resilience of DCI, to include energy, water, and
communications systems due to the enabling functions they provide across all missions.

       (2) Coordinates with the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and other relevant
Federal departments and agencies and, in coordination with appropriate DoD Component heads,
collaborates with critical infrastructure owners and operators.

       (3) Develops and oversees DoD strategy and policy to identify, prioritize, assess,
remediate, and secure DCI supporting primary mission-essential functions and or tasks.
Executes critical infrastructure security and resilience responsibilities through the MA construct
in accordance with DoDD 3020.40 and DoDI 3020.45.

         (4) Coordinates with relevant stakeholders, including the Assistant Secretary of Defense
for Cyber Policy and DoD Principal Cyber Advisor; the DoD CIO; Commander, United States
Cyber Command; Component Principal Cyber Advisors and chief information officers; and the
coordinating authority for cybersecurity issues relating to the defense industrial base designated
in accordance with Section 1724 of the NDAA for FY 2021 to strengthen and maintain resilient
critical infrastructure from cyber threats.

       (5) Oversees and implements the MA Coordination Board governance structure, in
accordance with DoDI 3020.45, to manage risk at the DoD level through the MA Senior Steering

   f. Combating Terrorism.

   In this capacity, the ASD(HD&HA):

       (1) Leads all efforts within the Office of the USD(P) for DoD support to domestic
counterterrorism activities.

             (a) Aligns defense policies with national policies for the prevention and disruption of
terrorist attacks in the homeland in support of key U.S. Government partners, including the
National Security Council (NSC) staff, DHS, and the Department of Justice, including the
Federal Bureau of Investigation.

           (b) Aligns HD policies, plans, and programs concerning the prevention and
disruption of the use of weapons of mass destruction by terrorists in the homeland, and
synchronizes domestic policy with overall DoD counterterrorism policies, plans, and programs.

           (c) Develops, coordinates, and oversees the implementation of DoD policy
concerning requests by other Federal departments and agencies for DoD assistance in response to
domestic terrorism crises. Advises the Secretary of Defense and other DoD officials, through the
USD(P), on the use of DoD resources and capabilities in domestic counterterrorism roles.

        (2) Develops, coordinates, and oversees the implementation of DoD force protection and
antiterrorism policies, standards, and guidance to reduce the vulnerability of DoD personnel,
dependent family members, installations, and other facilities and critical resources to hostile acts.

       (3) In coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security,
oversees the development, coordination, and maintenance of DoD policy for security of DoD
personnel abroad in accordance with DoDI 5210.84.

        (4) Coordinates with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Special Operations and Low-
Intensity Conflict on employment of U.S. special operations forces capabilities for combatting
terrorism requirements.

   g. Arctic and Global Resilience.

    The ASD(HD&HA) coordinates closely with DoD and other Executive Branch stakeholders
to develop guidance for and to oversee the implementation of strategy and policy issues relating
to Arctic security and engagement with national security forces of partner countries on defense-
related operational energy and environmental issues. In this capacity, the ASD(HD&HA):

        (1) Develops, coordinates, and integrates DoD Arctic strategy and policy, and conducts
and manages multilateral, regional, and bilateral coordination with allies and partners related to
Arctic security, in close coordination with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for International
Security Affairs; the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Indo-Pacific Security Affairs; the CJCS;
Commander, United States Northern Command; Commander, United States European
Command; and the Secretaries of the Military Departments.

       (2) In accordance with DoDD 5200.41E, and in coordination with the Assistant
Secretary of Defense for Strategy, Plans, and Capabilities, provides regional policy guidance to
the Ted Stevens Center for Arctic Security Studies.

       (3) Develops, coordinates, and integrates DoD policy and strategy on engagement with
national security forces of partner countries on defense-related operational energy and
environmental issues.

        (4) Manages multilateral, regional, and bilateral activities with allies and partners related
to global resilience, in close coordination with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for
International Security Affairs; the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Indo-Pacific Security
Affairs; the CJCS; the Combatant Commanders (CCDRs); the Secretaries of the Military
Departments, and appropriate Federal departments and agencies.

       (5) Advances efforts with allies and partners to bolster energy security, including
addressing the strategic implications of changes in the energy security environment and the
advent of new energy technologies and efficiency approaches.

       (6) Develops, coordinates, and integrates DoD policy and processes, assigns
responsibilities, and provides oversight for the Freedom of Navigation Program, in accordance
with Presidential Decision Directive-32 and the notification of significant military exercises and
freedom of navigation assertions, in collaboration with the Department of State and the NSC
staff.

   h. Western Hemisphere Affairs.

    Develops international security strategy and policy that relate to the nations and international
organizations of the Western Hemisphere, including their governments and defense
establishments; and provides regional oversight of security cooperation programs, including
foreign military sales in these regions. In this capacity, the ASD(HD&HA):

       (1) Conducts and manages multilateral, regional, and bilateral defense relations with
foreign governments in the Western Hemisphere.

        (5) Provides direction to AHI CFT staff on a day-to-day basis, under the authority,

                             SECTION 3: RELATIONSHIPS

In performing assigned functions and responsibilities, the ASD(HD&HA):

    a. Reports directly to the USD(P), who exercises authority, direction, and control.
Regarding matters related to the role of the ASD(HD&HA) as the DoD ACL for AHI, reports
directly to the Secretary of Defense, in accordance with Section 6603 of the NDAA for FY 2022.

   b. Coordinates and exchanges information with other OSD officials, the DoD Component

3.2. PSAs AND DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The PSAs and DoD Component heads coordinate with the ASD(HD&HA) on matters under their
purview related to the responsibilities and functions and authorities assigned in this issuance.

                              SECTION 4: AUTHORITIES
In accordance with DoD policies and issuances, the ASD(HD&HA) is delegated the authority to:

    a. Approve DoD manuals and directive-type memorandums in areas of assigned
responsibilities that implement policy already established by a DoDD, DoDI, directive-type
memorandum, or policy memorandum issued by the Secretary of Defense or Deputy Secretary of
Defense.

       (1) In these documents, assign responsibilities related to the authorities and
responsibilities in this issuance to PSAs and the DoD Component heads. Assignment of
responsibilities in these DoD issuances to Military Department officials must be made through
the Secretaries of those Departments. DoD issuances assigning responsibilities to the Combatant
Commands must be coordinated with the CJCS.

       (2) Such documents must be fully coordinated in accordance with DoDI 5025.01.

       (3) This authority may not be redelegated.

    b. Communicate directly with the PSAs and DoD Component heads, as necessary, to carry
out assigned responsibilities and functions, including transmitting requests for advice and
assistance. Communications to Military Department officials must be transmitted through the
Secretaries of the Military Departments or as otherwise provided in law or as directed by the
Secretary of Defense or Deputy Secretary of Defense in other DoD issuances. Communications
to the CCDRs must be in accordance with DoDD 5100.01.

    c. Communicate with other government officials, members of the public, and representatives
of foreign governments, as appropriate, in carrying out assigned responsibilities and functions.
Communications with representatives and members of the Legislative Branch must be conducted
through the Office of the ASD(LA), except for communications with the Committees on
Appropriations of the Senate and the House of Representatives, which must be coordinated with
the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department
of Defense.

   d. Communicate with agencies of the Executive Branch and Congress on special access
programs. For all special access program matters, the ASD(HD&HA) must conduct
communications with agencies of the Executive Branch and Congress consistent with
DoDD 5205.07.

    e. Obtain reports and information, in accordance with DoDI 8910.01, as necessary, to carry
out assigned responsibilities and functions.

   f. Establish arrangements for DoD participation in appropriate non-DoD governmental
programs for which the ASD(HD&HA) has been assigned primary DoD responsibility.

    g. Negotiate and conclude international agreements in areas of assigned responsibility, when
authorized by and in accordance with DoDI 5530.03. This authority is subject to coordination
with the General Counsel of the Department of Defense. For agreements addressed in

SECTION 5: REGIONAL AND COUNTRY FOCUS OF ASD(HD&HA)
The alignment of individual countries under the focus of the ASD(HD&HA) and the other
Assistant Secretaries of Defense with regional responsibilities is subject to adjustment by the
USD(P). Adjustments will be accomplished by administrative changes, pursuant to
DoDD 5111.01.

   a. North America.

   Includes Canada and Mexico.

   b. Central America.

   Includes Belize, Costa Rica, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Nicaragua, and Panama.

   c. South America.

   Includes Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Guyana, Paraguay, Peru,
Suriname, Uruguay, and Venezuela.

   d. Arctic Region.

    Includes the Arctic, as defined in Section 112 of Public Law 98-373 (also known and referred
to as the “Arctic Research and Policy Act of 1984”) and the Arctic region more broadly, which
includes the whole State of Alaska and Canada.

   e. Caribbean.

   Includes Antigua and Barbuda, the Bahamas, Barbados, Cuba, Dominica, the Dominican
Republic, Grenada, Haiti, Jamaica, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia, Saint Vincent and the
Grenadines, and Trinidad and Tobago.

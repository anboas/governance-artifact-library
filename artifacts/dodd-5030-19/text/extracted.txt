                                DOD DIRECTIVE 5030.19
             DOD RESPONSIBILITIES ON FEDERAL AVIATION

Originating Component:        Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment

Effective:                    March 6, 2023

Releasability:                Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                              at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Reissues and Cancels:         DoD Directive 5030.19, “DoD Responsibilities on Federal Aviation,”
                              September 13, 2013, as amended

Approved by:                  Kathleen H. Hicks, Deputy Secretary of Defense


Purpose: In accordance with the authority in Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.), this issuance:
• Designates the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment (USD(A&S)) as the
DoD official with the authority to establish policy, assign responsibilities, and provide procedures for
DoD interactions with the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) and other interagency partners with
Federal aviation equities.
• Establishes the DoD Policy Board on Federal Aviation (PBFA) and assigns responsibilities to the
DoD PBFA in accordance with DoD Instruction 5105.18.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands (CCMDs), the Office of
Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field
Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this
issuance as the “DoD Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

It is DoD policy to:

    a. Align DoD programs and initiatives in a comprehensive and cohesive effort to provide
aviation security and defend United States sovereign airspace, pursuant to National Security
Presidential Directive-47/Homeland Security Presidential Directive-16 (NSPD-47/HSPD-16) and
the National Strategy for Aviation Security.

    b. Support interagency partnerships established to plan and implement air transportation
system modernization for the United States and related international initiatives.

    c. Ensure applicable DoD activities are aligned with National Airspace System (NAS)
planning efforts to support an efficient and cost-effective development process, while
maintaining focus on the United States’ national security and homeland defense requirements,
including the responsibilities in Executive Order 11161.

   d. Ensure the DoD Components have sufficient access to the airspace and equipment
necessary to meet worldwide operational, training, exercise, test, and evaluation requirements for
peacetime, contingency, and wartime operations and planning.

    e. Support creating appropriate international civil and military cooperation mechanisms to
effectively and efficiently manage airspace systems.

   f. Release airspace designated for military use to the FAA or other air navigation service
providers, as appropriate, when the airspace is no longer needed for military requirements or
security considerations.

    g. Ensure appropriate DoD organizations actively participate as members in U.S.
Government delegations to international aviation, air traffic control, and airspace management
forums.

    h. Ensure operational and system interoperability among the DoD, FAA, and global civil and
military systems, except where unique military requirements, security, or other need-to-know
issues require otherwise.

    i. Ensure policies and procedures are in place to reduce the hostile use of navigational
signals in accordance with Part 245 of Title 32, Code of Federal Regulations.

    j. Ensure DoD operations in the NAS are aligned with cybersecurity requirements of
national security systems in accordance with Executive Order 14028 and National Security
Memorandum 8.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. USD(A&S).

The USD(A&S):

   a. Provides policy guidance on, and oversees, DoD interactions with the FAA on all NAS
matters to enable secure and resilient capabilities to the warfighter.

   b. Appoints a principal representative to the PBFA and its subgroups and advisory bodies
when the PBFA Chair or Executive Director requests.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION (ASD(A)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(A&S), the ASD(A):

    a. Recommends policy changes to DoD Components, and oversees DoD interactions with
the FAA on all NAS matters.

   b. Develops and synchronizes policy for DoD matters related to:

       (1) Air traffic control.

       (2) Airspace.

       (3) Joint use agreements at civil airports.

      (4) Associated communications, navigation, surveillance, and air traffic management
(CNS/ATM) systems development and acquisition activities.

    c. Maintains policies and procedures that ensure military access to airspace and protects
military equities in training, testing, and operational mission requirements.

   d. Supports the guidance in NSPD-47/HSPD-16 and the National Strategy for Aviation
Security by aligning DoD aviation security programs and initiatives in a comprehensive and
cohesive national effort to provide active layered aviation security and defense in-depth for the
United States.

   e. Facilitates transferring DoD technology to support the NAS architecture in accordance
with the National Strategy for Aviation Security so national security, homeland defense, and
mission capability are not jeopardized.

    f. Oversees policy on DoD interactions with Federal, State, and other public and private
sector entities in accordance with the National Strategy for Information Sharing.

    g. Serves as the focal point for issues concerning interagency or international acquisition
planning and technology the DoD transfers to support developing NAS.

    h. Designates the Chair of the Overarching Integrated Product Team under the Defense
Acquisition Board to provide acquisition guidance related to CNS/ATM, in accordance with
effective and efficient life-cycle management practices outlined in Chapter 1 of the Defense
Acquisition Guidebook and in accordance with DoD Instruction 4151.19.

   i. Appoints the PBFA Chair.

   j. Appoints a working group representative to the PBFA and its subgroups and advisory

2.3. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ENERGY, INSTALLATIONS, AND
ENVIRONMENT.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(A&S) and in accordance with
Section 183a of Title 10, U.S.C., and Part 211 of Title 32, Code of Federal Regulations, the
Assistant Secretary of Defense for Engergy, Installations, and Environment advises the ASD(A)
and the PBFA on:

    a. Issues related to building and permitting proposed projects that are filed with the Secretary
of Transportation pursuant to Section 183a of Title 10, U.S.C. and Section 44718 of Title 49,
U.S.C.

   b. Mitigating electromagnetic interference impacts that proposed projects described in
Paragraph 2.3.a. may have on DoD aviation activities.


2.4. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR INDUSTRIAL BASE POLICY
(ASD(IBP)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(A&S), the ASD(IBP):

   a. Oversees, and recommends changes to, policy on DoD interactions with the FAA on
industrial base matters.

    b. Advises the DoD PBFA on issues relating to international investment in the United States
consistent with national security protections, in accordance with Executive Order 11858 and
Title XVII of Public Law 115-232.

   c. Appoints a working group representative to the PBFA and its subgroups and advisory
bodies when the PBFA Chair or Executive Director requests.

2.5. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR RESEARCH AND ENGINEERING
(USD(R&E)).

The USD(R&E):

   a. Appoints a principal and working group representative to the PBFA and its subgroups and
advisory bodies when the PBFA Chair or Executive Director requests.

   b. Ensures Test Resource Management Center and Major Range and Test Facility Base
programs and equities are aligned with NAS development.


2.6. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR POLICY (USD(P)).

The USD(P):

    a. Provides oversight and guidance on policy matters related to USD(P) responsibilities
pertaining to this issuance.

   b. Appoints a principal representative to the PBFA and its subgroups and advisory bodies
when the PBFA Chair or Executive Director requests.


2.7. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR HOMELAND DEFENSE AND
HEMISPHERIC AFFAIRS (ASD(HD&HA)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P), the ASD(HD&HA):

   a. Oversees, and recommends changes to, policy on:

        (1) Air domain issues related to homeland defense integration, defense support of civil
authorities, DoD asset and infrastructure protection, defense readiness, and employment of
counter-small unmanned aircraft systems.

      (2) DoD aviation cybersecurity, in close coordination with the Assistant Secretary of
Defense for Space Policy and the Commander, United States Cyber Command.

       (3) DoD interactions with the FAA and other Federal agencies related to:

           (a) Securing and defending the homeland in the air domain, particularly, but not
exclusively, as they pertain to the implementation of NSPD-47/HSPD-16 and the National
Strategy for Aviation Security.

           (b) Homeland air defense operations conducted by the North American Aerospace
Defense Command (NORAD), the United States Northern Command (USNORTHCOM), the
United States Indo-Pacific Command, and other CCMDs, as required.

   b. Appoints a principal and working group representative to the PBFA and its subgroups
and/or advisory bodies when the PBFA Chair or Executive Director requests.

2.14. DOD CHIEF INFORMATION OFFICER (DOD CIO).

The DoD CIO:

    a. In accordance with DoD Directive 5144.02, develops DoD strategy and policy
recommendations for information technology (IT); communications; spectrum management;
network standards; information systems; cybersecurity; network-centric; positioning, navigation,
and timing policy; and information enterprise that supports DoD command and control,
including interoperability, collaboration, and interface between DoD and non-DoD systems.

   b. Oversees, and recommends changes to, policy on DoD interactions with the FAA and
other Federal agencies to implement NSPD-47/HSPD-16 and the National Strategy for Aviation
Security.

   c. Appoints a principal and working group representative to the PBFA and its subgroups and
advisory bodies when the PBFA Chair or Executive Director requests.


2.15. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments:

   a. Appoint a principal and working group representative to the PBFA and its subgroups
and/or advisory bodies when the PBFA Chair or Executive Director requests.

   b. Coordinate with the PBFA on CNS/ATM issues, as appropriate, to synchronize policies
and decisions concerning airspace access.

    c. Notify the PBFA of potential risks affecting military equities concerning access to
airspace for training, testing, and operational mission requirements.

    d. Advise the PBFA Chair and Executive Director when substantial changes are planned to
the use of military airports, landing areas, missile or rocket sites, or other facilities that may
affect the NAS or international air traffic.

    e. Coordinate all common future CNS/ATM-related research, development, and acquisition
efforts with the PBFA Chair and Executive Director.

    f. Support the guidance in NSPD-47/HSPD-16 and the National Strategy for Aviation
Security by aligning DoD aviation security programs and initiatives in a comprehensive and
cohesive national effort to provide layered aviation security and defense in-depth for the United
States.

2.16. SECRETARY OF THE AIR FORCE (SECAF).

                                SECTION 3: DOD PBFA

3.1. ORGANIZATION AND MANAGEMENT.

    a. Individuals appointed as PBFA principal members should be at the three star general
officer/flag officer or Senior Executive Service equivalent. Organizations without a three star
general officer/flag officer or Senior Executive Service equivalent available to serve as a PBFA
principal member will provide the most senior representative available. The principal PBFA
group is led by the PBFA Chair, and membership will consist of:

       (1) A general officer/flag officer, Senior Executive Service, or senior executive
representative from the Offices of the:

           (a) USD(A&S).

           (b) USD(R&E).

           (c) USD(P).

           (d) USD(C)/CFO.

           (e) GC DoD.

           (f) DOT&E.

           (g) DoD CIO.

       (2) One representative to represent both NORAD and USNORTHCOM.

       (3) One representative from the Office of the CJCS.

       (4) One representative from each Military Department.

    b. The PBFA working group chair is the PBFA Executive Director, and membership should
be at the O-6 level, or civilian equivalent. Organizations without an O-6 or equivalent individual
will provide a comparable representative. PBFA working group membership will consist of:

       (1) A senior field grade officer or equivalent representative from the Offices of the:

           (a) ASD(A).

           (b) ASD(IBP).

           (c) USD(R&E).

           (d) ASD(HD&HA).

           (e) USD(C)/CFO.

           (f) USD(I&S).

           (g) NGA.

           (h) GC DoD.

       (2) One representative to represent both NORAD and USNORTHCOM.

   c. The ASD(A) will appoint the PBFA Chair.

    d. The PBFA Executive Director, who will be appointed by the SECAF in coordination with

    f. The PBFA Chair or PBFA Executive Director may invite other U.S. Government
organizations that have an interest in Federal aviation and NAS matters to be represented at the
PBFA or its subgroups in a non-member status, or to participate in meetings during which
matters relevant to their organization’s interests will be addressed, provided those organization’s
attendee(s) are full-time or permanent part-time Federal employees.

    g. The PBFA Secretariat will help the PBFA Chair and PBFA Executive Director execute
their responsibilities in accordance with this issuance and Executive Order 11161.


3.2. FUNCTIONS.

   a. The PBFA will advise and assist the ASD(A) on matters related to:

       (1) Air traffic control.

       (2) Airspace management.

       (3) The NAS.

       (4) Joint systems acquisition.

        (5) International affairs.

  b. The PBFA will establish policies, procedures, and priorities in coordination with the
USD(P) to effectively develop international civil and military aviation cooperation programs.

    c. The PBFA principal members will serve as the PBFA Senior Advisory Group (SAG) to
the DoD on military aviation matters related to future NAS design. Although not a member of

ensure seamless operations between their two functions.

    f. The PBFA will be one of the DoD liaisons with:

        (1) International aviation organizations.

        (2) Foreign nation civil and military aviation departments, agencies, and air navigation
service providers.

   g. The PBFA will notify the FAA whenever substantial changes are planned to the use of
U.S. military airports, landing areas, missile or rocket sites, or other facilities that may affect the
NAS.

   h. The PBFA Chair, Executive Director, and SAG will synchronize DoD-wide policies and
decisions concerning the NAS throughout the DoD.

    i. The PBFA working group will provide the mechanism for addressing medium- to long-
term user and interoperability requirements planning for the NAS.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 air domain                The global airspace, including domestic, international, and foreign
                           airspace, as well as all manned and unmanned aircraft operating and
                           people and cargo present in that airspace, and all aviation-related
                           infrastructures.

 CNS/ATM                   The exchange of voice and data information between pilots and air
                           traffic controllers or flight information centers. It includes
                           communication of navigation information from aircraft to air traffic
                           control centers, which facilitates the continuous mapping of the
                           relative positions of aircraft.

 IGIA                      A group created in 1960 to provide coordinated interagency
                           recommendations on aviation matters to the U.S. Department of
                           State. The FAA manages the IGIA, and the PBFA Secretariat
                           coordinates with the IGIA Secretariat for DoD policy positions on
                           international aviation matters.

 NAS                       The common network of U.S. airspace-air navigation facilities,
                           equipment and services; airports or landing areas; aeronautical charts;
                           technical information and services; rules, regulations and procedures;
                           and manpower and material. Includes system components shared
                           jointly with the military.

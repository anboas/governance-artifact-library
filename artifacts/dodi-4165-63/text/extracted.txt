SUBJECT:      DoD Housing

References: See Enclosure


1. PURPOSE. This Instruction:

    a. Reissues DoD Directive 4165.63 (Reference (a)) as an Instruction to update policy and
assign responsibilities for the management of DoD housing in accordance with the authority in
DoD Directive 5134.01 (Reference (b)).

   b. Continues authorization for the publication of DoD 4165.63-M (Reference (c)), in
accordance with DoD Instruction 5025.01 (Reference (d)), to consolidate policy guidance and
procedures for the DoD Housing Management Program.


2. APPLICABILITY. This Instruction:

    a. Applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs
of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector General of the
Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the Department of Defense (hereafter referred to collectively as the
“DoD Components”).

    b. Encompasses housing for eligible personnel. All housing is covered to include units that
are owned and operated by the Department of Defense as identified in DoD 7000.14-R
(Reference (e)), or units that are privatized as identified in chapter 169 of title 10, United States
Code (Reference (f)).


3. POLICY. It is DoD policy:

    a. To ensure that eligible personnel and their families have access to affordable, quality
housing facilities and services consistent with grade and dependent status and generally
reflecting contemporary community living standards.

   b. To rely on the private sector as the primary source for housing accompanied and
unaccompanied personnel normally eligible to draw a housing allowance.

   c. To use a consistent DoD-wide analytical methodology for calculating the need to provide
housing.

    d. That the Commander of an installation shall be responsible for the military housing
programs with broad authority to decide the best use of resources to provide access to housing
for eligible personnel and their families.


4. RESPONSIBILITIES

    a. Assistant Secretary of Defense for Energy, Installations, and Environment (ASD(EI&E)).
The ASD(EI&E), under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense
for Acquisition, Technology, and Logistics, shall:

     (1) Serve as the DoD Housing Management program manager for all housing, whether
DoD-owned or privatized.

      (2) In coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness
(USD(P&R)), provide oversight of all lodging privatization activities undertaken by the Military
Departments and develop performance standards and measures to ensure that privatization meets
the DoD lodging requirements, consistent with DoD Instruction 1015.11 (Reference (g)) and
DoD Instruction 1015.12 (Reference (h)).

      (3) Provide oversight of and take responsibility for DoD real property to ensure it is
managed in the most economical manner consistent with DoD Directive 4165.6 (Reference (i)).

   b. Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of
Defense. The Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of
Defense shall provide procedures to the DoD Components on financing, budgeting, and
accounting for the DoD housing programs under DoD Directive 5118.03 (Reference (j)).

   c. USD(P&R). The USD(P&R) shall:

       (1) Monitor the morale and welfare aspects of DoD housing programs and services.

        (2) Issue policy and guidance for equal opportunity in off-base housing in accordance
with DoD Directive 5124.02 (Reference (k)) and for accessibility of housing to persons with
disabilities.

       (3) Serve as the principal DoD point of contact on all policy matters relating to DoD
lodging and the DoD lodging program, in accordance with References (g) and (h).

   d. Heads of the DoD Components. The Heads of the DoD Components shall manage
housing, whether DoD-owned or privatized, within their respective jurisdictions, including
program and financial management, accounting, and reporting.


5. RELEASABILITY. Cleared for public release. This instruction is available on the
Directives Division Website at http://www.esd.whs.mil/Directives/DD/.


6. SUMMARY OF CHANGE 2. This change reassigns the office of primary responsibility for
this instruction to the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment in accordance
with the July 13, 2018 Deputy Secretary of Defense Memorandum (Reference (l)).


7. EFFECTIVE DATE. This Instruction is effective July 21, 2008.

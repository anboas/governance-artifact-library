Program.

with DoD Directive (DoDD) 5101.01.

Assistant overseeing the activities of the DoD EA for AFE in accordance with DoDD 5101.01.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. The DoD AFE Program provides high-quality live and virtual professional entertainment
to Service personnel and their family members stationed outside the continental United States
(OCONUS). Priority is given to personnel in contingency operations, remote and isolated
locations, and ships at sea.

   b. Responsible communication and operations security practices will be applied when
promoting performances and using social media, in accordance with DoD Instruction
(DoDI) 8170.01 and DoDI 5400.17.

   c. Delays en route to or from the entertainment location may be authorized at U.S.
Government expense only when the delay is for the DoD’s convenience.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS.

The Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness:

   a. Serves as the Principal Staff Assistant overseeing the activities of the DoD EA for AFE in
accordance with DoDD 5101.01.

   b. Is responsible for the policy and oversight of the DoD AFE Program.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve Affairs develops DoD
policy governing the DoD AFE Program and provides oversight and guidance for implementing
the policies and responsibilities in this issuance.


2.3. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments assign at least one full-time active duty officer per
Military Service to:

   a. Staff and support the AFE office.

   b. Develop and validate entertainment needs.

   c. Collaborate on DoD-wide planning for effective and efficient program delivery.


2.4. SECRETARY OF THE AIR FORCE.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., and in their capacity as the DoD EA for
AFE, the Secretary of the Air Force:

   a. Administers the DoD AFE Program and designates and staffs an appropriate
organizational element within the Department of the Air Force as the AFE office.

    b. Manages the DoD AFE Program in accordance with DoDI 1015.10, this issuance, and
established policies and procedures.

   c. Coordinates annually with the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Military
Community and Family Policy to establish the scope of the DoD AFE Program and resource
requirements.

    d. Coordinates with the United Service Organizations, Incorporated, to provide invitational
travel orders for OCONUS entertainment in accordance with the January 7, 2008 Memorandum
of Understanding between the DoD and the United Service Organizations, Incorporated.

    e. Coordinates with Services when offering installation tours to ensure awareness and
visibility. Enters into contracts with a variety of entertainment groups to deliver high-quality
live and virtual professional entertainment to Service personnel and their families OCONUS.

   f. Budgets, funds, and maintains accountability for appropriated funds for authorized
expenses, including:

       (1) Performance fees, travel, lodging, production, and incidentals for entertainers.

       (2) Commercial and military air transportation for entertainment groups.

       (3) Civilian salaries and temporary duty costs for all AFE personnel.

   g. Clears all press releases concerning the DoD AFE Program with the Assistant to the
Secretary of Defense for Public Affairs before release in accordance with DoDD 5122.05.

    h. Obtains permission to film or record part or all of contracted performances from the
Assistant to the Secretary of Defense for Public Affairs in accordance with DoDD 5122.05 and
other Public Affairs policies, as applicable.

   i. Conducts periodic assessments and annual audits of AFE financial, operational, and
management controls.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 AFE                       A U.S. Air Force command operation that serves as the single point
                           of contact within the DoD for providing high-quality live and virtual
                           professional entertainment to Service personnel and their family
                           members stationed OCONUS.

 EA                        Defined in the DoD Dictionary of Military and Associated Terms.

 OCONUS                    Outside the 48 contiguous states and the District of Columbia. This
                           includes Alaska, Hawaii, and the U.S. territories and possessions.

                                DOD DIRECTIVE 5000.71
  RAPID FULFILLMENT OF COMBATANT COMMANDER URGENT
      OPERATIONAL NEEDS AND OTHER QUICK ACTION
                     REQUIREMENTS

Originating Component:        Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment

Effective:                    October 18, 2022

Releasability:                Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                              at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Reissues and Cancels:         DoD Directive 5000.71, “Rapid Fulfillment of Combatant Commander
                              Urgent Operational Needs,” August 24, 2012

Approved by:                  Kathleen H. Hicks, Deputy Secretary of Defense


Purpose: This issuance:
• Establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and provides direction to facilitate the urgent delivery of
capabilities in response to quick action requirements (QARs), consistent with all applicable laws and
governing regulations.
• Establishes the Warfighter Senior Integration Group (SIG) as a standing DoD-wide forum chaired by
the Deputy Secretary of Defense (DepSecDef) and co-chaired by the Vice Chairman of the Joint Chiefs
of Staff. The purpose of the Warfighter SIG is to lead and facilitate agile and rapid responses to
Combatant Commander QARs and other urgent and emergent operational needs. The Warfighter SIG
will recognize, respond to, and develop solutions to mitigate the risk of unforeseen threats to ongoing or
anticipated near-term operations.
   o QARs include, but are not limited to, joint urgent operational needs (JUONs), joint emergent
operational needs (JEONs), and DoD Component-specific urgent operational needs.
   o QARs may also include other Warfighter SIG-identified urgent issues; Secretary of Defense
(SecDef)- or DepSecDef-identified urgent issues; or SecDef or DepSecDef rapid acquisition authority
(RAA) actions.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to:

    a. OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff
(CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands (CCMDs), the Office of Inspector General
of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

   b. The requirements, resources, acquisition, fielding, operation, support, and disposition of
capabilities that fulfill QARs.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. The DoD’s highest priority is to provide warfighters involved in conflict or preparing for
imminent operations with the capabilities urgently needed to overcome unforeseen threats,
achieve mission success, and reduce risk of casualties. An agile U.S. defense is essential in a
world of rapid change. Responding with QARs occurs in 3 steps. The Warfighter SIG oversees,
prioritizes, and facilitates these steps:

       (1) The need or requirement is validated.

       (2) The appropriate DoD Component(s) identifies a valid solution, consisting of a
materiel solution and tactics, techniques, and procedures. To be valid for purposes of execution
through the Warfighter SIG, DoD Components must be capable of fielding the solution within 2
years of validation of the urgent need, in a manner that resolves or substantially mitigates the
underlying need.

       (3) DoD Components must rapidly execute the solution, including completing its
development, acquisition, identification, and prioritization of funding, training, and fielding.
Given the required timelines, development efforts will necessarily be minimal.

   b. DoD Components will use QAR processes to identify operational vulnerabilities that
require resources and capabilities:

       (1) Beyond those available through the global force management process.

      (2) More urgently than the traditional Planning, Programming, Budgeting and Execution
System and the deliberate lane of the Defense Acquisition System allow.

    c. The co-chairs of the Warfighter SIG will direct and prioritize actions and integrate DoD-
wide efforts with other stakeholders to resolve urgent requirements and manage the DoD
response to unforeseen threats. The Warfighter SIG is the overarching entity through which

OSD’s previous and future urgent needs organizations and task forces will report to the SecDef
regarding the status of QAR-related actions.

    d. DoD Components will consider all available authorities to fund, develop, assess, produce,
deploy, operate, and sustain solutions to QARs quickly. DoD Components will not delay the
fielding of an interim solution, even if it provides less than full capability, to further develop
another full technology solution. In those cases where technology is not sufficiently mature to
resolve or substantially mitigate the identified need within 2 years from the date of validation,
the requirement should be considered in the normal budget review process.

    e. DoD Components will link requirements, acquisition, budgeting processes, and decisions
to support a timeline appropriate to the need.

        (1) To the extent possible, DoD Components will accomplish actions to resolve QARs
by using parallel, rather than sequential, processes to refine and prioritize requirements, execute
urgent acquisition actions, and allocate resources on a more expedited timetable than the
deliberate Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution System process. DoD
Components will maximize transparency for congressional oversight, ensuring the disciplined
use of enabling authorities.

        (2) Subject to statutes and regulations, the DoD will optimize QAR solutions for speed
and accept reasonable risk with regard to cost; performance; and other doctrine, organization,
training, materiel, leadership and education, personnel, and facilities considerations. DoD
Components will take swift actions, and senior leaders will ensure that staffing processes do not
unnecessarily delay the fielding of critical capabilities.

      (3) DoD Components will not unduly delay communication of QARs to the acquisition
community to resolve information shortcomings that could be addressed by direct interaction
between the requestor and the responsible acquisition organization.

                            SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. JOINT RAPID ACQUISITION CELL (JRAC) EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR.

Under the authority, direction, and operational control of the DepSecDef, the JRAC Executive
Director:

   a. Serves as executive secretary to the Warfighter SIG.

   b. Convenes Warfighter SIG meetings at the request of the Warfighter SIG chair or co-chair.

   c. Develops implementing policies and procedures for the operation of the Warfighter SIG
and the JRAC.

   d. Establishes, chairs, or assigns working groups subordinate to the Warfighter SIG as
required to:

         (1) Integrate DoD Component efforts to facilitate agile and rapid responses to urgent
needs.

       (2) Respond to and mitigate the risk to ongoing or anticipated near-term operations from
unforeseen threats or, from SecDef- or DepSecDef-identified urgent issues.

    e. Receives and evaluates validated JUONs, JEONs, and Warfighter SIG-identified urgent
issues, SecDef - or DepSecDef-identified urgent issues. Designates DoD Components to be
responsible for the fulfillment of urgent requirements, except for RAA actions. Monitors the
progress and completion of the actions to ensure urgent fielding of needed capabilities.

    f. Maintains visibility of DoD Component efforts to satisfy QARs and informs the
Warfighter SIG of the funding, contracting, delivery, performance, training, fielding,
sustainment, and other issues affecting timely satisfaction of QARs.

    g. Facilitates, generates, and sponsors legislative and policy actions that promote the
effective, efficient, and timely satisfaction of validated QARs.

    h. Serves as the senior official within OSD, with primary responsibility for making
recommendations to the SecDef on the use of RAA, pursuant to Subsections (c) and (d) of
Section 806 of Public Law 107-314, as amended.

   i. Advises the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer,
Department of Defense (USD(C)/CFO) of resources required to facilitate the timely resolution of
urgent needs, and assist in the resolution of funding issues as required.

2.2. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION AND SUSTAINMENT
(USD(A&S)).

The USD(A&S):

   a. In the absence of the DepSecDef, serves as the chair of the Warfighter SIG.

   b. In coordination with the DepSecDef, designates a general or flag officer (GO/FO) or
Senior Executive Service (SES) member as the Warfighter SIG Executive Secretary and JRAC
Executive Director.

  c. Provides fiscal, administrative, logistic, and human capital resources to enable the
Warfighter SIG and the JRAC to effectively function.

   d. Establishes policy and prescribes procedures to facilitate DoD acquisition of urgent
capabilities to resolve QARs and to operate and sustain QAR solutions.


2.3. DIRECTOR, DEFENSE THREAT REDUCTION AGENCY (DTRA).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(A&S), the Director, DTRA:

   a. Designates a GO/FO or SES representative to the Warfighter SIG in accordance with
Section 3 of this issuance.

    b. Provides visibility to the Warfighter SIG of all strategies developed and actions taken in
response to QARs and other relevant DTRA initiatives.


2.4. USD(C)/CFO.

The USD(C)/CFO:

   a. Designates a GO/FO or SES representative to the Warfighter SIG in accordance with
Section 3 of this issuance.

    b. Establishes policy and prescribes procedures to ensure that QARs are rapidly funded and
that any use of execution year transfer authority is well disciplined and fully transparent to
Congress.

    c. Assesses DoD Component funding strategies to support QAR solutions and identifies and
recommends current execution year offsets when required.

   d. Supports and manages, in coordination with the JRAC Executive Director, the JUON
Fund as established by Section 2216a of Title 10, United States Code.

2.12. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments:

organizational readiness to identify and respond to QARs.

   e. Request RAAs when appropriate, including when waivers of law, regulation, or policy are

Manual 5000.78.

    f. Identify new legislative or authorities that could rapidly fill capability gaps and
recommend them to the JRAC Executive Director for action.

2.14. CJCS.

The CJCS:

    a. Designates the Vice CJCS as co-chair of the Warfighter SIG in accordance with Section 3
of this issuance.

    b. Establishes policies and prescribes procedures through which CCMDs may quickly
identify, prioritize, and communicate urgent capability gaps that represent a significant near-term
risk to mission success, or safety of personnel of the Military Services. Gaps may be associated
with an ongoing operation or represent such significant risk to future missions that the CCMD
believes urgent year of execution resourcing and acquisition actions are justified.

    c. Establishes procedures for the CCMDs, Military Services, ASD(SO/LIC) and combat
support agencies to identify emerging threats to ongoing or imminent operations, and promptly
notify the Warfighter SIG executive secretary of instances where urgent action may mitigate the
risk to tactical and operational level forces.

   d. Serves as the validation authority for JUONs and JEONs, and establishes procedures to
ensure the timely validation of needs. In validating such requirements, the CJCS should consider
both the operational risk and the likely impact of not addressing the capability gap on mission
success or the safety of U.S. forces.

    e. Establishes procedures for the Combatant Commanders to assess the sufficiency of rapidly
fielded solutions to mitigate QARs.

                       SECTION 3: DOD WARFIGHTER SIG

3.1. PURPOSE.

The Warfighter SIG is established to provide a standing DoD-wide forum with the mission of
leading and facilitating agile and rapid response to QARs and also recognizing, responding to,
and mitigating the risk of emerging threats to ongoing or anticipated near-term operations, and
SecDef- or DepSecDef-identified urgent issues.


3.2. MEMBERSHIP.

   a. DepSecDef, Chair

   b. Vice CJCS, Co-Chair

   c. Members include GO/FO or SES level representatives from:

       (1) Department of the Army.

       (2) Department of the Navy (two members, Navy and United States Marine Corps).

       (3) Department of the Air Force (two members, Air Force and Space Force).

       (4) USD(A&S).

       (5) Under Secretary of Defense for Policy.

       (6) USD(C)/CFO.

       (7) Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness.

       (8) Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence Security.

       (9) Under Secretary of Defense for Research and Engineering.

       (10) ASD(SO/LIC)

       (11) Director, Operational Test and Evaluation.

       (12) Director, Cost Assessment and Program Evaluation.

       (13) Director, DTRA.

       (14) JRAC Executive Director.

       (15) Combatant Commanders, as identified by one of the Warfighter SIG chairs.

       (16) Other members, as identified by one of the Warfighter SIG chairs.

   d. The JRAC Executive Director will serve as executive secretary.


3.3. RESPONSIBILITIES.

The Warfighter SIG:

    a. Ensures that all efforts across the DoD are synchronized, streamlined, and tightly
integrated to recognize, respond to, and mitigate the risk from emerging threats to ongoing or
anticipated near-term operations and SecDef- or DepSecDef-identified urgent issues.

   b. Prioritizes and directs actions to meet urgent requirements and directs action to identify,
develop, fund, acquire, field, and sustain solutions to those needs.

   c. Works with and through established organizations and structures to facilitate rapid
acquisition support.

   d. Reviews, assesses, and monitors critical warfighter and other QARs, identifies those
requiring rapid response, joint management, oversight, and resolution by the Warfighter SIG.

   e. Adjudicates issues concerning the fulfillment of validated QARs and reviews, monitors,
and oversees efforts to provide urgent acquisition support.

   f. Ensures reporting mechanisms are effectively communicating the highest priority
warfighter needs to the requirements, resourcing, and acquisition communities, as well as
providing timely feedback on the performance of solutions.

    g. Continuously assesses the actions taken by DoD Components in resolving QARs, and
directs the Components’ implementation of any changes necessary to satisfy urgent needs
quickly.

   h. Establishes DoD-level task forces, as required, to focus DoD efforts to resolve QARs or
address capability surprises.

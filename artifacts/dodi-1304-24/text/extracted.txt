   USE OF DIRECTORY INFORMATION ON SECONDARY SCHOOL
       STUDENTS FOR MILITARY RECRUITING PURPOSES

Originating Component:       Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness

                             at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

                             School Students for Military Recruiting Purposes,” September 20, 2005

Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02 and the provisions in Section 503
of Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.) and Section 7908 of Title 20, U.S.C., this issuance establishes
policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for accessing secondary school campuses and
student directory information for military recruiting purposes.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments (including the Coast Guard at all times,
including when it is a Service in the Department of Homeland Security by agreement with that
Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the
Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the
Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD.


1.2. POLICY.

The Department, and the Military Services, will work with education officials to obtain access to
directory information consistent with Section 503(c) of Title 10, U.S.C., and Section 7908 of
Title 20, U.S.C. Recruiters will strive for voluntary cooperation with school officials in
obtaining directory information. The directory information obtained from education officials
will be used to attract a high-quality cross-section of American youth for military service.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ASISSTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve Affairs:

    a. Provides overall guidance for implementing the policies and responsibilities established
by this issuance.

    b. Ensures full compliance by the Military Departments with this issuance and the
authorities referenced within it.

    c. In accordance with Section 503(b)(5) of Title 10, U.S.C., ensures any regulations
prescribed by the Secretaries of the Military Departments to implement the policies in this
issuance are as uniform as practicable.

    d. Oversees the management of the Defense Manpower Data Center’s Recruiter Access to
High Schools (RAHS) database as it pertains to the access to and use of secondary school student
directory information throughout the United States and in DoD Education Activity schools
worldwide.

    e. In accordance with Section 503(c) of Title 10, U.S.C., requests, receives, compiles, and
reports incidents of schools denying military recruiters access to student directory information
for recruiting purposes.


2.2. DIRECTOR, DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE HUMAN RESOURCES ACTIVITY.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, the Director, Department of Defense Human Resources Activity:

   a. Provides maintenance and support to ensure functionality of the RAHS database.

   b. Reports misuse of student directory information by the Military Services to the Assistant
Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve Affairs.


2.3. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND COMMANDANT OF
THE COAST GUARD.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Commandant of the Coast Guard:

    a. Ensure recruiting officials collect, compile, use, safeguard, and dispose of student
directory information in accordance with this issuance, Section 552a of Title 5, U.S.C. (also

   SECTION 3: GUIDANCE ON COLLECTING AND USING DIRECTORY
                        INFORMATION
    a. The Military Services will coordinate information collection procedures to avoid
duplication of effort and to facilitate continued access to education officials and student directory
information.

  b. Coordination will be accomplished at the local level by the InterService Recruitment
Committee (IRC).

    c. Military recruiters’ access to secondary school campuses and student directory
information will be entered in the RAHS database to maintain a centralized record of access
afforded to each Military Service.

    d. The local commander of the military recruiting service who obtains student directory
information will provide the information to the IRC. The IRC will compile and disseminate the
information to the appropriate recruiters from each of the Military Services.

   e. Student directory information, collected and compiled consistent with this issuance, will
only be used for military recruiting purposes and will be handled as follows:

        (1) Consistent with Section 503(c) of Title 10, U.S.C., and Section 7908 of Title 20,
U.S.C., directory information will be collected and compiled by the Military Services on each
student unless the parent, guardian, or student (if the student is 18 years or older) has submitted
to the school a request that such information not be released. No information will be collected
from private secondary schools that document a religious objection to service in the armed
forces.

        (2) When directory information is collected on individuals from sources other than
secondary schools and the level of education is missing, the Military Services will try to ensure
the information compiled is based on the date of birth, to include those individuals who are likely
to be enrolled in the eleventh grade (or its equivalent) or higher.

        (3) Consistent with the Privacy Act of 1974, as amended, DoD Instruction 5400.11 and
DoD 5400.11-R, the use and collection of student directory information is limited to the purpose
of military recruiting.

        (4) Directory information about any student will be maintained for no more than 3 years
after the initial date of collection and compilation. Disposition guidance, including destruction
of data and documentation, will comply with DoD Instruction 5015.02.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 directory                 The names, addresses, electronic mail address, and telephone listings
 information               of secondary school students.

 IRC                       A committee composed of commanders of the Army Recruiting
                           Battalion, the Navy Recruiting Command District, the Air Force
                           Recruiting Squadron, the Marine Corps Recruiting Station, the
                           Military Entrance Processing Station (MEPS), and the Recruiter-in-
                           Charge of the Coast Guard Recruiting Office or Recruiting
                           Detachment. The IRC serves as the coordinating body through which
                           harmonious relationships between local recruiting organizations and
                           MEPS are maintained. It functions to coordinate discussion and
                           resolve areas of mutual concern pertaining to the MEPS and does not
                           have the authority to make operational decisions for the MEPS.

 RAHS                      The web-based database on secondary school campus access and
                           access to student directory information supporting Section 503 of
                           Title 10, U.S.C., and Section 7908 of Title 20, U.S.C.

 student                   A person 17 years or older, or in the eleventh grade (or its equivalent)
                           or higher, enrolled in a secondary school in the United States, its
                           territories, possessions, or the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, or in
                           DoD high schools worldwide.

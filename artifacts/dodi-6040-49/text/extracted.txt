                             DOD INSTRUCTION 6040.49
                 BIRTH AND DEATH REPORTING IN THE DOD

Originating Component:       Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness

Effective:                   September 24, 2025

Releasability:               Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                             at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Approved by:                 Anthony J. Tata, Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness


Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02 and the guidance in DoD
Instruction (DoDI) 1300.18, this issuance establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes
procedures for reporting the births and deaths of Service members and applicable civilians, including
military dependents, DoD civilian employees, and eligible contractors in the United States and outside
the United States.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

It is DoD policy that appropriate personnel notify designated authorities for the births and deaths
of Service members and applicable civilians, including military dependents, DoD civilian
employees, and eligible contractors in the United States and outside the United States.in
accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR HEALTH AFFAIRS (ASD(HA)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, the ASD(HA) oversees compliance with this issuance.


2.2. DIRECTOR, DEFENSE HEALTH AGENCY (DHA).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, through the ASD(HA), the Director, DHA coordinates with the Secretaries of the
Military Departments to:

   a. Develop and issue policy, if necessary, to implement this issuance.

   b. Require military medical treatment facility (MTF) directors to comply with, oversee
implementation of, and execute this issuance.


2.3. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

In coordination with Director, DHA, the Secretaries of the Military Departments require
commanding officers and personnel under the jurisdiction of the Military Departments concerned
to comply with this issuance and any implementing policies, procedures, and guidance, including
DoDI 1300.18.

                                 SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. OVERVIEW.

   a. Appropriate personnel will comply with DoDI 6040.45 regarding health records and local
laws, regulations, and agreements when registering births and recording and reporting deaths
covered in this issuance.

    b. Appropriate personnel must comply with DoDIs 5400.11, 6025.18, and 8580.02, and
DoD Manual 6025.18 to ensure collected personally identifiable information or protected health
information is:

          (1) Only used for the birth and death reporting procedures in this issuance.

       (2) Shared only with personnel with a need to access the personally identifiable
information or protected health information to complete reporting procedures.


3.2. BIRTH REPORTING.

    a. Birth Reporting in the U.S.

    The patient administration department will register all births occurring at MTFs, on ships and
aircraft, and at other DoD installations or facilities to the proper civil authorities in accordance
with local laws and regulations. MTFs, ships, aircraft, and DoD installations or facilities that do
not have a patient administration department will report all births to the commanding officer.
The commanding officer will also report the birth to the proper civil authorities in accordance
with local laws and regulations.

    b. Birth Reporting Outside the U.S.

    Appropriate personnel will:

       (1) Report all births occurring at MTFs or at other DoD installations or facilities, in
accordance with SOFAs and U.S. State Department requirements.

        (2) When births occur on aircraft or ships operating, report them to the commanding
officer to be recorded in the aircraft or ship log. The commanding officer will also report the
birth to the local authorities in the first port of entry in accordance with the SOFA of that local
authority and State Department requirements.

            (a) If the local registrar of vital statistics or other civil authority does not accept such
a report, the MTF personnel or medical officers will advise the parents to seek the advice of the
nearest Embassy or U.S. Consular’s offices as soon as possible.

             (b) Embassy and U.S. Consular’s offices are in ports of entry and in major U.S.
cities.

       (3) Report births occurring on courses outbound to the U.S. Consular representative at
the next appropriate foreign port. When the aircraft or ship does not enter a foreign port,
procedures described in Paragraph 3.3.b.(2)(a) will be followed.

       (4) Provide the parents with birth registration documentation in accordance with SOFAs
and State Department requirements.


3.3. DEATH REPORTING.

   a. Death Reporting in the U.S.

   Appropriate personnel will:

       (1) Notify the local casualty affairs office in accordance with DoDIs 1300.29 and
1300.18.

       (2) For natural deaths occurring within the jurisdiction of the local MTF, complete and
submit the appropriate local death certificate worksheets to the appropriate civil authorities in
accordance with local laws and regulations.

       (3) For unnatural deaths occurring at MTFs and DoD installations or facilities:

           (a) Notify the military criminal investigative offices and local civil authorities.

           (b) Notify the AFME in accordance with DoDI 5154.30. Information regarding
contacting the Armed Forces Medical Examiner System (AFMES) is located at
https://www.health.mil/Military-Health-Topics/Health-Readiness/AFMES.

            (c) Coordinate with the AFME, military criminal investigative offices, and local civil
authorities as appropriate.

            (d) Provide information to DoD Components who must complete applicable death
reporting, investigation, and recordkeeping requirements specified in Section 1904.39 of
Title 29, Code of Federal Regulations, relating to reporting employee deaths to the Occupational
Safety and Health Administration and DoDI 6055.07 relating to mishap notification.

   b. Death Reporting Outside the U.S.

    In accordance with SOFAs, security cooperation agreements in accordance with DoD
Directive 5132.03, host nation laws, and State Department requirements, appropriate personnel
will:

       (1) Notify the local casualty affairs office in accordance with DoDIs 1300.29 and
1300.18.

       (2) For natural deaths:

           (a) Report deaths to the appropriate local authorities. Local laws and regulations
may require a completed DD Form 2064, “Certificate of Death Overseas,” this form can be
obtained by request from the AFMES org box: usarmy.dover.medcom-
afmes.mbx.information@health.mil.

            (b) When deaths occur on aircraft or ships operating outside the U.S., report the
death to the commanding officer to be recorded in the aircraft or ship log.

                1. The commanding officer will report the death to the AFME and local
authorities in the first port of entry in accordance with the SOFA of that local authority and State
Department requirements.

              2. Information regarding contacting the AFMES is located at
https://www.health.mil/Military-Health-Topics/Health-Readiness/AFMES.

       (3) For unnatural deaths:

          (a) Notify the military criminal investigative offices, local civil authorities, and the
AFME. Information regarding contacting the AFMES is located at
https://www.health.mil/Military-Health-Topics/Health-Readiness/AFMES.

            (b) When deaths occur on aircraft or ships operating outside the U.S., report the
death to the commanding officer to be recorded in the aircraft or ship log. The commanding
officer will report the death to the AFME and local authorities in the first port of entry in
accordance with the SOFA of that local authority and State Department requirements.
Additionally, commanding officers must complete applicable death reporting, investigation, and
recordkeeping requirements specified in Section 1904.39 of Title 29, Code of Federal
Regulations, relating to reporting employee deaths to the Occupational Safety and Health
Administration and DoDI 6055.07, relating to mishap notification.

           (c) Coordinate with the military criminal investigative offices, local civil authorities,
and the AFME for notification requirements for deaths of Service members and their dependents
occurring outside of MTFs or other DoD facilities, ships, or aircraft, as appropriate.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 civilian                  A person who is not a Service member, including a member of the
                           general public (U.S. citizen and non-U.S. citizen), a defense
                           contractor, a DoD civilian employee, a non-DoD Federal employee,
                           or a military dependent.

 medical officer           MTF director or health care provider designated to perform birth and
                           death reporting procedures.

 military dependent        A member of a Service member’s family considered to be a
                           dependent as defined by Section 401 of Title 37, United States Code.

 MTF                       A fixed MTF established to furnish medical or dental care to eligible
                           individuals, including all operations of each such facility and all
                           health care delivery associated with each such facility.

TERM               DEFINITION

MTF director       The person who is responsible for:

                      Ensuring the readiness of the Service members at an MTF on
                   behalf of the Military Departments.

                     Furnishing the health care and medical treatment provided at an
                   MTF on behalf of the DHA.

MTF personnel      Employees, volunteers, trainees, and other personnel whose conduct,
                   in the performance of work for a DoD covered entity or business
                   associate, is under the direct control of the DoD covered entity or
                   business associate whether they are paid by the DoD covered entity
                   or business associate.

personally         Any representation or information that permits the identity of an
identifiable       individual to whom the information applies to be reasonably inferred
information        by either direct or indirect means.

protected health   Individually identifiable health information (as defined in the Health
information        Insurance Portability and Accountability Act Privacy Rule) that,
                   except as provided in this issuance, is transmitted or maintained by
                   electronic or any other form or medium. Protected health
                   information excludes individually identifiable health information in
                   employment records held by a DoD covered entity in its role as
                   employer. Information that has been de-identified in accordance with
                   the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act Privacy Rule
                   is not protected health information.

SOFA               A bilateral or multilateral agreement that defines the legal position of
                   a visiting military force deployed in the territory of a friendly state.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

It is DoD policy that:

   a. EUCs be executed and required when:

       (1) Such certificates facilitate DoD purchases of foreign products or services.

       (2) It is in the best interest of the United States to purchase such products or services.

    b. Consistent with U.S. laws, regulations, and acquisition policy, DoD Components purchase
foreign products to obtain products and services that best meet U.S. requirements.

       (1) Acquisition of foreign products and services that meet DoD requirements promote
interoperability, standardization, and an expanded procurement base.

        (2) U.S. worldwide security responsibilities are extensive; recognition of these
circumstances requires flexibility in U.S. international commitments concerning the authorized
uses or retransfer of products and services acquired from foreign nations. Use for defense
purposes (See Glossary):

           (a) Reflects the necessary flexibility in EUCs to be signed with foreign governments.

           (b) Establishes the baseline position for routine EUCs.

                          SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1 UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION AND SUSTAINMENT
(USD(A&S)).

The USD(A&S):

   a. Monitors compliance with this issuance.

    b. In coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense for Policy (USD(P)) and the Under
Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (USD(I&S)), develops procedures to ensure
the timely review of EUCs.

   c. After receiving USD(P)’s concurrence, authorizes the signature of the EUC, in accordance
with Section 3, when purchase of a foreign product is in the best interest of the United States.

   d. When requested, and in coordination with the USD(P), assists the DoD Components with
negotiating the elimination or alleviation of an EUC’s restrictive language.


2.2. USD(P).

The USD(P):

    a. Consults with the USD(A&S) and, as appropriate, coordinates with the Department of
State on EUC requests authorized by this issuance.

   b. When requested, and in coordination with the USD(A&S), assists the DoD Components
with negotiating the elimination or alleviation of an EUC’s restrictive language.


2.3. USD(I&S).

   The USD(I&S):

   a. Oversees EUCs under their purview to ensure compliance with current policies and
procedures.

  b. In coordination with the USD(A&S), establishes DoD coordination procedures for all
EUCs within USD(I&S)’s purview.


2.4. DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The DoD Component heads:

   a. Approve signature of EUCs as described in Section 3.

            SECTION 3: EUC EXECUTION REVIEW AND APPROVAL

3.1. GENERAL.

EUC approval falls into two tiers: Tier A and Tier B. All EUCs within USD(I&S)’s purview
must be coordinated between the USD(I&S) and the USD(A&S) in accordance with
Paragraph 2.3.b.


3.2. TIER A.

   a. The DoD Component heads will submit EUCs to the USD(A&S) for review, when one or
more of the following conditions apply:

          (1) Integration of the foreign product into higher level equipment is planned.

          (2) Use of the foreign product is restricted beyond use for defense purposes.

       (3) No reciprocal defense procurement memorandum of understanding (MOU) or
agreement exists. (See the list of reciprocal defense procurement MOU or agreement
signatories, also known as “qualifying countries,” as defined in Subpart 225.0 of the Defense
Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement.)

          (4) No general security agreement or general security of military information agreement
exists.

    b. If there are issues with the EUC review request, the USD(A&S) will engage with the DoD
Component within 21 working days to discuss and seek resolution of issues. As required, the
DoD Component may request expedited review for activities that are time-critical. Once the
USD(A&S) approves the Tier A EUC request, the DoD Component heads may sign the Tier A
EUC. In any event, DoD Component heads must provide a copy of all approved and signed
Tier A EUCs to the USD(A&S) 21 working days after signature.


3.3. TIER B.

  a. The DoD Component heads may authorize EUCs without the requirement for review by
USD(A&S), noted in Paragraph 2.3., when all of the following conditions apply:

        (1) No integration of the foreign product into higher level equipment is planned and the
use of the foreign product is not restricted beyond use for defense purposes.

       (2) A reciprocal defense procurement MOU or agreement exists. (See the list of
reciprocal defense procurement MOU or agreement signatories, also known as “qualifying
countries,” as defined in Subpart 225.0 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation
Supplement.)

      (3) There is a general security agreement or general security of military information
agreement.

   b. The DoD Component heads will notify the USD(A&S) of all Tier B EUCs within
21 working days of the properly authorized signature of the EUC.


3.4. REQUESTS FOR REVIEW AND APPROVAL.

Requests for review and approval should specify:

   a. Why it is in the interest of the U.S. Government to procure the item.

   b. The limitations to be imposed by the exporting government and a justification for the U.S.
Government to accept those limitations.

    c. That no satisfactory alternative to the foreign product (considering cost, schedule, or
operational requirements) is available from domestic or foreign sources without equivalent
limitations.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 EUC                       A certification required for exporting foreign products by a foreign
                           government that identifies the end user of the foreign products. The
                           certification may restrict the use or transfer of the foreign product and
                           is signed by an authorized representative of the U.S. Government.

 qualifying country        Defined in the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement,
                           Subpart 225.0.

 use for defense           Includes direct use by or for the U.S. Government in any part of the
 purposes                  world and transfer by means of grant aid, international military
                           education and training programs, foreign military sales, and other
                           security assistance and armaments cooperation authorities.

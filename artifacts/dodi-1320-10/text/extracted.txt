SUBJECT:      Discharge of Commissioned Officers Not Qualified for Promotion to the Grade of
              First Lieutenant or Lieutenant (Junior Grade)

References: See Enclosure 1


1. PURPOSE. In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02 (Reference (a)), this
instruction reissues DoD Instruction (DoDI) 1320.10 (Reference (b)) to establish policy, assign
responsibilities, and prescribe procedures to discharge commissioned officers found not qualified
for promotion to the grade of first lieutenant or lieutenant (junior grade).


2. APPLICABILITY. This instruction applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of
the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the
Office of the Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD
Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD.


3. POLICY. It is DoD policy to discharge officers who are not qualified to be promoted to the
grade of first lieutenant or lieutenant (junior grade). However, it is recognized that such officers
are new to commissioned military service and should be afforded a reasonable opportunity to
overcome their deficiencies before discharge action is taken.


4. RESPONSIBILITIES. See Enclosure 2.


5. PROCEDURES. Enclosure 3 provides procedures for the discharge of commissioned officers
found not qualified for promotion to the grade of first lieutenant or lieutenant (junior grade).


6. RELEASABILITY. Cleared for Public release. This instruction is available on the
Directives Division Website at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

                                      RESPONSIBILITIES


1. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR READINESS AND FORCE
MANAGEMENT (ASD(R&FM)). Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under
Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness (USD(P&R)), the ASD(R&FM) develops and
oversees the policies governing the discharge of commissioned officers found unqualified for
promotion to the grade of first lieutenant or lieutenant (junior grade).


2. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS. The Secretaries of the Military
Departments:

   a. Implement this instruction.

   b. Prescribe regulations consistent with the policy in this instruction.

   c. Recommend policy changes to this instruction, as necessary, to the USD(P&R).

                                          ENCLOSURE 3

                                          PROCEDURES


1. RETENTION. The continued military service of officers found unqualified to be promoted to
the grade of first lieutenant or lieutenant (junior grade) is inconsistent with the mission
requirements of the Military Services and the productivity and efficiency of the officer force.


2. MINIMUM RETENTION PERIOD. The Secretary of the Military Department concerned
will retain an officer, whether on the active-duty list or reserve active-status list, who is found
unqualified for promotion to the grade of first lieutenant or lieutenant (junior grade) for a
minimum of 6 months after the date on which the promotion would have occurred, unless
retention is inconsistent with good order and discipline.

    a. If the officer qualifies for promotion during or at the completion of the retention period,
he or she will be promoted, in accordance with the regulations and procedures prescribed by the
Secretary of the Military Department concerned.

    b. If the officer does not qualify for promotion at any time after the completion of the 6-
month retention period, he or she may be discharged under the regulations and procedures
prescribed by the Secretary of the Military Department concerned.


3. FURTHER RETENTION OF DISQUALIFIED OFFICERS. An officer who is found
unqualified for promotion to the grade of first lieutenant or lieutenant (junior grade) at the end of
the 6-month period described in section 2 of this enclosure may be retained on active duty or in
an active status by the Secretary of the Military Department in accordance with appropriate
Service regulations and procedures. However, such an officer will be discharged at the end of
the 18-month period beginning on the date on which the officer was first found not qualified for
promotion, unless that officer has been promoted in the meantime.


4. SEPARATION PAY. Separation pay policy and procedures are included in DoDI 1332.29
(Reference (c)).

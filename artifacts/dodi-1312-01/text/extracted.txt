SUBJECT:     Department of Defense Occupational Information Collection and Reporting

References: See Enclosure 1


1. PURPOSE. In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive (DoDD) 5124.02 (Reference
(a)), this Instruction:

   a. Reissues DoD Instruction (DoDI) 1312.1 (Reference (b)) which establishes and
implements policy, defines responsibilities, and provides procedures for the collection and
maintenance of military and civilian occupational information to meet DoD requirements.

   b. Directs continued maintenance of a DoD-level master Occupational Database (ODB) to
provide standardized information on Service occupational classification codes, a DoD
occupation coding structure that groups similar Service occupations (DoD Occupational
Conversion Index File) and crosswalks to Federal Government and private sector civilian
occupational classifications.

  c. Establishes and implements the DoD Military Occupational Classification Joint Service
Working Group in accordance with DoD Instruction 5105.18 (Reference (c)).


2. APPLICABILITY. This Instruction applies to OSD, the Military Departments (including the
Coast Guard at all times, including when it is a service in the Department of Homeland Security
by agreement with that Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and
the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department of
Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities
within the DoD (hereinafter referred to collectively as the “DoD Components”).


3. POLICY. It is DoD policy that:

   a. USD(P&R) shall monitor the Military Services’ occupational classification systems and
provide assistance and guidance, where appropriate.
                                                             DoDI 1312.01, January 28, 2013


    b. The information collected, stored, and maintained in the DoD ODB shall comply with the
standards prescribed in DoDI 8320.02, DoDI 8320.07, DoDI 8910.01, DoD Manual 8910.01
(References (d), (e), (f), and (g)), and this Instruction.


4. RESPONSIBILITIES. See Enclosure 2.


5. PROCEDURES. See Enclosure 3.


6. INFORMATION COLLECTION REQUIREMENTS. The “DoD Occupational Conversion
Index File” referred to in this issuance has been assigned report control symbol DD-
P&R(Q,SA)959 in accordance with the procedures in Reference (g).


7. RELEASABILITY. Cleared for public release. This Instruction is available on the
Directives Division Website at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.


8. SUMMARY OF CHANGE 2. The changes to this issuance are administrative and update
language to comply with Executive Order 14168 (Reference (h)) and correct references.


9. EFFECTIVE DATE. This Instruction is effective January 28, 2013.

                                        ENCLOSURE 2

                                      RESPONSIBILITIES


1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS
(USD(P&R)). The USD(P&R) shall:

    a. Provide overall policy and guidance for the DoD collection and reporting of occupational
information.

   b. Monitor and evaluate the content, consistency, completeness, and currency of military
occupational data.

   c. Provide a representative to convene and chair the DoD Military Occupational
Classification Joint Service Working Group.


2. DIRECTOR, DEFENSE HUMAN RESOURCES ACTIVITY (DHRA). The Director,
DHRA, under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R), shall ensure the Director,
DMDC:

    a. Acts as the DoD point of contact for military and Federal civilian occupational
information and crosswalk of military to civilian occupations.

    b. Develops and maintains the DoD ODB, and serves as requester, custodian, and receiver of
all Military Service and civilian occupational information updates.

    c. Provides quality control (in coordination with the Military Services and other Federal
Government Agencies), report generation, inquiry capability, and administrative support to users
of occupational information.

    d. Appoints an occupational information specialist to the DoD Military Occupational
Classification Joint Service Working Group and take final action on the recommendations and
findings of the Group.


3. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND THE COMMANDANT OF
THE UNITED STATES COAST GUARD. The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the
Commandant of the United States Coast Guard shall:

   a. Develop and implement occupational classification identifiers and specifications
according to their internal Service policies and this Instruction.

    b. Establish procedures to provide occupational classification updates to DMDC for
inclusion with the DoD ODB, as indicated in this Instruction. All updates and changes to

Enclosure 4 of this Instruction must be provided to DMDC within 10 business days of the

Working Group. This representative will review Service occupational changes to determine their
impact on Reference (j) and provide technical advice to the Director, DMDC, on updates of the
related ODB data tables.

                                        ENCLOSURE 3

                                        PROCEDURES


1. MILITARY SERVICES. The Military Services will furnish electronic versions of their
occupation classification publications representing all occupation and skill code taxonomies with
associated descriptive information as defined in Table 1 of Enclosure 4 of this Instruction as
changes are implemented, normally on a quarterly basis. Acceptable formats are Microsoft
Access, Excel, and Word, or Adobe Acrobat. The Services must provide all initial publications
and subsequent changes within 10 business days of the effective date of the publication or
change to the DMDC ODB file manager.


2. DANTES. DANTES shall facilitate DMDC access to daily electronic updates of Reference
(j) (http://www.militaryguides.acenet.edu/).


3. DMDC. The DMDC shall:

    a. Enter new and revised Service and DoD occupational identifier information in the ODB
within 20 business days of the effective date of the publication or changes. Descriptions of each
Service ODB table, to include data elements and sources of information, are provided in Table 1
of Enclosure 4 of this Instruction.

    b. Conduct quality review of ODB data, to include verification of occupation conversion
relationships as presented in Table 2 of Enclosure 5 of this Instruction.

    c. Obtain comparable civilian occupation information from the Office of Personnel
Management (OPM), the Office of Management and Budget, the Bureau of Labor Statistics
(BLS), and the Department of Labor (DOL). This occupational information is available on the
respective agency government web site or classification office.

   d. Complete the crosswalk analysis of military occupations to the various civilian
occupational classifications (e.g., OPM, Standard Occupation Classification (SOC), and the
Occupational Network (O*NET)) and enter associated civilian codes in the ODB Master
Crosswalks File. All ODB files and tables are maintained by the ODB file manager at DMDC.

   e. Use the ODB in the development of the verification of military experience and training
documents under section 1143(a) of title 10, United States Code (Reference (k)) by matching
occupational codes in the ODB to those maintained by the DMDC in the military personnel files
submitted by the Services in accordance with DoDI 7730.68 (Reference (l)).

    f. Provide the DoD Military Occupational Classification Joint Service Working Group with
the ODB occupational crosswalks to validate the various linkages established between Service
occupation codes, DoD occupation codes, and the various civil sector occupational coding

    i. Produce and distribute updated military and civilian occupational information in

Purpose: This issuance:
• Establishes the structure, policy, and administrative guidance for the DoD senior governance
framework pursuant to the authority vested in the Secretary of Defense (SecDef) by Section 113 of
Title 10, United States Code.
• Outlines the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff’s (CJCS) contribution of providing military advice
to the SecDef through participation in DoD senior governance forums.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the CJCS and the Joint
Staff, the Combatant Commands (CCMDs), the Office of Inspector General of the Department of
Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities
within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

    a. Senior DoD civilian and military officials will conduct governance of the DoD in a
transparent, collaborative, and aligned framework of forums to promote unity of effort; integrate
civilian and military perspectives; efficiently and effectively develop, coordinate, and implement
DoD policies and programs; readily share information; and provide decision support to the
SecDef and the Deputy Secretary of Defense (DepSecDef) on cross-cutting priorities.

    b. Senior governance forums are chaired by the SecDef or DepSecDef, with assistance from
the CJCS or Vice CJCS (VCJCS), and deliberate on issues and recommendations for SecDef and
DepSecDef consideration.

   c. A supporting tier governance forum, generally chaired by an OSD Principal Staff
Assistant (PSA), will vet issues before advancing to a senior governance forum for
consideration, as necessary. See Appendix 3A for a list of supporting tier governance forums
and their chairs.

    d. Senior and supporting tier governance forum meeting participants express views openly in
an environment of non-attribution.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. DIRECTOR OF ADMINISTRATION AND MANAGEMENT (DA&M).

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the DA&M, through the Director,
Organizational Policy and Decision Support (OP&DS):

   a. Directs the OP&DS Engine Room to provide administrative and meeting support to the
SecDef and DepSecDef for senior governance forum execution, as required.

   b. Serves as the primary staffing element of DepSecDef-led governance processes.

   c. Coordinates with and supports each senior governance forum’s executive secretariat with
meeting execution, attendance management, and follow-up.

  d. Supports executive secretariats with agenda development to address DoD priorities across
OSD senior and supporting tier governance forums.

    e. Tracks and shares information on supporting tier governance forum establishment and
activities.

    f. Develops and hosts forum and governance-related websites to assist with senior
governance forum management, integration across governance activities, and storage and
distribution of governance-related content.

    g. Coordinates with the Chief Information Officer of the Department of Defense (DoD CIO);
the Deputy Comptroller for Enterprise Data and Business Performance; and the OSD and DoD
Component heads to provide analytics support within the senior governance framework, as
appropriate.

    h. Provides supporting tier executive secretariats with advice and guidance on best practices,
recommended management processes, and information sharing systems for standing up and
running governance forums.


2.2. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE (COMPTROLLER)/CHIEF FINANCIAL
OFFICER, DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE (USD(C)/CFO).

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the USD(C)/CFO:

    a. Through the Deputy Comptroller for Enterprise Data and Business Performance, provides
data analytics, as appropriate.

    b. Maintains the Advancing Analytics (Advana) platform repository for common enterprise
data and cross-functional and enterprise-wide analytics.

2.3. DOD CIO.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the DoD CIO, through the DoD Chief Data
Officer, coordinates with the Deputy Comptroller for Enterprise Data and Business Performance;
OSD and DoD Component heads; and the Director, OP&DS to generate data analytics to support
senior governance forums.


2.4. OSD AND DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

When identified as primary lead for a senior governance forum topic presentation, OSD and
DoD Component heads:

    a. Use rigorous analysis to develop courses of action and recommendations on specific
issues.

   b. Provide a forum’s executive secretariat with timely documentation, including read-ahead
material and meeting notes, in accordance with each governance forum’s established procedures
and requirements.

    c. Coordinate with the DoD CIO; the Deputy Comptroller for Enterprise Data and Business
Performance; and the Director, OP&DS for generating and presenting data analytics in support
of senior governance forum briefings, as applicable.


2.5. CJCS.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the CJCS:

   a. Co-chairs select DoD senior governance forums with the SecDef, as designated by the
SecDef.

   b. Coordinates with the SecDef on meeting agenda development and agenda item proposals.

  c. As chair of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Meeting, or “Tank,” can represent Service and
CCMD-related perspectives and recommendations in SecDef governance forums.


2.6. VCJCS.

The VCJCS:

   a. Co-chairs select DoD senior governance forums with the DepSecDef, as designated by the
DepSecDef.

   b. Coordinates with the DepSecDef on meeting agenda development and agenda item
proposals.

    c. As chair of the Joint Requirements Oversight Council, can represent Service and CCMD-
related perspectives and recommendations in DepSecDef governance forums.

     SECTION 3: SENIOR GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK STRUCTURE

3.1. SENIOR GOVERNANCE FORUMS.

    a. Senior governance forums meet at regular intervals; rely on their executive secretariats to
promote effective and collaborative deliberations; and leverage a combination of subject matter
expertise, data-based analytics, intelligence, and OSD PSA-generated reporting to support
effective decision-making, organizational alignment, and implementation of SecDef and
DepSecDef direction. Effective forums rely on disciplined processes, consistency, transparency,
accuracy, and timeliness.

   b. SecDef-led forums include:

       (1) The Senior Leadership Council (SLC).

        Chaired by the SecDef and co-chaired by the CJCS, the SLC meets twice a year to
address broad, cross-cutting issues affecting OSD, the Military Departments, the CCMDs, and
other Federal agencies, as applicable. The SLC provides advice and assistance on strategic
issues to the SecDef.

       (2) The SecDef’s Large Group.

      Chaired by the SecDef, the Large Group meets at the discretion of the SecDef to address
DoD issues and high-level priorities. The Large Group provides advice and assistance to the
SecDef on the strategic direction and ongoing operations of the Department.

       (3) SecDef China Brief.

        The China Brief is the SecDef’s primary forum to review and oversee China-related
policies, operations, and intelligence.

   c. DepSecDef-led forums include:

       (1) The Deputy’s Management Action Group.

       Chaired by the DepSecDef and co-chaired by the VCJCS, it is the Department’s principal
governance forum for management actions affecting the defense enterprise, including resource
management; planning, programming, budgeting, and execution; and strategic and policy
guidance.

       (2) The Deputy’s Workforce Council.

       Chaired by the DepSecDef and co-chaired by the VCJCS, it is the Department’s principal
governance forum for people management, personnel policy, and total force requirements.

    d. At the discretion of the Chair, attendees and advisors include the OSD PSAs, Secretaries
of the Military Departments, Chiefs of the Military Services, Chief of the National Guard

   e. Senior and supporting tier governance forums have a clear guidance document, issued by

   f. Forums use the Advana platform as the enterprise authoritative data management and

   g. The SecDef and DepSecDef establish and disestablish senior governance forums as
needed.


3.2. SENIOR GOVERNANCE FORUM EXECUTIVE SECRETARIATS.

   a. SecDef- and DepSecDef-chaired forums are managed by executive secretariats
comprising staff from within the immediate office of the SecDef and DepSecDef. These
executive secretariats support the SecDef and DepSecDef with agenda development, scheduling,
and oversight of senior governance forums, and manage the processes needed to plan and
execute decision-quality SecDef- and DepSecDef-chaired meetings and ensure alignment
between cross-cutting priorities.

   b. The executive secretariat of a senior governance forum:

       (1) Vets strategically aligned topics and agendas; schedules, coordinates, and executes
meetings; and manages briefing preparations and resultant decisions and tasks.

       (2) Organizes and schedules SecDef and DepSecDef preparations for senior governance
forums.

      (3) Tracks task completion, and promotes and maintains a disciplined content
management process.

       (4) Directs supporting tier forum review of all topics going to senior governance forums.

       (5) Promotes an active reclama process to consider differing positions after meetings.

        (6) Promotes and guides the application of data analytics derived from common,
authoritative data sources in support of senior governance forum processes and briefings.

       (7) Guides OP&DS Engine Room support as provided by the DA&M.


3.3. SUPPORTING TIER FORUMS.

    a. The SecDef, DepSecDef, OSD and DoD Component heads, and other designated senior
leaders will establish and disestablish supporting tier governance forums, as needed.
Appendix 3A outlines the current supporting tier governance forum structure.

    b. OSD PSAs will minimize the number of active supporting tier forums, and align forums
to specific SecDef and DepSecDef priorities, where practical.

    c. Supporting tier governance forums can be established in DoD issuances or memorandums,
as well as directed in public law.

   d. Supporting tier governance forums are generally chaired by OSD PSAs. Attendees and
advisors generally include senior leaders from the OSD PSAs, Secretaries of the Military
Departments, Chiefs of the Military Services, Chief of the National Guard Bureau, Combatant
Commanders, and the DJS.

   e. Supporting tier governance forums operate in a transparent, collaborative, and analytical
manner, with maximized information sharing between civilian and military stakeholders, to
maintain transparency and alignment of priorities.

   f. Forums use the Advana platform as the enterprise authoritative data management and
analytics platform, as appropriate, for the SecDef, DepSecDef, and OSD PSAs.

    g. OSD PSA-chaired governance forums that do not advance issues and recommendations
directly to the SecDef or the DepSecDef, or do not directly support a senior governance forum,
are not included in the supporting tier governance structure pursuant to this issuance.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 executive secretariat     The primary staffing element for a governance forum and supports
                           the executive secretary on all meeting management, execution, and
                           follow up. The executive secretariat, as directly supported by the
                           OP&DS Engine Room, performs meeting room access control,
                           attendance tracking, and long-term schedule distribution.

TERM                  DEFINITION

executive secretary   The executive secretary of a governance forum is typically a SecDef
                      or DepSecDef Special Assistant, and is responsible for setting the
                      forum’s agenda and overseeing staffing, analytic, and meeting
                      execution support, as carried out by the executive secretariat.

governance            The structure, relationships, and interactions, among authorized and
                      responsible stakeholders, that facilitate decision-making on an
                      organization’s policies, direction, and performance.


governance forum      An advisory and deliberative body that is formally constituted and
                      meets regularly to support its chairs in formulating policy and
                      directing the affairs of an institution.


OP&DS Engine          Part of the primary staffing element of the executive secretariat, and
Room                  provides administrative and meeting support to the SecDef and
                      DepSecDef for senior governance forum management.

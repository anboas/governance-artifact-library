                              DOD DIRECTIVE 8470.01E
    DOD EXECUTIVE AGENT (DOD EA) FOR COMMERCIAL
   SOFTWARE PRODUCT MANAGEMENT OF CORE ENTERPRISE
          TECHNOLOGY AGREEMENTS (CETAS)

Originating Component:        Office of the Chief Management Officer of the Department of Defense

Effective:                    September 6, 2018

Releasability:                Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                              at http://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Approved by:                  Patrick M. Shanahan, Deputy Secretary of Defense


Purpose:     This issuance:
• Pursuant to Section 113 of Title 10, United States Code, establishes policy and assigns
responsibilities for the commercial software product management of CETAs designated by the Defense
Business Council (DBC) in support of information technology (IT) category management (ITCM) and
the DoD Enterprise Software Initiative.
• Designates the Secretary of the Navy as the DoD EA, with assigned specific responsibilities,
functions, and authorities for Commercial Software Product Management of CETAs in accordance with
DoD Directive 5101.1.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

    a. This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the
Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

   b. This issuance does not apply to National Intelligence Program-funded software Enterprise
Agreements.


1.2. POLICY. It is DoD policy that:

    a. The Secretary of the Navy is designated as the DoD Executive Agent for commercial
software product management of Core Enterprise Technology Agreements (CETAs).

   b. CETAs are enterprise purchasing agreements for Commercial-Off-The-Shelf Information
Technology (IT) products or capabilities identified by the DBC for DoD EA implementation and
execution, and are being established to focus resources on efficient and effective supplier
management.

   c. CETAs will be used to acquire and manage identified commercial software products
based on enterprise use and spending or products competitively selected that are aligned with the
Department’s current or planned strategic direction.

    d. The CETA contracts will be established as DoD Enterprise Software Agreements as
defined by Subpart 208.74 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement, and will
provide best in class purchasing solutions for IT Category Management (ITCM) for commercial
software licenses in accordance with Office of Management and Budget (OMB) IT Category
Management guidance in OMB M-16-12. The ITCM approach will enable the Department to
eliminate redundancies, increase efficiency, and deliver greater value and savings to the
Department. CETA contracts will be established to provide the best value to the government and
are mandatory for use when a procurement requirement is within the scope of the CETA
contract.

                          SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. DOD CHIEF INFORMATION OFFICER. The DoD Chief Information Officer:

    a. Serves as the OSD Principal Staff Assistant who oversees the DoD EA for Commercial
Software Product Management of CETAs; and periodically assesses the DoD EA for
Commercial Software Product Management of CETAs for continued need, currency,
effectiveness, and oversight of Enterprise Software Initiatives.

   b. In consultation with the Chief Management Officer of the Department of Defense and the
Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment, establishes policy and provides
guidance and oversight to ensure the timely and successful implementation of ITCM for CETAs.

   c. Identifies enterprise-wide products or requirements for CETA consideration to the co-
chairs of the DBC, as needed.

   d. Ensures the effective and efficient use of CETAs.

    e. Addresses issues and elevates unresolved matters to the Defense Business Council (DBC)
as needed. The DBC serves as a principal supporting tier of governance to the Deputy Secretary
of Defense for vetting defense management and business improvement issues.

   f. Prescribes policy and guidance and provides oversight of enterprise ITCM functions.

   g. Ensures optimization and standardization of the life-cycle management of CETAs,
consistent with DoD Instruction 8440.01.


2.2. DOD COMPONENT HEADS. The DoD Component heads:

   a. Establish ITCM processes required to implement CETAs.

  b. Identify and submit validated IT requirements for CETA acquisitions to the DoD EA for
Commercial Software Product Management of CETAs.

       (1) Combatant Commanders and the Commander, United States Element, the North
American Aerospace Defense Command. The Combatant Commanders and the Commander,
United States Element, the North American Aerospace Defense Command, will submit relevant
IT requirements to their designated Combatant Command support agent in accordance with DoD
Directive 5100.03.

        (2) All Other DoD Component Heads. All other DoD Component heads will submit
validated IT requirements for CETA acquisitions to the DoD EA as follows:

          (a) Combatant Command Support Agency Requirements. Submit the relevant IT
requirements for CETA acquisitions for the Combatant Command(s) assigned in DoD Directive
5100.03.

          (b) Pentagon IT Services (ITS) Requirements. Submit relevant component IT
requirements for Pentagon ITS through the Single Agency Manager (SAM) for Pentagon ITS in
accordance with DoD Directive 8220.1.

            (c) SAM for Pentagon ITS. Submit the relevant requirements for CETA acquisitions
for other components managed by the SAM for Pentagon ITS in accordance with DoD Directive
8220.01.

          (d) All Other CETA Requirements. Submit all other relevant component IT
requirements for CETA acquisitions to the DoD EA.

   c. Fund their portion of requirements procured using CETAs and comply with applicable
laws, regulations, and policy. The DoD EA for Commercial Software Product Management of
CETAs’ use of Defense Working Capital Funds to purchase DoD inventory does not relieve the
DoD Components of this responsibility.

   d. Ensure that financial management and funding policies and procedures support efficient
and effective use of CETAs, including use and reimbursement of Defense Working Capital
Funds when applicable.

   e. Share best practices with the DoD EA.


2.3. SECRETARY OF THE NAVY. In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.2., the
Secretary of the Navy, as the DoD EA for Commercial Software Product Management of
CETAs:

   a. Prescribes implementation guidance for the execution of this issuance.

    b. Validates, in writing, when DoD Component IT requirements associated with Paragraph
2.2.b. are not within the scope of a CETA.

   c. Assigns responsibilities for commercial software product management of CETAs in
accordance with the July 26, 2000 Assistant Secretary of Defense for Command, Control,
Communications, and Intelligence memorandum.

   d. Conducts market analysis in support of business case analysis.

    e. Analyzes alternatives for contracting options, including federal vehicles open to the DoD
for ordering.

   f. Establishes CETAs that may, where appropriate and efficient, be open for ordering by
non-DoD agencies and provide assistance to federal civilian agency contracting activities for any
contract or agreement that is designated as either a government-wide acquisition contract or a
multi-agency contract as a federal category management leadership initiative.

   g. Executes CETA procurements.

G.2. DEFINITIONS. Unless otherwise noted, terms and their definitions are for the purpose of
this issuance.

category management. An approach the government applies to buy smarter and more like a
single enterprise.

    Enables the government to eliminate redundancies, increase efficiency, and deliver more
value and savings from the government’s acquisition programs.

   Involves identifying core areas of spending; collectively developing heightened levels of
expertise; leveraging shared best practices; and providing acquisition, supply, and demand
management solutions.

CETA. Enterprise purchasing agreements for the Department’s identified commercial software
products based on annual spending, or commercial software products aligning with Department’s
current or planned strategic direction.

commercial item. Defined in the Federal Acquisition Regulation, Subpart 2.1.

commercial off-the-shelf. Refer to the Federal Acquisition Regulation, Subpart 2.1 definition
for "commercially available off-the-shelf (COTS) item."

commercial software. Defined in the Federal Acquisition Regulation Subpart 2.1.

DoD EA. Defined in DoD Directive 5101.1.

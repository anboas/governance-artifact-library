SUBJECT:     Reserve Component Representation, North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO)
             National Reserve Forces Committee (NRFC) and NATO Schools

References: See Enclosure 1


1. PURPOSE. In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02 (Reference (a)), this
instruction:

    a. Establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and provides procedures regarding Reserve
Components (RCs) of the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs
of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff representation at the NATO NRFC, a NATO Subcommittee
of the Military Committee, and NATO schools in accordance with DoD Directive 5105.20
(Reference (b)).

   b. Cancels Office of the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Reserve Affairs Memorandum
(Reference (c)).


2. APPLICABILITY. This instruction applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of
the CJCS and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD.


3. POLICY. It is DoD policy:

   a. That the DoD provides guidance within respective fields of responsibility to NATO in
accordance with Reference (b).

   b. To provide a U.S. National Delegation to the NRFC.

    c. To advise the NRFC on Reserve issues, and enhance the utility and interests of U.S. RCs
as a vital component of the combined Armed Forces of the Alliance Nations, known in this
issuance as “the Alliance,” in accordance with Reference (b).

    d. To provide guidance to the NRFC to strengthen the readiness and effectiveness of the
Alliance Reserves to support NATO across the full spectrum of operations and ensure policies
encourage optimum integration of RC personnel to provide the most operational benefits to
mission capability.

   e. To provide technical advice, direction, and policy guidance in relation to the integration of
RC forces to the Alliance, in coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense for Policy.

    f. That training opportunities are available to all personnel in DoD RC NATO schools,
including the shared use of resources, operational benefits, and mission capabilities in
accordance with DoD Directive 1200.17 (Reference (d)).

    g. That RC Service members enroll in appropriate NATO courses supporting individual or
unit readiness for mobilization in support of current or future missions.


4. RESPONSIBILITIES. See Enclosure 2.


5. PROCEDURES. See Enclosure 3.


6. RELEASABILITY. Cleared for public release. This instruction available on the Internet
from the DoD Issuances Website at http://www.dtic.mil/whs/directives.


7. EFFECTIVE DATE. This instruction is effective November 12, 2015.

                                        ENCLOSURE 2

                                      RESPONSIBILITIES


1. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS. Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for
Personnel and Readiness, the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve
Affairs:

   a. Maintains awareness of RC issues relating to NATO.

    b. In coordination with the CJCS representative to the NRFC, develops policies that promote
the use of Total Force capabilities.

   c. Coordinates with the CJCS representative to the NRFC and the Military Departments
concerning NRFC RC topics.

   d. Ensures actions and responsibilities relating to the NRFC are implemented in agreement
with applicable international treaties.


2. CJCS. The CJCS:

    a. Designates a U.S. Head of Delegation (HoD) and Staff Officer (SO) to the NRFC
congresses (i.e., informal meetings) to represent the RCs and coordinate and oversee NRFC
activities for the DoD.

   b. Coordinates and oversees RC requirements, authorizations, reservations, commitment of
funds and attendance to NATO school courses.


3. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS. The Secretaries of the Military
Departments:

   a. Provide NATO school attendance requirements to the CJCS.

    b. Project the number of required school seats for the following fiscal year, program training
requirements, and coordinate with the CJCS point of contact for NATO schools for students to
attend NATO courses.

   c. Ensure funding requirements within each Military Service to attend appropriate NATO
courses.

    d. Coordinate RC Service member enrollment in appropriate NATO courses supporting
individual or unit readiness for mobilization in support of current or future missions.

                                          ENCLOSURE 3

                                          PROCEDURES


1. HoD

   a. Requirements. In accordance with NATO Military Committee Directive 0392/1
(Reference (f)), the HoD must be:

        (1) A senior official, such as a general or flag officer or a civil employee of equivalent
rank.

     (2) In a national decision-making position, able and authorized to speak for the DoD in
NRFC deliberations.

b. Activities. The HoD:

        (1) Attends the NRFC summer and winter congresses as the U.S. senior official.

       (2) Accompanied by the SO, is the senior representative to the semi-annual NRFC
congresses providing input and advice on RC issues relevant to the NATO Military Committee.

       (3) Presents approved DoD positions, policies, plans, and programs to the NRFC
concerning the RCs.

        (4) Evaluates proposed plans, positions, policies, and programs that could potentially
affect U.S. RCs, and presents recommendations to the Assistant Secretary of Defense for
Manpower and Reserve Affairs when appropriate.

       (5) Assists with the development and implementation of policies and guidance
concerning the RCs as it relates to the NRFC and other programs within the international training
programs portfolio.

       (6) Serves as a liaison to the NATO Allied Command Transformation for DoD RC
matters.

       (7) Oversees the RC support on matters including NRFC and Inter-Allied Confederation
of Reserve Officers events.

     (8) Maintains awareness of relevant issues and identifies common activities of interest to
NATO partners regarding the RCs.

       (9) Creates programs and budgets for attendance of the NRFC formal and informal
meetings.

        (10) Coordinates and oversees the Military Departments’ NATO school courses.

2. SO

   a. Requirements. In accordance with Reference (e), the SO:

        (1) Is a senior field grade officer, such as a colonel.

        (2) Has the authority to speak on behalf of the HoD.

   b. Activities. The SO:

     (1) Follows NRFC developments, and prepares efforts to support RC interests at the
summer and winter congresses.

        (2) Attends spring and fall SO meetings and summer and winter congresses.

      (3) Serves as a permanent member to the NRFC NATO School Professional
Development Working Group.

        (4) Regarding the NRFC:

            (a) Coordinates with the NRFC secretariat to address issues and questions brought to
the attention of the NRFC chairman before informal (i.e., congresses) and formal (i.e., SO
meeting) meetings.

            (b) Attends semi-annual preliminary formal and informal meetings. Provides input;
coordinates the congress agenda, information requirements, and attendance; and resolves any
issues not requiring the full committee.

                                    PART II. DEFINITIONS

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for purpose of this instruction.

Alliance Reserves. The Reserve Components of NATO Alliance members, partners, and
observers.

Allied Command Transformation. NATO's leading agent for change, driving, facilitating, and
advocating continuous improvement of Alliance capabilities to maintain and enhance the
military relevance and effectiveness of the Alliance. NATO Allied Command Transformation is
responsible for the planning and delivery of NATO education and training programmes using
NATO and national facilities.

Inter-Allied Confederation of Reserve Officers. An independent body founded in 1948, which
represents the Reserve officers from 28 NATO members and eight associated countries. It was
officially recognized by NATO in 1976 (see North Atlantic Military Committee Directive 0248/1
(Reference (g))) with the objectives of providing advice on the best utilization of Reservists,
continuing to improve the knowledge of NATO authorities about national Reserve Forces, and
exchanging information between member nations. It is a non-political, non-governmental, non-
profit-making organization, which cooperates with the NATO Alliance on Reservists’ issues (see
North Atlantic Military Committee Directive 0248/2 (Reference (h))).

Military Committee. The senior military authority in NATO and the oldest permanent body in
NATO after the North Atlantic Council. It is the primary source of military advice to NATO’s
civilian decision-making bodies: the North Atlantic Council and the Nuclear Planning Group.

NRFC. An advisory body that prepares conceptual proposals and develops approaches for the
Military Committee and member countries concerning Reserve forces issues.

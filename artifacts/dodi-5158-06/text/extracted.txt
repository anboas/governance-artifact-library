Purpose: In accordance with the authority in the current Unified Command Plan, DoD
Directive 5134.01, and the July 13, 2018 Deputy Secretary of Defense Memorandum, this issuance:
•   Establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and provides procedures for the JDDE in support of the
    development, improvement, and sustainment of deployment and distribution capabilities.
•   Establishes policy for OSD and DoD Component participation in the development of the campaign
    plan for global deployment and distribution to ensure overarching plans and the theater distribution
    plans are mutually supportive.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

DoD will maintain a JDDE that is:

   a. Robust, agile, effective, efficient, resilient, and flexible.

   b. Capable of projecting and sustaining U.S. military power anywhere in the world.

   c. In alignment with DoD-wide deployment and distribution requirements, related initiatives,
and activities.

                            SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION AND SUSTAINMENT
(USD(A&S)).

The USD(A&S):

   a. Serves as the Defense Logistics Executive, with overall responsibility for improving and
maintaining the defense logistics and global supply chain management system.

    b. Establishes policies on, and oversees all elements of, the DoD relating to sustainment,
including logistics, maintenance, and materiel readiness.


2.2. DIRECTOR, DEFENSE LOGISTICS AGENCY (DLA).

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., under the authority, direction, and control of
the USD(A&S), through the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Sustainment, the Director, DLA,
in conjunction with the JDDE and Joint Deployment and Distribution Coordinator (JDDC):

   a. Integrates deliberate planning efforts to include jointly developing global posture and
capacity which address distribution platforms and capabilities as a cohesive whole.

   b. As the DoD Executive Agent for Bulk Petroleum, provides support to the Joint Staff
Director for Logistics to develop and maintain a global petroleum distribution plan under DoD
Directive (DoDD) 5101.08E.

    c. Integrates management and operational activities to include, but not limited to, pallet and
containerization business rules and the development of additions or alternative routings within
the DoD global distribution network (GDN).

   d. Participates in source selection boards for enterprise transportation contracts.


2.3. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE (COMPTROLLER)/CHIEF FINANCIAL
OFFICER, DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE.

The Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of Defense,
establishes financial policy governing working capital funds and other fund types necessary to
facilitate the transportation and distribution financial process as outlined in DoD 7000.14-R.


2.4. DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The DoD Component heads:

  a. Align and harmonize planning and operations for deployment, distribution, global patient
movement, and continental U.S. (CONUS) patient distribution in coordination with
USTRANSCOM under DoD Instruction (DoDI) 6000.11.

    b. Identify, evaluate, prioritize, approve, fund, and implement deployment- and distribution-

   c. Mitigate deployment and distribution-related capability gaps.

    d. Develop joint cybersecurity and mission assurance, data, and information technology
strategies and initiatives.

    e. Participate in analysis and assessment of JDDE initiatives, operations, readiness, and
related studies.


2.5. CJCS.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the CJCS coordinates with the JDDC on the
use of JDDE deployment, distribution, transportation, and mobility policies, processes, and
capabilities to meet Combatant Commander (CCDR) readiness and operational requirements.


2.6. CCDRS.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the CCDRs:

    a. Coordinate with the JDDE to ensure deployment, distribution, transportation, and mobility
policies, processes, and capabilities meet operational readiness requirements.

   b. Integrate planning and conduct of deployment and distribution operations, including:

       (1) Joint reception, staging, onward movement, and integration activities.

       (2) Follow on combat sustainment.

        (3) Patient movement and evacuation within their respective areas of responsibility
consistent with DoDI 6000.11.

    c. Develop a theater distribution plan, a posture plan, a theater logistics overview, and a
theater patient movement plan, as appropriate, which are mutually supportive to United States
Transportation Command’s (USTRANSCOM’s) functional campaign plan for global
deployment and distribution in accordance with Enclosure D of CJCS Instruction 3110.01K.

   d. Integrate theater security cooperation activities, deployments, and capabilities supporting
global distribution.

2.7. COMMANDER, USTRANSCOM (CDRUSTRANSCOM).

In addition to responsibilities in Paragraphs 2.4., and 2.6., the CDRUSTRANSCOM:

    a. Serves as the JDDC and in that role, exercises coordinating authority for operations and
planning across all domains and will collaborate with other CCMDs, the Secretaries of the
Military Departments, and, as needed, U.S. Government agencies. This includes force
deployment, distribution, global patient movement, and CONUS patient movement under DoDI
6000.11.

   b. In accordance with the Unified Command Plan:

       (1) Provides analysis and assessment, develops and implements process improvements,
and advocates for global deployment and distribution capabilities.

      (2) Leads collaborative planning efforts to align and harmonize logistics functions and
recommend sequencing of logistics actions.

        (3) Develops and implements process improvements for information systems that
provide key capabilities of distribution-related activities (e.g., force movement, sustainment,
patient movement through the Defense Transportation System (DTS) in accordance with DoDI
6000.11.

        (4) Provides military representation to U.S. Government agencies, U.S. Commercial
entities, and international agencies for DoD GDN operations, as directed.

       (5) Integrates theater security cooperation activities, deployments, and capabilities
supporting the DoD GDN, in coordination with the geographic CCDRs.

        (6) Makes recommendations to the Secretary of Defense on the prioritized requirements
for the JDDE.

   c. Establishes the functional campaign plan for global deployment and distribution in
accordance with Enclosure D of CJCS Instruction 3110.01K.

   d. Serves as the lead for joint logistics over the shore doctrine and provides oversight of
annual joint logistics over the shore exercise and training.

    e. Serves as the lead DoD functional proponent for in-transit visibility (ITV) and related
automated identification technology capabilities for cargo and personnel moving in the DTS.

   f. Coordinates with other members of the JDDE to establish and charter forums to support
JDDE objectives.

                          SECTION 3: JDDE FUNCTIONS

3.1. JDDE.

    a. The JDDE boundaries include movement of forces and materiel from the point of origin to
the CCDR designated point of need, including CONUS, inter-theater, and intra-theater
movements.

   b. The JDDE is comprised of representatives from:

       (1) OSD.

       (2) Military Services.

       (3) Joint Staff.

       (4) Combatant Commands.

       (5) DLA.

       (6) Defense Health Agency.

       (7) USTRANSCOM, its component commands, and subordinate commands.

       (8) U.S. commercial industries (e.g., commercial transportation service providers and the
National Security Innovation Base).

      (9) U.S. Government agencies (e.g., Federal Emergency Management Agency,
Department of Health and Human Services, and Department of Veteran Affairs).

     (10) DoD and Service agencies (e.g., Army Air Force Exchange Service, Defense
Commissary Agency, Defense Security Cooperation Agency, Navy Exchange Command).

   c. The JDDE may also include:

       (1) Non-DoD U.S. Government organizations.

       (2) Non-governmental organizations.

      (3) Multinational organizations when DoD approves access to movement support to
accomplish a specified mission.

         (4) Allies and coalition partner nations when DoD approves security cooperation
activities access to movement to accomplish a specified mission.

   d. JDDE members will collaborate with the JDDC to:

     (1) Synchronize efforts to reform and enhance deployment, distribution, and patient
movement-related processes and systems.

       (2) Ensure end-to-end ITV from the point of origin to point of need with a holistic
approach and optimal solutions to meet various stakeholder requirements.

       (3) Routinely review JDDE capabilities and operations to:

           (a) Focus efforts on precision, reliability, efficiency, cost effectiveness, and ITV.

           (b) Assess performance against measures of effectiveness:

                1. Established indicators and indicator thresholds of JDDE precision, reliability,
efficiency, cost effectiveness, and ITV.

             2. New indicators and indicator thresholds that are developed when needed to
improve measures of precision, reliability, efficiency, cost effectiveness, and ITV.


3.2. JDDC.

The JDDC:

    a. Exercises coordinating authority for operations and planning across all domains and will
collaborate with other CCMDs, the Military Services, and as needed, U.S. Government agencies.

    b. Coordinates efforts and initiatives to reform and enhance deployment, distribution, and
patient movement-related processes and systems in coordination with USTRANSCOM under
Paragraph 2.11 of DoDI 6000.11.

   c. Leads the JDDE and exercises coordinating authority, as required to:

       (1) Provide analysis and assessment, develop and implement process improvements, and
advocate for global deployment, distribution, and patient movement capabilities in coordination
with USTRANSCOM that adheres to the policy in DoDI 6000.11.

        (2) Enhance and transform operations and planning through innovative and
transformational research and analysis.

    d. Uses analytical products to make recommendations, through the CJCS, to the Secretaries
of the Military Departments, and the USD(A&S) on the capability, capacity, characteristics,
design, sourcing, and other requirements for mobility assets needed to execute the
CDRUSTRANSCOM mission in accordance with the Unified Command Plan.

    e. Prioritizes requirements for joint interoperable processes and leverages joint
experimentation capabilities. Obtains funding for and conducts capabilities-based assessments,
technology demonstrations, and research, development, testing and evaluation which promote
joint interoperable processes, leveraging joint experimentation capabilities, as appropriate.

   f. Develops deployment and distribution training, doctrine, wargames and exercises.

        (1) Identifies and advocates for recommendations that incorporate joint deployment and

      (2) Develops, coordinates, and reviews objectives and mission templates for joint
deployment and distribution exercises, as depicted in joint doctrine.

       (3) Nominates exercises for entry into CJCS training and exercise programs.

   g. Develops, coordinates, and implements deployment and distribution performance metrics
and standards in coordination with JDDE members.

   h. Conducts periodic reviews focused on precision, reliability, efficiency, cost effectiveness,

  i. Monitors readiness of global mobility capabilities and maintains readiness of strategic
mobility capabilities.

   j. Leads collaborative planning efforts to align and harmonize deployment and distribution

       (1) Develops and implements process improvements for information systems which
provide key capabilities of distribution-related activities.

        (2) Conducts, coordinates, and participates in appropriate analyses of deployment and

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 DTS                       The portion of the worldwide transportation infrastructure that
                           supports DoD transportation needs in peace and war. The DTS
                           consists of two major elements: military (organic) and commercial
                           resources. Those resources include aircraft, assets, services, and
                           systems organic to, contracted for, or controlled by the DoD. The
                           DTS infrastructure, including ports, airlift, sealift, railway, highway,
                           ITV, information management systems, customs, and traffic
                           management that the DoD maintains and exercises in peacetime, is a
                           vital element of the DoD capability to project power worldwide. It
                           provides for responsive force projection and a seamless transition
                           between peacetime and wartime operations.

 DoD GDN                   The physical means, processes, systems, and personnel required for
                           DoD storage, staging, and movement of DoD materiel and forces

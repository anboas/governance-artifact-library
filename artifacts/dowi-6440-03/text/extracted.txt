             DOW LABORATORY AND RESPONSE NETWORK

Originating Component:        Office of the Under Secretary of War for Personnel and Readiness

Effective:                    February 3, 2026

Releasability:                Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                              at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02, this issuance establishes policy,
assigns responsibilities, provides procedures, and introduces a forum that allows DoW laboratories,
programs, and activities with analytic or response capabilities to coordinate execution, develop
consensus, and make recommendations governing the detection; identification, characterization, and
diagnosis; and reporting of chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear (CBRN) agents; infectious
diseases; and other all-hazards agents of military or national significance to support the DoW’s global
and homeland defense missions.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSW, the Military Departments (including the Coast Guard at all times,
including when it is a Service in the Department of Homeland Security by agreement with that
Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the
Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the
Defense Agencies, the DoW Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the
DoW (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoW Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

  a. A system of DoW laboratories, programs, and activities (hereinafter referred to as the
DoW Laboratory and Response Network (DL&RN)) will be maintained to:

        (1) Improve data collection and query or interrogation, analysis and interpretation,
fusion, and information sharing.

       (2) Harmonize, validate, and enhance quality assurance and quality control of laboratory
protocols, methods, and materials.

       (3) Standardize the data elements used, by test methodology, for reporting results and the
associated test result notification schemes.

       (4) Provide a unified DoW position on related issues external to the DoW.

    b. The DL&RN will function as an active member network of the Federal interagency
Integrated Consortium of Laboratory Networks (ICLN) and its Network Coordinating Group
(NCG) in accordance with the August 1, 2016 Memorandum of Agreement for an ICLN and the
ICLN Charge and Charter.

       (1) Individual DL&RN member laboratories may also serve as members of any of the
individual laboratory networks comprising the ICLN.

        (2) DL&RN member laboratories or activities with analytic or response capabilities
regarding CBRN agents, infectious diseases, and other all-hazards agents of military or national
significance will provide defense support of civil authorities on a fully reimbursable basis, when
resources are available and approved by the Secretary of War, in accordance with DoD
Directive 3025.18; as depicted for the Laboratory Response Network (LRN) in DoD
Instructions (DoDIs) 3025.24 and 6200.03; future interagency agreements; Sections 5121
through 5206 of Title 42, United States Code; or Section 1535 of Title 31, United States Code
(also known as “The Economy Act”).

                           SECTION 3: DL&RN MISSION

3.1. PURPOSE.

The DL&RN functions as an active member network of the ICLN and its NCG. Additionally,
the DL&RN’s activities seek to:

    a. Improve data collection and query or interrogation, analysis and interpretation, fusion, and
information sharing.

    b. Harmonize, validate, and enhance quality assurance, proficiency/performance
testing/challenge programs, and the quality control of laboratory protocols, methods, and
materials.

    c. Standardize data elements used, by test methodology, for reporting results and the
associated test result notification schemes.


3.2. GENERAL.

   a. Program, Project, or Service Description.

    The DoW has a substantial detection, diagnostic, analysis, and reporting capability for CBRN
agents, infectious diseases, and other all-hazards agents of military or national significance. For
certain agents, the DoW constitutes the only national resource to accomplish these tasks. In
addition, the DoW has a substantial detection, diagnostic, analysis, and reporting capability for
emerging infectious diseases. The DL&RN’s activities enhance efforts regarding the
coordination and integration of these capabilities within DoW. The DL&RN also provides a
mechanism for improving interagency collaboration and collaboration among DoW Components.

   b. Organizational Interfaces.

        (1) The DoW is a signatory of the August 1, 2016 Interagency Memorandum of
Agreement for an ICLN, and the DL&RN participates as a member network of the ICLN.
Participation in the DL&RN does not supersede the relationships, roles, responsibilities, and
obligations of DL&RN member laboratories that are members of any of the individual laboratory
networks comprising the ICLN.

       (2) Participation by appropriate DoW clinical laboratories in the Centers for Disease
Control and Prevention-sponsored LRN is required (as coordinated through the Defense Health
Agency’s Assistant Director, Health Care Administration or Assistant Director, Support, as
appropriate, and the DoW Liaison (Gatekeeper) for the LRN).

       (3) The DASW(HRP&O) serves as the DoW representative to the ICLN’s JLC. The
DASW(HRP&O) and DASW(CBRND) represent the DoW on the ICLN’s NCG. All DoW
Components may participate in ICLN subgroups and will notify the DL&RN Executive
Secretary of this participation.

   c. DL&RN Funding.

       (1) Administrative Costs.

       Costs for organizational and administrative support will be provided, on an equal cost
sharing basis, by the Offices of the ASW(HA), ASW(ND-CBD), and ASW(HD&HA).

       (2) Network Member Funding.

       Funding for DL&RN member laboratories, programs, and activities will continue through
each participant’s established line of funding. Additional funding for new obligations or taskings
placed on the members will be provided as determined appropriate by and coordinated amongst
the Offices of the ASW(HA), ASW(ND-CBD), and ASW(HD&HA).

       (3) Establishment and Funding of a DL&RN Executive Secretary Position.

      The Office of the ASW(HA) is the lead for and will establish a full-time equivalent U.S.
Government position to serve as the DL&RN Executive Secretary.


3.3. DL&RN EXECUTIVE SECRETARY RESPONSIBILITIES.

The DL&RN Executive Secretary:

   a. Will oversee administration and management of the DL&RN, attend all ICLN-related
meetings, and serve as the DoW Liaison (Gatekeeper) for the LRN.

    b. Generates and maintains a comprehensive inventory of the DoW’s detection; testing;
analysis; identification, characterization, and diagnosis; and result reporting capabilities for
CBRN agents; infectious diseases; and other all-hazards agents of military or national
significance.

   c. Reviews and identifies priority issues on an ongoing basis to the proponent offices of the
Secretary of War, with recommendation of solutions requiring policy review and resourcing.

    d. Provides data, reports, and consultation to DoW officials and, as appropriate, other
Federal officials regarding laboratory detection; testing; analysis; identification, characterization,
and diagnosis; and result reporting before, during, and after CBRN and all-hazards incidents of
national and military significance.

   e. Advocates for the DoW and its Components within the ICLN and its member networks.

   f. Ensures continued participation in the ICLN and its NCG and working groups.

    g. Generates an annual report regarding DL&RN activities as described in Paragraphs 3.3.b.
through 3.3.f. on a fiscal year basis. Within 60 calendar days of the end of each fiscal year,
submits the annual report to the Offices of the ASW(HA), ASW(ND-CBD), and
ASW(HD&HA).

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 ICLN                      A coordinated and operational system of Federal department or
                           agency-sponsored laboratory networks that provide timely, high
                           quality, and interpretable results for early detection and effective
                           consequence management of acts of terrorism, and other events
                           requiring an integrated laboratory response.

TERM                 DEFINITION

ICLN JLC             A group composed of one senior policy member from each Federal
                     department or agency participating in the ICLN. The JLC provides
                     advice to senior Federal Government officials, with the objective of
                     ensuring that the appropriate strategy and funding are in place for
                     effective laboratory testing for detection and response to health and
                     national security emergencies. The JLC, in accordance with the
                     August 1, 2016 Memorandum of Agreement for an ICLN, is chaired
                     by a Department of Homeland Security representative to the JLC, and
                     is supported by the Executive Secretariat.

ICLN NCG             The NCG is under the direction of the JLC and is a group composed
                     of senior technical and program representatives from the Department
                     of Homeland Security, DoW, Department of Justice, Department of
                     State, and each network represented in the ICLN. The NCG develops
                     and proposes policies and procedures, and helps carry out the
                     operations of the ICLN through a close and formal working
                     relationship among the operational leadership of the individual
                     networks and other involved parties. The NCG, in accordance with
                     the August 1, 2016 Memorandum of Agreement for an ICLN, is
                     chaired by a Department of Homeland Security representative. The
                     NCG is supported by the Executive Secretariat and receives input
                     from technical working groups.

infectious disease   A disease potentially caused by a pathogenic organism (including a
                     bacteria, virus, fungus, or parasite) that is acquired by a person and
                     that reproduces in that person. An infectious disease may occur as
                     the result of an infection by a naturally occurring and well
                     characterized infectious agent, or by an emerging or novel infectious
                     agent.

TERM       DEFINITION

LRN        A diverse national and international laboratory network of local,
           State, and Federal public health, hospital-based, food testing,
           veterinary and environmental testing laboratories that provide rapid
           detection assays, laboratory diagnostics, and has the capacity to
           respond to biological and chemical terrorism and other public health
           emergencies.

           A reciprocal multi-agency collaboration and partnership involving
           key stakeholders in the preparation and response to biological and
           chemical terrorism. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention,
           the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the DoW, and the Association of
           Public Health Laboratories are its founding partners. The mission of
           the LRN and its partners is to maintain an integrated national and
           international network of laboratories that are fully equipped to
           respond quickly to acts of chemical or biological terrorism, emerging
           infectious diseases, and other public health threats and emergencies.

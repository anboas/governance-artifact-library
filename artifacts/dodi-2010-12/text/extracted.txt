SUBJECT: Aviation Leadership Program

References: See Enclosure


1. PURPOSE. This Instruction:

    a. Reissues DoD Directive 2010.12 (Reference (a)) as a DoD Instruction in accordance with
the guidance in DoD Instruction 5025.01 (Reference (b)) and the authority in Deputy Secretary
of Defense Memorandum (Reference (c)) and DoD Directive 5111.1 (Reference (d)).

    b. Updates DoD policy and responsibilities for the Aviation Leadership Program (ALP)
established by chapter 905 of title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.) (Reference (e)).


2. APPLICABILITY. This Instruction applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of
the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands
(CCMDs), the Office of the Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense
Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the Department
of Defense (hereafter referred to collectively as the “DoD Components”).


3. DEFINITIONS. See Glossary


4. POLICY. It is DoD policy that:

    a. The Secretary of the Air Force may, as part of the ALP program, provide undergraduate
pilot training (UPT) and necessary related training to personnel of the air forces of friendly, less-
developed foreign nations. Training under ALP shall include language training and programs to
promote better awareness and understanding of the democratic institutions and social framework
of the United States. For the purpose of programming and budgeting for costs associated with
this program, tuition costs will be those actual costs incurred by the U.S. Air Force (USAF).

    b. Training will be provided to ALP students under the Joint Security Assistance Training
Regulation (Reference (f)). Under this regulation, students participating in the ALP will be
considered to be students participating in the International Military Education and Training
(IMET) program (see sections 2347-2347h of title 22, U.S.C. (Reference (g))), except that costs
for ALP students will be charged to USAF appropriations.

    c. Foreign participation in the ALP shall be at no cost to the U.S. Government except as
otherwise stated in this Instruction. For estimating and programming costs associated with the
ALP, tuition, transportation incident to the training, supplies and equipment to be used during the
training, flight and other special clothing required for training, billeting, food, health services,
and other related costs incurred by the USAF will be those actual costs incurred.

    d. The Secretary of the Air Force may establish ALP scholarships and provide ALP
supplemental living allowances to individual participants from USAF appropriations, taking into
account the amount of living allowances authorized for a member of the U.S. Armed Forces
under similar circumstances. The Secretary of the Air Force may provide these scholarships and
supplemental living allowances to cover all, or a part of, the costs of a country’s participation in
the ALP. Any cost incurred by a country for the ALP that is not paid for by an ALP scholarship
or supplemental living allowance shall be paid for by that country in accordance with section
2751 of Reference (g), under the procedures in Defense Security Cooperation Agency (DSCA)
Manual 5105.38-M (Reference (h)).


5. RESPONSIBILITIES

    a. The Secretary of the Air Force, consistent with the statutory authority in Reference (e),
shall:

       (1) Establish and administer the ALP, including ALP scholarships and ALP
supplemental living allowances, within available Air Force appropriations.

       (2) Identify selection methodology and a feedback mechanism.

       (3) Initiate the annual request to CCMDs for a prioritized list of country nominations.

        (4) Forward country nominations to the DSCA for review at least 30 calendar days
before the desired date for offering an ALP scholarship to foreign country.

       (5) Offer an ALP scholarship and supplemental living allowances for participation in the
ALP.

   b. The Under Secretary of Defense for Policy (USD(P)) shall exercise policy oversight and
ensure that the Director, DSCA, under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P), shall:

      (1) Review countries submitted by the Secretary of the Air Force for participation in
ALP to confirm IMET eligibility.

                                           GLOSSARY

The following terms and their definitions are for the purposes of this Instruction only.

ALP. An Air Force-funded UPT program for students from friendly, less-developed foreign
nations. The ALP shall consist of language training, UPT, and necessary related training, as well
as programs to promote better awareness and understanding of the democratic institutions and
social framework of the United States.

ALP scholarships. Defense articles and defense services provided by the USAF to, or for the use
of, students enrolled in the ALP, including:

   Language training, UPT, and necessary related training.

   Transportation incident to the training.

   Supplies and equipment to be used during the training.

   Flight clothing and other special clothing required for the training.

   Billeting, food, and health services.

    Programs to promote better awareness and understanding of the democratic institutions and
social framework of the United States.

ALP supplemental living allowances. Funds provided by the USAF for use by students enrolled
in the ALP to cover their living expenses, limited to supplemental living allowance rates
authorized under the IMET Program in accordance with section 2751 et. seq. of Reference (h).

Friendly, less-developed foreign nation. Any nation so designated by the Secretary of the Air
Force, with the concurrence of the Secretary of Defense.

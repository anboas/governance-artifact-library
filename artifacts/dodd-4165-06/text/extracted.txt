               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

    a. This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, Office of the Chairman of the
Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

   b. This issuance applies to all DoD real property holdings except:

       (1) Civil works projects (unless relating to mineral exploration and extraction).

     (2) The acquisition and management of defense industrial plants that are governed by
DoD Directive 4275.5.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. The acquisition, management, and disposal of real property within the DoD is a function
of the Military Departments acting on behalf of the DoD, subject to such specific exceptions as
are established by law or by direction of the Secretary of Defense.

    b. The acquisition, management, and disposal of real property will be performed to advance
the overall mission of the DoD and will not be governed solely by the individual interests of the
DoD Components.

    c. DoD real property will be managed in the most economical manner to reduce DoD costs
without obstructing or prejudicing current or projected defense requirements. Management of
real property must be in accordance with DoD Instruction (DoDI) 4165.70.

   d. A Military Department will meet the real property acquisition requirements of the DoD
Components utilizing real property under its jurisdiction or by acquiring additional real property,
applying policies of the Military Department and the DoD as a whole. Acquisitions must be in
accordance with DoDI 4165.71.

    e. DoD real property that is no longer required for current or projected defense requirements
will be disposed. Disposals must be in accordance with DoDI 4165.72.

    f. Utilizing the multiple-use principle, DoD real property will be made available for mineral
exploration and extraction to the maximum extent possible consistent with military operations,
national defense activities, environmental conservation and protection, and Army civil works
activities.

   g. In accordance with Executive Order 13327, Public Law 114-287, and Public Law 114-
318, DoD real property will be managed to promote the most efficient and economic use of DoD

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION AND SUSTAINMENT
(USD(A&S)).

The USD(A&S):

   a. Exercises overall policy making and oversight responsibility of DoD real property.

    b. Establishes overarching guidance and procedures for the acquisition, management, and
disposal of DoD real property.

    c. Is hereby delegated or re-delegated, as the case may be, the authorities and
responsibilities:

       (1) Vested in the Secretary of Defense in accordance with Chapter 159 of Title 10,
United States Code;

       (2) Delegated from the Administrator of General Services relating to real property
matters;

        (3) Vested in the Secretary of Defense by any other provision of the United States Code,
a national defense authorization act, or a DoD appropriations act, relating to the acquisition,
management (including mineral exploration and extraction), or disposal of real property; or

       (4) Vested in the Secretary of Defense by Executive order or regulation and relating to
the acquisition, management (including mineral exploration and extraction), or disposal of real
property.


2.2. DIRECTOR, WASHINGTON HEADQUARTERS SERVICES (WHS).

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., and with respect to the Pentagon Reservation
only, the Director, WHS, implements policies and programs to manage and dispose of real
property that comprises the Pentagon Reservation in accordance with this issuance. Such
policies and programs will specifically ensure that WHS:

   a. Establishes and maintains an accurate inventory of the interests in real property that
comprise the Pentagon Reservation.

   b. Has a comprehensive master plan for the Pentagon Reservation.


2.3. DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The DoD Component heads:

  a. Determine the real property requirements necessary for the performance of the
Component’s mission.

   b. Communicate those requirements to the servicing Military Department in a timely
manner.

    c. Budget and financially manage for the acquisition and sustainment of real property needed
to meet the Component’s mission.


2.4. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., the Secretaries of the Military Departments
implement policies and programs to acquire, manage, and dispose of real property in accordance
with this issuance. Such policies and programs will specifically ensure that their Department:

    a. Establishes and maintains for all properties in which DoD maintains a legal interest (to
include owned, leased, or other managed properties) an accurate inventory to account for the real
property under its management responsibility.

   b. Holds or makes plans to obtain and sustain the real property it needs for its own missions
and serve as a real property agent for the missions of the DoD Components its real property
supports in accordance with the February 22, 2021 Deputy Secretary of Defense Memorandum.

   c. Has comprehensive master plans that cover each installation under its jurisdiction.

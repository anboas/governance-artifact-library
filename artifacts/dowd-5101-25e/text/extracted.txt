                     TRAINING SCHOOL

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSW, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoW Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoW (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoW
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

    a. Training and education of allies and partners is essential to naval small craft
interoperability with U.S. forces.

    b. DoW efforts to improve small craft interoperability with allies and partners should be
available to all geographic Combatant Commands and should include designing opportunities to
improve interoperability between the Military Services and other security forces of friendly
foreign countries.

    c. Training and education of allies and partners in small craft interoperability should support
the participation of the security forces of friendly foreign countries in coalition or international
military operations, or participation in DoW exercises.

    d. All DoW training and technical schools must conform to applicable human rights and
international humanitarian laws.

                          SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF WAR FOR POLICY (USW(P)).

The USW(P):

  a. Serves as the Principal Staff Assistant overseeing the Secretary of the Navy designation as
DoD EA for NAVSCIATTS.

    b. Establishes policy and provides oversight on all security cooperation (SC) matters,
including regional and country-level guidance to DoW Components regarding desired outcomes
of SC programs and activities, in accordance with DoD Directive 5132.03.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF WAR FOR STRATEGY, PLANS, AND FORCES
(ASW(SPF)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USW(P), the ASW(SPF):

   a. Develops policy and oversees resourcing to support the DoD EA for NAVSCIATTS and
advocates for additional resources as necessary through appropriate resourcing processes.

   b. Not later than March 15 of each year, reviews and transmits, on behalf of the Secretary of
War and with the concurrence of the Secretary of State, the annual report to the appropriate
congressional committees on NAVSCIATTS activities and operating costs for the previous fiscal
year as required by Section 352(f) of Title 10, United States Code.


2.3. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF WAR FOR SPECIAL OPERATIONS AND LOW-
INTENSITY CONFLICT (ASW(SO/LIC)).

The ASW(SO/LIC) exercises authority, direction, and control of all special operations-peculiar
administrative matters relating to NAVSCIATTS through the administrative chain of command
of the Commander, United States Special Operations Command (CDRUSSOCOM).


2.4. UNDER SECRETARY OF WAR FOR INTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY

The Under Secretary of War for Intelligence and Security establishes and oversees policy for the
vetting of international military students attending NAVSCIATTS and the family members
accompanying them to the United States for that training.

2.5. SECRETARY OF THE NAVY.

As the DoD EA for NAVSCIATTS, the Secretary of the Navy:

    a. Provides programming, budgeting, and financial management of the resources necessary
to support the operation of NAVSCIATTS, including all operation and maintenance costs (e.g.,
personnel costs and base-operations support costs).

    b. Not later than February 1 of each year, submits a detailed report on NAVSCIATTS
activities and operating costs for the previous fiscal year to the ASW(SPC) for review and to
inform the ASW(SPC) annual report to the appropriate congressional committees addressed in
Paragraph 2.2.b.

    c. Approves the NAVSCIATTS annual program and activity plan, including training
activities, in coordination with the geographic Combatant Commanders and with the Department
of State.

    d. In coordination with the ASW(SPC) and the ASW(SO/LIC), reviews and resources
personnel requirements to conduct small craft instruction at NAVSCIATTS.

   e. Establishes appropriate agreements with the CDRUSSOCOM to support NAVSCIATTS.

    f. Submits to the Director, Defense Security Cooperation Agency, SC workforce training and
certification requirements.


2.6. CDRUSSOCOM.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the ASW(SO/LIC), the CDRUSSOCOM:

  a. As appropriate, coordinates special operations-peculiar curriculum with the
ASW(SO/LIC) and the DoD EA for NAVSCIATTS.

    b. Funds and provides program management for NAVSCIATTS special operations forces
information environment.

                              DOD INSTRUCTION 4105.70
PROHIBITION OF THE SALE OR RENTAL OF SEXUALLY EXPLICIT
              MATERIAL ON DOD PROPERTY

Originating Component:        Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness

Effective:                    November 30, 2016

Releasability:                Cleared for public release. Available on the DoD Issuances Website at
                              http://www.dtic.mil/whs/directives.

Reissues and Cancels:         DoD Instruction 4105.70, “Sale or Rental of Sexually Explicit Material on
                              DoD Property,” June 2, 2006, as amended

Approved by:                  Peter Levine, Performing the Duties of the Under Secretary of Defense for
                              Personnel and Readiness


Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02, this issuance:
• Establishes policy and assigns responsibilities for restrictions on the sale or rental of sexually
explicit materials:
   o On property under the jurisdiction of the DoD; or
   o By Service members or DoD civilian employees acting in their official capacities in accordance
with Section 2495b of Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.) and consistent with DoD Instruction
1330.09.
• Establishes the Resale Activities Review Board (referred to in this instruction as the “Board”)
consistent with Section 2495b of Title 10, U.S.C. and DoD Instruction 5105.18.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY. This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of
the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the
Office of the Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD
Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in
this issuance as the “DoD Components”).


1.2. POLICY. It is DoD policy that:

    a. No sexually explicit material will be offered for sale or rental on property under DoD
jurisdiction.

    b. No Service member or DoD civilian employee, acting in an official capacity, may offer
for sale, remuneration, or rental sexually explicit material to another person.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS (ASD(M&RA)). The ASD(M&RA), under the authority, direction, and control of
the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness, and in accordance with DoD
Directives 5124.02 and 5124.09:

   a. Monitors and ensures compliance with this instruction.

   b. Establishes the nine-member Board.

    c. Appoints six Board members and three alternates in accordance with Section 2495b of
Title 10, U.S.C., and Paragraph 3.1. of this instruction.

   d. Designates one Board member to be the Chair.

    e. Monitors the activities of the Board and ensures that the Board discharges its
responsibilities as described in Section 3 of this instruction.

   f. Reviews the Board’s recommendations and makes the final determination whether
material is sexually explicit, as defined by Section 2495b of Title 10, U.S.C.


2.2. DEPUTY ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MILITARY
COMMUNITY AND FAMILY POLICY. Under the authority, direction, and control of the
ASD(M&RA), the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Military Community and Family
Policy:

   a. Administers the Board and provides military resale policy guidance.

   b. Publicizes the Board’s recommendations through the Office of the Under Secretary of
Defense for Personnel and Readiness public website.


2.3. GENERAL COUNSEL OF THE DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE. The General
Counsel of the Department of Defense provides legal advice to the Board.


2.4. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS. The Secretaries of the
Military Departments:

   a. Ensure that their respective Department’s resale activities comply with this instruction.

   b. Each appoint one member to the Board, in accordance with Section 2495b of Title 10,
U.S.C.

    c. Provide nominees to the ASD(M&RA) concerning the six Board members and three
alternates appointed by the ASD(M&RA) in accordance with Paragraph 3.1.

              SECTION 3: RESALE ACTIVITIES REVIEW BOARD

3.1. COMPOSITION. The Board will have nine members and three alternates, including at
least one member, appointed by the ASD(M&RA), with experience managing or advocating for
military family programs and who is also an eligible patron of the Defense commissary system
and the Defense exchange system.

   a. Board members and alternates will be representatives of the following:

       (1) The Senior Enlisted Advisors of the Army, the Navy, the Air Force, Marine Corps,
and the National Guard Bureau.

       (2) The Surgeons General of the Army, the Navy, and the Air Force.

      (3) The military community and family programs of the Army, Navy, Air Force, and
Marine Corps.

    b. Board members will be senior full-time or permanent part-time DoD civilian employees
or active duty Service members not currently assigned to or employed by the Army and Air
Force Exchange Service, the Navy Exchange Service Command, or the Marine Corps Exchange
Service.

    c. The ASD(M&RA) will appoint one member to be Chair for a 2-year term; the Chair will
rotate among the Military Departments.

   d. A vacancy on the Board will be filled in the same manner as the original appointment.


3.2. AUTHORITIES.

    a. The Board has the authority and responsibility to review material offered or to be offered
for sale or rental on property under DoD jurisdiction and to recommend to the ASD(M&RA)
whether any such material is sexually explicit as defined in Section 2495b of Title 10, U.S.C.

    b. If the ASD(M&RA) determines that any material offered for sale or rental on property
under DoD jurisdiction is sexually explicit, such material must be withdrawn from all retail
outlets where it is sold or rented and returned to distributors or suppliers. Any material that is
determined to be sexually explicit will not be offered for sale or rental on property under DoD
jurisdiction unless the Board reconsiders the material under Paragraph 3.3.d of this Section, and
the ASD(M&RA) decides that the material is not sexually explicit.


3.3. PROCEDURES.

    a. The Board will convene at least once a year, and additionally as necessary, to review and
make recommendations to the ASD(M&RA) concerning whether any material offered or to be
offered for sale or rental on property under DoD jurisdiction is sexually explicit as defined in

Section 2495b of Title 10, U.S.C. The Board will, to the extent practicable, maintain and update
relevant information about Board recommendations.

    b. At the conclusion of the Board’s review and the ASD(M&RA) determination, the
ASD(M&RA) will issue guidance to the Military Departments for exchange service
headquarters, purchasing agents, and managers of retail outlets about the purchase, withdrawal,
and return of sexually explicit material. The ASD(M&RA) may also provide guidance to the
Military Departments for exchange service headquarters, purchasing agents, and managers of
retail outlets about material that he or she has determined is not sexually explicit. Purchasing
agents and managers of retail outlets will continue to follow their usual purchasing and stocking
practices unless instructed otherwise by the ASD(M&RA).

    c. Any purchasing agent or manager of a retail outlet must request in writing a review from
the Board and ASD(M&RA) determination about questionable material either before purchase or
as soon as possible if:

       (1) He or she has reason to believe that material offered or to be offered for sale or rental
on property under DoD jurisdiction may be sexually explicit as defined in Section 2495b of Title
10, U.S.C.

        (2) Such material is not addressed by the ASD(M&RA)’s guidance issued in Paragraph
3.b of this Section.

   d. Material determined to be sexually explicit by the ASD(M&RA) may be submitted to the
Board for reconsideration every 5 years. If substantive changes in the publication standards
occur earlier, the purchasing agent or manager of a retail outlet under DoD jurisdiction may
request a review.

   e. The Board will establish procedures for the exchange services to provide material for the
Board to review.

G.2. DEFINITIONS. These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

dominant theme. A theme of any material that is superior in power, influence, and importance
to all other themes in the material combined.

lascivious. Lewd and intended or designed to elicit a sexual response.

material. An audio recording, a film or video recording, or a periodical with visual depictions,
produced in any medium.

property under the jurisdiction of the DoD. Includes commissaries, all facilities operated by
the Army and Air Force Exchange Service, the Navy Exchange Service Command, the Navy
Resale and Services Support Office, and the Marine Corps Exchange; and ship stores.

sexually explicit material. Material, the dominant theme of which is the depiction or
description of nudity, including sexual or excretory activities or organs, in a lascivious way.

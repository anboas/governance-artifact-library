of Defense (SecDef) Memorandum, this issuance establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and
provides procedures for defense intelligence and counterintelligence expense (DICE) authority pursuant
to Public Law 116-92, also known and referred to in this issuance as the “National Defense
Authorization Act (NDAA) for Fiscal Year (FY) 2020.”

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to:

    a. OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff
and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department
of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities
within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD Components”).

   b. Military Intelligence Program (MIP)-funded DICE authority for FYs 2020 through 2025
and in future FYs if Section 1057 of the NDAA for FY 2020 is amended to extend this authority.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. DoD Components must use DICE authority instead of the emergency and extraordinary
expense authority in accordance with Section 127 of Title 10, United States Code for all MIP-
funded intelligence and counterintelligence (CI) activities pursuant to Paragraph (a) of Section
1057 of the NDAA for FY 2020, regarding intelligence and CI objects of a confidential,
extraordinary, or emergency nature.

   b. The SecDef must approve a single expenditure under DICE authority exceeding $100,000.

  c. DICE authority does not provide additional appropriation, funding, or dollars to DoD
Component MIP budgets.

  d. MIP-funded DICE authority for FYs 2020 through 2025 may be extended through future
NDAA legislation.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR INTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY
(USD(I&S)).

The USD(I&S):

   a. Serves as the MIP Executive, in accordance with DoD Directive 5205.12.

    b. Establishes policy and oversees implementation of DICE authority in consultation with
the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of Defense
(USD(C)/CFO).

   c. Oversees and inspects the execution of DICE expenditures.

    d. Allocates the amount of MIP funds that each DoD Component may expend using DICE
authority.

    e. Executes SecDef certification and reporting responsibilities for MIP intelligence and CI
activities in accordance with Section 1057 of the NDAA for FY 2020.

       (1) Based on input from the DoD Component heads, certifies that each expenditure was
made for an object of a confidential, extraordinary, or emergency nature.

        (2) By December 31 of each calendar year, submits a report of expenditures made during
the preceding FY to the congressional defense committees and the congressional intelligence
committees.

   f. Obtains SecDef decision of DoD Component requests for any single expenditure
exceeding $100,000.

  g. Upon submission of appropriate rationale and justification, and in consultation with the
USD(C)/CFO, may approve new allocations:

       (1) Of five percent or less of the amounts made available for the MIP in any FY in which
Section 1057 of the NDAA for FY 2020 is in effect.

        (2) In excess of five percent of amounts made available for the MIP 30 days after
notifying the congressional defense committees and congressional intelligence committees.


2.2. DIRECTOR FOR DEFENSE INTELLIGENCE (COLLECTION AND SPECIAL
PROGRAMS) (DDI(C&SP)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(I&S), the DDI(C&SP):

    a. Develops DICE policy and provides guidance to DoD Components on the implementation
of DICE authority for intelligence and CI objects of a confidential, extraordinary, or emergency
nature.

   b. In coordination with the Director for Defense Intelligence (Counterintelligence, Law
Enforcement, and Security) (DDI(CL&S)) and the Director for Defense Intelligence (Intelligence
and Security Programs and Resources) (DDI(ISP&R)):

       (1) Prepares and processes DoD Component DICE requests of single expenditures
exceeding $100,000 for review. Forwards recommended DICE requests to the USD(I&S) for
SecDef decision.

        (2) Consolidates DoD Component requests for DICE allocations and coordinates
ceilings.

       (3) Reviews DoD Component DICE requests of single expenditures exceeding $100,000
for SecDef approval.

       (4) Reviews DoD Component use of DICE authority to ensure that DICE funds are used
only for intelligence and CI objects of a confidential, extraordinary, or emergency nature.

   c. In coordination with the DDI(ISP&R), prepares the SecDef’s annual report to Congress in
accordance with Section 1057(d) of the NDAA for FY 2020.


2.3. DDI(CL&S).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(I&S), the DDI(CL&S), in coordination
with the DDI(C&SP) and the DDI(ISP&R):

   a. Develops guidance on the implementation of DICE for CI.

   b. Reviews all DICE actions with a CI equity.

   c. Reviews DoD Component CI requests for SecDef approval of any single expenditure
exceeding $100,000.


2.4. DDI(ISP&R).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(I&S), the DDI(ISP&R):

   a. In coordination with the DDI(C&SP) and the DDI(CL&S):

       (1) Establishes DoD Component allocation ceilings and tracks DICE expenditures
through the overall DoD planning, programming, budgeting, and execution process.

       (2) Reviews DoD Component requests for SecDef approval of any single expenditure
exceeding $100,000.

       (3) Tracks allocation requirements less than $100,000.

       (4) By December 31 of each calendar year in which Section 1057 of the NDAA for FY

        (5) Consolidates DoD Component annual DICE requests and recommends DICE

   b. Conducts reviews of DoD Component quarterly DICE execution and evaluates mid-year

    c. Provides timelines and formats to DoD Components for annual requests for DICE

2.5. USD(C)/CFO.

The USD(C)/CFO:

   a. Advises the USD(I&S) on DICE best practices of use of DICE authority and recording of
DICE expenses.

   b. Consults with the USD(I&S) on DoD Component requests for any single expenditure
exceeding $100,000.


2.6. GENERAL COUNSEL OF THE DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE.

The General Counsel of the Department of Defense provides legal review of DICE matters
including, but not limited to, DoD Component requests for expenditures requiring SecDef
approval.


2.7. DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The DoD Component heads authorized to expend DICE:

   a. Program sufficient MIP operation and maintenance funds in accordance with DoD
Directive 5205.12.

    b. For any year in which Section 1057 of the NDAA for FY 2020 is in effect, expend DICE
allocations for intelligence and CI objects of a confidential, extraordinary, or emergency nature,
within the amounts allocated to them by the USD(I&S).

    c. Establish internal policies and procedures to record and manage the DoD Component use
of DICE authority and make them available to the Office of the USD(I&S) upon request.

                              SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. REPORTING INSTRUCTIONS.

   a. Forecasting of DICE Authority.

     Forecasting for DICE authority requires DoD Components to program operational
intelligence and CI MIP funds in their annual budget. This baseline provides the USD(I&S)
initial visibility of DoD Component DICE requirements.

   b. Recording DICE Expenses.

       (1) DoD Components will oversee and review execution of current allocations, submit
expenditures, and coordinate with the USD(I&S) through DDI(ISP&R) and DDI(C&SP) if an
increase in authority is required. DoD Components may expend DICE as reported in their
program element.

        (2) Quarterly DICE reports will include a description of each expenditure, purpose,
program element, and certifying official. Officials must certify that expenditures are made for
intelligence and CI objects of a confidential, extraordinary, or emergency nature.


3.2. SINGLE EXPENDITURE EXCEEDING $100,000.

DoD Components that submit requests for SecDef approval of any single expenditure exceeding
$100,000 must provide justification to the DDI(C&SP) that the expenditure is for an intelligence
and CI object of a confidential, extraordinary, or emergency nature.

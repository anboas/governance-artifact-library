                  TRAINING PROGRAM

Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5111.10 and consistent with the
authorities detailed in Section 322 of Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.), this issuance establishes
policy, assigns responsibilities, and provides procedures for the oversight, management, and execution
of the readiness program for U.S. special operations forces (SOF) to train with the forces of friendly
foreign countries (commonly known and referred to in this issuance as “joint combined exchange
training” (JCET)).

                SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff (JS), the Combatant Commands, the Office of
Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field
Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. U.S. SOF are trained in the appropriate mission-essential tasks required to conduct special
operations activities identified in Section 167(k) of Title 10, U.S.C., including, but not limited to,
foreign internal defense and unconventional warfare.

   b. The Assistant Secretary of Defense for Special Operations and Low-Intensity Conflict
(ASD(SO/LIC)) must approve all JCET activities.

    c. The primary purpose of training events governed by this issuance is U.S. SOF readiness
training, therefore;

       (1) Any benefits to the partner nation resulting from JCET events with U.S. SOF are
incidental to the purpose of JCET events.

        (2) JCET events designed and planned to primarily build capacity or capability of
foreign forces are considered security cooperation and are not authorized by Section 322 of
Title 10, U.S.C.

       (3) JCET events are not considered operational deployments.

        (4) JCET events will be executed in accordance with Section 362 of Title 10, U.S.C. and
the August 18, 2014 Secretary of Defense Memorandum. Therefore, absent separate approval of
a waiver, JCET events may not be conducted with any unit of a foreign security force if the
Secretary of Defense has credible information that the unit has committed a gross violation of
human rights. Prior to approval of a JCET event, favorable vetting of the participating foreign
units for gross violations of human rights (“Leahy vetting”) must be completed. Vetting
individual foreign participants is required under circumstances specified in the Department of
State (DOS) Leahy Vetting Guide.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ASD(SO/LIC).

The ASD(SO/LIC):

   a. Approves all training activities pursuant to Section 322 of Title 10, U.S.C., in
coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense for Policy (USD(P)) and the CJCS.

  b. Reviews expenditures for JCET events by Commander, United States Special Operations
Command (CDRUSSOCOM), including:

        (1) Expenses of training U.S. SOF in conjunction with training, and training with armed
forces and other security forces of a friendly foreign country.

       (2) Expenses of deploying U.S. SOF for JCET events.

       (3) In the case of training in conjunction with a friendly developing country, the
incremental expenses incurred by that country as a direct result of such training.

   c. Monitors compliance with this issuance.

    d. Provides oversight of the training program established to train U.S. SOF in mission-
essential tasks.

   e. Prior to approving a proposed JCET:

       (1) Coordinates with the General Counsel of the Department of Defense for legal review
of proposed JCET events.

        (2) Coordinates with the USD(P) to ensure the appropriate regional desks provide a
policy review.

       (3) Coordinates with the DOS.

    f. Not later than 30 days after the end of each calendar quarter, submits a report on the
obligation and expenditure of funds for JCET activities to the Director, Defense Security
Cooperation Agency.


2.2. USD(P).

In coordination with the ASD(SO/LIC), the USD(P) conducts a policy review for all proposed
JCET events before their approval to confirm that the proposed training is consistent with U.S.
defense policy goals and DoD guidance.

2.3. GENERAL COUNSEL OF THE DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE.

In coordination with the ASD(SO/LIC), the General Counsel of the Department of Defense
provides legal reviews for all proposed JCET events before their approval.


2.4. CJCS.

The CJCS ensures the policies and procedures for global force management provide for force
allocation to the Combatant Commanders with a Presidentially-designated physical area of
responsibility (AOR) in support of JCET activities.


2.5. COMBATANT COMMANDERS WITH A PRESIDENTIALLY-DESIGNATED
PHYSICAL AOR.

Combatant Commanders with a Presidentially-designated physical AOR will:

   a. Coordinate with CDRUSSOCOM on requirements and resourcing for JCET events.

   b. Execute JCET events conducted in their AOR in accordance with the Secretary of
Defense’s Global Force Management Implementation Guidance (GFMIG).

   c. Exercise operational control of U.S. SOF units executing a JCET event in their AOR.

   d. Establish processes to ensure JCET events are nested in theater strategy.

    e. Provide guidance to their respective theater special operations command regarding the
status-of-forces agreement or other relevant arrangements applicable to the locations in their
AOR where a JCET event will occur.


2.6. CDRUSSOCOM.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the ASD(SO/LIC) pursuant to Section 167(f) of
Title 10, U.S.C., the CDRUSSOCOM:

    a. Oversees and manages the execution of the JCET Program in accordance with this
instruction, the GFMIG, and CJCS Manual (CJCSM) 3130.06.

   b. Coordinates JCET events with the respective Combatant Commanders with
Presidentially-designated physical AORs.

   c. Identifies, deploys, and transfers forces to Combatant Commands to conduct approved
JCET events.

   d. Authorizes obligations and expenditures for any of the following expenses:

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. JCET PROGRAM.

    a. The JCET program is a training program modeled after the Joint Training System, as
outlined in CJCSM 3500.03. It is unique in that its requirements are based on U.S. SOF mission-
essential task lists. The individual events are shaped by CDRUSSOCOM’s priorities and the
theater strategies established by the Combatant Commanders with a Presidentially-designated
physical AOR. They are planned in coordination with relevant U.S. Embassy country teams.

    b. In accordance with the GFMIG, JCET events are for U.S. SOF readiness training and are
not considered operational deployments. Therefore, JCET events are not subject to the
operational deployment-to-dwell and mobility-to-dwell limits and goals specified in the GFMIG.

    c. The purpose of the JCET program is to train U.S. SOF in mission essential tasks alongside
foreign forces in the foreign forces’ indigenous environments. The JCET program enables U.S.
SOF to build its capability to conduct combined operations in unfamiliar environments, develop
language skills, and develop familiarity with host nation geography and culture. As an important
complementary effect, JCET events support the strategies of Combatant Commanders with a
Presidentially-designated physical AOR.

  d. JCET events are short duration readiness training and, absent an exception approved by
ASD(SO/LIC), must take place outside the United States.

    e. U.S. payment of incremental expenses incurred by an eligible foreign country during
JCET events must not include any form of lethal assistance. Payment for training ammunition
may be authorized only as necessary for training purposes. Ammunition not consumed during
the training will not be transferred to a host nation. Unexpended training ammunition may
remain in country only if all these conditions are met:

       (1) An approved follow-on JCET event exists.

       (2) Service-specific hand receipt procedures are implemented.

        (3) U.S. Government personnel maintain appropriate security, accountability, and control
of the ammunition until it is either expended in training or transferred to a U.S. military unit or
installation for use in future JCET events.

   f. Combatant Commands with a Presidentially-designated physical AOR may re-mission
U.S. SOF temporarily deployed for JCET events in the Combatant Commander’s AOR in
accordance with GFMIG requirements.

3.2. PLANNING.

    a. The planning and prioritization for JCET activities by CDRUSSOCOM and other
Combatant Commanders should reflect the long-term strategies for regional and functional
objectives identified in the National Security Strategy, National Defense Strategy, National
Military Strategy, and the Joint Strategic Campaign Plan. These references guide U.S. SOF
training priorities and shape decisions about the scope, substance, and selection of partner
nations for JCET activities executed each fiscal year.

   b. CDRUSSOCOM uses the Global Special Operations Synchronization process to prioritize
proposed JCET activities. This process uses standard methods, including global force
management allocation, to identify force requirements and sourcing solutions for JCET
deployments.

   c. The Secretary of Defense issues JCET force deployment guidance in Section IV of the
GFMIG. The CJCS issues allocation direction in CJCSM 3130.06. Enclosure C of
CJCSM 3130.06 contains additional JCET deployment policies and procedures. The
ASD(SO/LIC) acts as the Secretary of Defense’s designee for purposes of the Secretary of
Defense approval referred to in CJCSM 3130.06.


3.3. REPORTS.

   a. Quarterly.

   Not later than 30 days after the end of each calendar quarter, CDRUSSOCOM submits a
report to the ASD(SO/LIC) on the obligation and expenditure of funds for JCET activities. The
ASD(SO/LIC) forwards the report to Director, Defense Security Cooperation Agency, and
provides an informational copy to the CJCS.

   b. Annually.

       (1) Not later than February 1 of each year, CDRUSSOCOM will submit to the
ASD(SO/LIC), via the Joint Staff Deputy Directorate for Special Operations (JS J-37), a report
regarding JCET training events during the preceding fiscal year for which expenses were paid.
Each report specifies:

           (a) All countries in which the training was conducted.

           (b) The type of training conducted, including whether such training was related to
counternarcotics or counterterrorism activities, the duration of that training, the number of
members of the armed forces involved, and expenses paid.

             (c) The extent of participation by foreign forces, including the numbers and service
affiliation of foreign personnel involved and physical and financial contribution of each host
nation (or in the case of multilateral activities, of all nations) to the training effort.

3.4. FUNDING AND ACCOUNTING PROCEDURES.

    a. The CDRUSSOCOM will budget for and execute the funding for all JCET expenses. This
is primarily executed with Major Force Program-11 operation and maintenance funds used for
organizing, training, and equipping forces to conduct special operations missions. Other types
of funding may be authorized in conjunction with Major Force Program-11 to execute the JCET
program.

    b. Funds expended to facilitate the participation of friendly developing country armed forces
and other security forces in training with U.S. SOF must be separately accumulated and
identified. These funds are used only for the incremental expenses incurred by that country as a
direct result of such training.

3.5. JCET APPROVAL PROCESS.

   a. The ASD(SO/LIC) approves all JCET events before execution. The ASD(SO/LIC)
considers, among other things:

        (1) The appropriateness of military-to-military training activities in light of broader
policy goals.

       (2) JCETs require vetting for gross violations of human rights (“Leahy vetting”) in
accordance with Section 362 of Title 10, U.S.C., and applicable DoD directives, policies,
procedures (see Paragraph 3.5.f. for guidance)

       (3) The appropriateness of certain JCET activities in regard to training objectives for the
designated host country forces and the balance of lethal and non-lethal training activities
considering the host nation’s level of military capacity.

  b. Before JCET deployments, the CDRUSSOCOM coordinates with the CJCS when the
CDRUSSOCOM relinquishes operational control to the gaining Combatant Commanders.

   c. The CDRUSSOCOM submits a notification message to the ASD(SO/LIC) and the
Director, JS J-37, a minimum of 35 days before a proposed JCET deployment date.

        (1) The Director, JS J-37, and the ASD(SO/LIC) conduct the appropriate level staffing
for the ASD(SO/LIC) to approve an action memorandum authorizing the execution of a JCET
event.

     (2) Execution authority is contingent on ASD(SO/LIC) approval via an action
memorandum provided to the Director, JS J-37, and the CDRUSSOCOM.

       (3) Execution of the event is conducted in accordance with the action memorandum.

   d. Once a JCET is approved, changes to host nation units or number of host nation personnel
require a modification to the action memorandum to ensure policy review and confirmation of
human rights verification for the additional host nation units and personnel.

   e. The CDRUSSOCOM is authorized to make other changes as detailed in the GFMIG.

   f. Human Rights Verification.

       (1) Section 362 of Title 10, U.S.C. prohibits the DoD from using funds for any training,
equipping, or other assistance to any unit of a foreign security force if the Secretary of Defense
has credible information that such unit has committed a gross violation of human rights. The

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

TERM                   DEFINITION

incremental expenses   In accordance with Section 322 of Title 10, U.S.C. and Chapter 15,
                       Section 1.2.3 of the DoD Financial Management Regulation, the
                       reasonable and proper cost of rations, fuel, training ammunition, and
                       transportation (except from one country to another country), and
                       other goods and services consumed by the developing country
                       participating in which the JCET is occurring. The term does not
                       include pay, allowances, and other normal costs of the developing
                       country’s personnel. The term does not include any form of lethal
                       aid.

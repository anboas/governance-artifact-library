                            DOD INSTRUCTION 5410.01
         RELEASE OF INFORMATION CONCERNING INCIDENTS
        INVOLVING DOD PERSONNEL OR SENIOR PERSONNEL

Originating Component:       Office of the Assistant to the Secretary of Defense for Public Affairs

Effective:                   September 5, 2024

Releasability:               Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                             at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Reissues and Cancels:        DoD Instruction 5410.01, “Release of Information Concerning Accidents
                             Involving Military Personnel or Equipment or Concerning Senior
                             Personnel,” June 2, 2009

Approved by:                 Chris Meagher, Assistant to the Secretary of Defense for Public Affairs


Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive (DoDD) 5122.05, this issuance establishes
policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for the release of information concerning
deceased, injured, or missing DoD military and civilian personnel and senior U.S. and foreign
government personnel involved in incidents while on a DoD installation or being transported by military
vehicle.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

   a. This issuance applies to:

        (1) OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of
Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the
Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

        (2) The release of information concerning incidents involving deceased, injured, or
missing DoD military and civilian personnel and senior U.S. and foreign government personnel
occurring while they are on a DoD installation or being transported by military vehicle, whether
inside or outside of the United States, its territories, or its possessions.

   b. This issuance does not apply to military incidents involving nuclear weapons; that
guidance is found in DoD Instruction (DoDI) 5230.16.


1.2. POLICY.

   a. Release of information concerning deceased, injured, or missing senior U.S. and foreign
government personnel must be carefully coordinated due to national and international interest
and security implications.

   b. DoD public affairs (PA) personnel will provide maximum cooperation as well as timely
and accurate information to news media representatives and the public, consistent with national
security responsibilities and while protecting the privacy of the personnel and their families in
accordance with DoDD 5122.05.

    c. Release of information concerning senior U.S. and foreign government personnel when
they become deceased, injured, or missing while on a DoD installation or being transported by
military vehicle will be directed to the offices designated in this issuance for public
announcement.

    d. For incidents involving DoD personnel occurring outside the United States, including its
territories and possessions, additional considerations may be found in applicable treaties,
intergovernmental agreements, and laws of other nations. Additional coordination with the U.S.
Embassy or Consulate concerned may be required, including to provide notification to the host
government.

    e. Information concerning deceased, injured, or missing DoD military or civilian personnel
will be released to the media or public in accordance with DoDI 1300.18.

    f. Information related to the execution of this issuance will be managed in accordance with
applicable records management, operations security, and information security policies, including
but not limited to DoDD 5205.02E, DoDI 5015.02, and DoD Manual 5200.01. The creation,
collection, use, processing, storage, maintenance, dissemination, and disclosure of personally
identifiable information will comply with the requirements of applicable privacy policies,
including but not limited to DoDI 5400.11 and DoD 5400.11-R.

     g. Nothing in this issuance is intended to contradict guidance provided in DoDI 6055.07 to
protect privileged safety information from unauthorized release. DoDI 6055.07 has precedence
if a contradiction is discovered.

                            SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ASSISTANT TO THE SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PUBLIC AFFAIRS
(ATSD(PA)).

The ATSD(PA):

    a. Approves and synchronizes the release of information to news media acknowledging
senior DoD and other U.S. Government personnel who become deceased, injured, or missing
because of an incident while on a DoD installation or being transported by military vehicle.
Except as otherwise provided in Paragraph 3.1., such personnel include:

       (1) U.S. Senators other than the Speaker Pro Tempore of the Senate.

       (2) Members of the U.S. House of Representatives other than the Speaker of the House
of Representatives.

       (3) Secretaries of the Military Departments.

      (4) The Deputy Secretary of Defense; Principal Staff Assistants; and Presidentially
Appointed, Senate-confirmed officials assigned to OSD.

        (5) The Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, the Vice Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of
Staff, and general officers or flag officers in the grade of O-9 or above assigned to the Joint Staff.

       (6) The Chief of Staff, U.S. Army.

       (7) The Chief of Naval Operations.

       (8) The Chief of Staff, U.S. Air Force.

       (9) The Commandant of the U.S. Marine Corps.

       (10) The Chief of Space Operations.

       (11) The Chief of the National Guard Bureau.

       (12) Combatant Commanders.

       (13) Directors of the Defense Agencies and DoD Field Activities.

    b. Releases information acknowledging all deceased, injured, or missing DoD military and
civilian personnel of any rank resulting from incidents involving combat, hostile act(s), or a
named military operation (i.e., not only those incurred from a hostile act), unless delegated
through approved PA guidance in accordance with DoDI 5405.03.

2.2. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS.

In accordance with their authority in DoDD 5124.02, the Under Secretary of Defense for
Personnel and Readiness:

    a. Ensures the policy and procedures in DoDI 6055.07 regarding release of information
concerning mishap notification, investigation, reporting, and record keeping are consistent with
this issuance.

    b. Ensures the policy and procedures in DoDI 1300.18 on the release of information to news
media or the public regarding deceased, injured or missing DoD personnel are consistent with
this issuance.


2.3. ASSISTANT TO THE SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PRIVACY, CIVIL
LIBERTIES, AND TRANSPARENCY.

The Assistant to the Secretary of Defense for Privacy, Civil Liberties, and Transparency provides
advice and guidance for the implementation of this issuance and adequate application of
protections for privacy and civil liberties in accordance with DoDI 5400.11 and DoD 5400.11-R.


2.4. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments:

    a. Approve the release of information acknowledging deceased, injured, or missing DoD
military and civilian personnel under the provisions of their respective Department regulations
only when:

       (1) The personnel are not identified in Paragraphs 2.1.a.(1) through 2.1.a.(13).

         (2) The death, injury, or missing status of the personnel is not a result of combat, hostile
act(s), or a named operation, or the ATSD(PA) has delegated release authority in accordance
with Paragraph 2.1.b.

    b. Must inform the Defense Press Operations Directorate in the Office of the ATSD(PA)
before the release of information acknowledging deceased, injured, or missing U.S. military
officers in the grade of O-9 and above and DoD civilian personnel of equivalent rank meeting the
criteria of Paragraphs 2.4.a.(1) and 2.4.a.(2)

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. INFORMATION RELEASED BY THE WHITE HOUSE.

The White House has the sole approval authority for release of information regarding the
following personnel if they die or are injured or missing because of an incident while on a DoD
installation or being transported by military vehicle:

   a. The President of the United States.

   b. The Vice President of the United States.

   c. The President Pro Tempore of the Senate.

   d. The Speaker of the House of Representatives.

   e. Members of the Cabinet of the United States.

     f. Foreign visitors of political or royal rank equivalent to the U.S. Government officials
listed in Paragraphs 3.1.a. through 3.1.e.

    g. Members of foreign governments below the rank of the preceding category who are guests
of the U.S. Government.


3.2. INCIDENTS OCCURRING OUTSIDE OF DOD INSTALLATIONS.

DoD PA personnel at the scene of incidents covered by this issuance, in coordination with the
on-scene commander, will:

   a. Inform media representatives of the potential presence of information that, if published,
could cause damage to national security or harm U.S. Government personnel or ongoing
operations and ask them to cooperate in its protection.

    b. Inform media representatives and DoD personnel of the sensitivity of using visual
information (VI) that identifies deceased or injured personnel. Inform photographers that
Federal criminal statutes prohibit photographing classified national security information.

    c. Stress the importance of protecting such information if media representatives refuse to
cooperate in the protection of classified national security information. Request the cooperation
of the superiors of the media representatives, informing them that publication of such classified
national security information or refusal to return it to military authority may constitute a
violation of Federal law, including but not limited to Sections 793(e) and 795 through 797 of
Title 18, United States Code.

    d. Submit a report concerning suspected refusals of media representatives or their superiors
to cooperate, as described in Paragraph 3.2.c., to the ATSD(PA) through command PA channels.

    e. Provide official DoD photographers and videographers with access to the scene to record
VI to use during safety or legal investigations of the incident. VI must be initially identified as
“not cleared for public release” and will be reviewed by the appropriate PA officer before public
release, in accordance with DoDD 5122.05 and DoDI 1300.18 and as outlined in Section 2 and
Paragraph 3.1. of this issuance. The following outlines the release of VI of deceased or injured
personnel:

       (1) When it is possible for the personnel to be clearly identified, VI will not be released.

       (2) When it is not possible for the personnel to be identified, VI may be released;
however, information concerning the time or circumstances of the incident will only be released
in general terms (e.g., a traffic accident that happened in the morning).

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 Cabinet of the United Principal advisory body to the President. Members are appointed by
 States                the President and usually include the Vice President and the heads of
                       Executive Branch Departments. The President also may designate
                       heads of other agencies and members of the Executive Office of the
                       President as members.

 DoD installation          Defined in DoDI 1000.15.

 incident                  An occurrence, caused by either human action or natural phenomena,
                           that has resulted or may result in deceased, injured, or missing
                           personnel or loss of or other risks to property, such as DoD
                           equipment, information, or natural resources.

 military vehicle          Any mode of transportation that is owned, rented, or leased by a DoD
                           Component and operated by DoD personnel including, but not
                           limited to, passenger cars, trucks, buses, trains, watercraft, aircraft,
                           and combat and tactical vehicles.

 on-scene commander        The U.S. military authority in the immediate vicinity of an isolating
                           event who temporarily assumes command of the incident.

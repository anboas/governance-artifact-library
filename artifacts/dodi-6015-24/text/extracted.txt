               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments (MILDEPS), the Office of the Chairman
of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD.


1.2. POLICY.

It is DoD policy that:

   a. The MILDEPS maintain the medical capabilities to support wartime and related
operational missions. The MILDEPS develop specialty-trained physicians ready to support
wartime and related operational missions and force generation.

    b. GME programs are prioritized, reviewed, restructured, and realigned, as needed, to meet
the operational medical force requirements of the MILDEPS.

    c. The DoD GME Program provides military physicians with didactic and clinical education
to prepare them for independent practice in a medical specialty or subspecialty. These programs
must meet the standards of the appropriate civilian accrediting body.

   d. MILDEPS have primary responsibility for the identification of operational medical force
requirements and the organizing of military medical personnel. MILDEPS determine the
number and medical specialty of military personnel to be trained in GME and reflect their
operational medical force requirements in annual GME training plans. GME programs are
conducted jointly and in military medical treatment facilities to the maximum extent practicable.

   e. A Military Health System-wide oversight process, promulgated through Defense Health
Agency (DHA) procedural instructions, ensures that GME programs prioritize the medical
capabilities to support wartime and related operational missions of the MILDEPS and,
additionally, support the medical readiness of the Armed Forces.

                          SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS
(USD(P&R)).

The USD(P&R) has overall responsibility for the DoD GME Program.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR HEALTH AFFAIRS (ASD(HA)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R), the ASD(HA):

   a. Provides oversight for the DoD GME Program.

   b. Provides appropriate policy recommendations to the USD(P&R).


2.3. DIRECTOR, DHA.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R), through the ASD(HA), the
Director, DHA:

    a. Is responsible for policy, procedures, and direction of the DoD GME Program and GME
in military medical treatment facilities.

   b. Issues procedural guidance for the implementation of this issuance.

   c. Establishes procedures to ensure that GME programs are conducted jointly, to the extent
practical, and focused on operational medical force requirements.

   d. Coordinates with the Secretaries of the MILDEPS to assign Service members to GME
programs, including faculty, support staff, and trainees necessary to operate GME programs.

    e. Provides sufficient resources, including personnel to augment the personnel provided by
the MILDEPS, as required, to maintain operational readiness and GME program accreditation or
certification.

   f. Establishes policies and procedures to enter into Federal and non-Federal partnerships and
agreements to fulfill civilian accrediting body and GME operational requirements.

  g. In conjunction with the Secretaries of the MILDEPS establishes and conducts the Joint
GME Selection Board annually.

    h. Conducts a review of GME programs at least once every 2 years and provides a report to
the ASD(HA), through the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Health Services Policy and
Oversight, with an assessment of the DoD GME program and recommendations for
improvement.

2.4. PRESIDENT, UNIFORMED SERVICES UNIVERSITY OF THE HEALTH
SCIENCES.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the ASD(HA), the President, Uniformed Services
University of the Health Sciences:

  a. Provides advice and recommendations concerning the DoD GME Program to the
ASD(HA), in coordination with the Services’ GME representatives.

    b. Develops and implements overall procedures for operation and oversight of Uniformed
Services University support to GME programs, including, but not limited to, strengthening the
teaching skills of GME program directors and faculty throughout the Military Health System.


2.5. SECRETARIES OF THE MILDEPS.

The Secretaries of the MILDEPS, in coordination with the Director, DHA, and through the
MILDEPS’ Surgeons General:

   a. Annually determine the required GME positions by type and number required to meet
operational medical force requirements.

    b. For approved GME programs, select trainees and assignments based on the annual
training plan, listing the number and types of each specialty of physician to be trained, as
determined by operational medical force requirements.

    c. Provide Service members, to include GME faculty, clinical support staff, and trainees,
through assignment of medical personnel with duty at GME medical treatment facilities.

   d. Provide necessary resources to the Director, DHA, to support MILDEP-specific
operational readiness training requirements beyond GME accreditation requirements when these
requirements are managed by DHA.

   e. Comply with procedural instructions published by the Director, DHA.

                        SECTION 3: SELECTION PROCESS
3.1. MILDEPS will submit their GME training plans, which will be based on the operational
medical force requirements of the MILDEPS and listed by specialty and number, for the Fiscal
Year Defense Program annually to DHA by 1 July each year.

3.2. DHA will consolidate these inputs from the MILDEPs, and, in conjunction with the
MILDEPS’ Surgeons General, will size the DoD GME program to assure adequate training
opportunities either within the Military Health System or with civilian GME programs.

3.3. During the annual Joint GME Selection Board, the MILDEPs’ Surgeons General will select
applicants for GME positions in accordance with the annual training plan.

3.4. Alterations in the numbers and mix of specialties is permitted during the board meeting, but
the MILDEPS and the USD(P&R) will be briefed on any changes to the numbers and mix of
specialties made by the board.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 annual training plan      A plan made annually by each Military Department listing the
                           number and types of each specialty of physician to be trained.

 GME                       Didactic and clinical education in a medical specialty or subspecialty
                           that follows the completion of undergraduate medical education and
                           prepares physicians for the independent practice of medicine in that
                           specialty or subspecialty. This education is also referred to as
                           residency or fellowship education. Completion of this education
                           typically results in board eligibility and certification by the national
                           accrediting body for that specialty or subspecialty. This definition
                           also includes other physician graduate professional education in
                           clinical settings that incur an active duty service obligation.

 graduate                  Defined in DoD Instruction 6000.13
 professional
 education

 operational medical       The required military medical personnel identified by the MILDEPS
 force requirements        for their wartime and related operational missions. This includes
                           physician requirements by specialty, and contributes to the military
                           medical personnel requirements necessary to meet the operational
                           medical force readiness requirements.

                              DOD DIRECTIVE 1000.33E
     DOD EXECUTIVE AGENT FOR SPECIFIED SUPPORT TO
  SCOUTING ORGANIZATIONS AND THE AMERICAN RED CROSS

Originating Component:       Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness

Effective:                   September 28, 2022

Releasability:               Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                             at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Incorporates and Cancels: Paragraphs 1.4.2-1.4.3 and 5.2 of DoD Directive 1000.26E, “Support for
                          Non-Federal Entities Authorized to Operate on DoD Installations,”
                          February 2, 2007

Approved by:                 Kathleen H. Hicks, Deputy Secretary of Defense


Purpose: This issuance:
• Establishes policies and assigns responsibilities for specified support to the Boy Scouts of America
and the Girl Scouts of the United States of America (referred to in this issuance collectively as
“Scouting Organizations”), and the American Red Cross.
• Designates the Secretary of the Army as the DoD Executive Agent (EA) for Specified Support to
Scouting Organizations and the American Red Cross in accordance with DoD Directive
(DoDD) 5101.01.
• Designates the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness (USD(P&R)) as the
Principal Staff Assistant overseeing the activities of the DoD EA for Specified Support to Scouting
Organizations and the American Red Cross in accordance with DoDD 5101.01.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD.


1.2. POLICY.

The DoD may provide logistical support services to Scouting Organizations and the American
Red Cross operations taking place on DoD installations and operational areas overseas, as
appropriate.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. USD(P&R).

The USD(P&R):

   a. Serves as the Principal Staff Assistant overseeing the activities of the DoD EA for
Specified Support to Scouting Organizations and the American Red Cross in accordance with
DoDD 5101.01.

    b. Establishes DoD policy and guidance for Specified Support to Scouting Organizations and
the American Red Cross on DoD installations in accordance with DoDD 5124.02.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R), the Assistant Secretary of Defense
for Manpower and Reserve Affairs:

   a. Develops policy for Specified Support to Scouting Organizations and the American Red
Cross on DoD installations.

   b. Oversees and provides guidance for implementing policies to support Specified Support to
Scouting Organizations and the American Red Cross that operate on DoD installations overseas.


2.3. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND CHAIRMAN OF THE
JOINT CHIEFS OF STAFF.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff:

  a. Coordinate with the DoD EA for Specified Support to Scouting Organizations and the
American Red Cross on:

      (1) Overseas assignments of professional staff from Scouting Organizations and the
American Red Cross.

      (2) Professional staff visits from Scouting Organizations and the American Red Cross to
DoD installations overseas,

   b. Designate a representative to coordinate with the DoD EA for Specified Support to
Scouting Organizations and the American Red Cross.

2.4. SECRETARY OF THE ARMY.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., and in their capacity as the DoD EA for
Specified Support to Scouting Organizations and the American Red Cross, the Secretary of the
Army:

    a. Coordinates support to Scouting Organizations in accordance with DoD
Instruction 1015.09 and this issuance.

   b. Performs an annual audit of American Red Cross accounts and prepares and submits the
annual report to Congress in accordance with Section 300110 of Title 36, United States Code,
and this issuance.

   c. Facilitates deployment, administrative, and logistical support services to eligible
American Red Cross professional staff traveling or assigned to DoD installations overseas and
operational areas in accordance with DoD Instruction 1000.15, the March 10, 2009
Memorandum of Understanding between the American Red Cross and DoD, and this issuance.
This includes:

        (1) Initiating and funding pre-assignment background investigations for American Red
Cross employees and prospective employees being considered for assignment to DoD
installations overseas and operational areas.

        (2) Providing necessary in-processing support for the American Red Cross professional
staff assigned to DoD installations overseas and those deploying to operational areas. The U.S.
Army will:

           (a) Cover initial costs, except those paid by the American Red Cross.

         (b) Be reimbursed, upon its request, by the entity directly benefiting from the
American Red Cross support.

   d. Designate a point of contact to coordinate matters regarding the DoD EA responsibilities,
functions, and authorities.

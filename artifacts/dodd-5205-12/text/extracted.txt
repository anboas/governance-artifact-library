               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands (CCMDs), the Office of Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. DoD intelligence, counterintelligence, and other intelligence-related activities will be
planned, programmed, and funded through either:

       (1) The MIP as established in this issuance;

        (2) The National Intelligence Program (NIP) as defined in Executive Order
(E.O.) 12333; or

       (3) Any other appropriate budget program or process involving intelligence,
counterintelligence, or intelligence-related activities.

    b. MIP capabilities, resources, and issues will be reviewed and assessed by the Defense
Intelligence and Security Integration Council or other oversight organizations as directed by the
Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (USD(I&S)).

     c. The MIP Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution (PPBE) decisions will reflect
all relevant elements of guidance documents issued by the:

       (1) Congress.

       (2) Office of Management and Budget.

       (3) Office of the Director of National Intelligence (DNI).

       (4) OSD.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. USD(I&S).

The USD(I&S):

   a. Serves as the MIP Executive.

    b. Provides MIP governance and oversight for DoD intelligence, counterintelligence, and
intelligence-related activities through a structured business process.

  c. Issues planning guidance for MIP activities according to the priorities established by the
DoD with input from the Office of the DNI.

   d. Oversees the development of the portion of the President’s Budget request utilizing MIP
program element codes and DoD special program code identifiers 296, 355, 365, and 398 in
accordance with DoD 7000.14-R and DoD Directives (DoDDs) 7045.14 and 7045.20 in
coordination with the:

      (1) Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of
Defense.

       (2) Under Secretary of Defense for Policy.

       (3) Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness.

       (4) Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff.

       (5) Director of Cost Assessment and Program Evaluation.

       (6) Other DoD Components heads, as appropriate.

       (7) DNI.

   e. Issues MIP resource PPBE procedures and associated timelines to MIP Component
Managers, in coordination with the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial
Officer, Department of Defense; the Under Secretary of Defense for Policy; and the Director of
Cost Assessment and Program Evaluation, as appropriate, to include:

       (1) Annual MIP program and budget development.

       (2) Other related requirements and activities in accordance with Paragraph 2.1.d.

       (3) Any reprogramming requests in accordance with Paragraphs 2.1.h.-i.

   f. Publishes the MIP Congressional Justification Book that consolidates annual MIP budget
and prior supplemental request submissions from all MIP Components to present and defend the
annual MIP budget request to Congress.

   g. Coordinates the MIP Congressional Justification Book MIP/NIP Collective Investment
Exhibit with DNI.

    h. Certifies that DoD intelligence, counterintelligence, and intelligence-related activities of
the MIP complement and are compatible with NIP activities, including coordination and
implementation across DoD and DNI enterprises and forums.

    i. Approves proposed resource changes affecting MIP capabilities, programs, and projects
throughout the PPBE process in coordination with Under Secretary of Defense
(Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of Defense and the Director of Cost
Assessment and Program Evaluation.

    j. Reviews and coordinates MIP program or project adjustments or realignments with the
Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of Defense and
the Director of Cost Assessment and Program Evaluation that:

        (1) Exceed reprogramming thresholds directed by Congress in guidance accompanying
the relevant appropriations act; or

       (2) Exceed reprogramming thresholds established in DoD 7000.14-R.

   k. Oversees MIP resource classification compliance in accordance with DoD
Manual 5200.01 and DoD Financial Management Sensitive Activities Security Classification
Guidance.

   l. Serves as the MIP Component Manager for the OSD, with responsibilities as described in
Section 3 of this issuance.


2.2. DIRECTOR, DEFENSE INTELLIGENCE AGENCY.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(I&S) and in addition to the
responsibilities in Paragraph 2.3., the Director, Defense Intelligence Agency, as the resource
sponsor for CCMD intelligence directorates and joint intelligence operations centers:

    a. Prepares programmatic guidance and oversees annual program budget development for
CCMD intelligence directorates and joint intelligence operations centers capabilities and
activities.

    b. Coordinates annual budget requests for MIP resources with representatives from the Joint
Staff, the MIP Service and Agency Components, the CCMD intelligence directorates, and the
joint intelligence operations centers and related intelligence activities with the Combatant
Commanders, consistent with the Combatant Commanders’ responsibilities in Section 164 of
Title 10, United States Code.

    c. Coordinates proposed realignment of MIP resources supporting CCMD intelligence
directorates and the joint intelligence operations centers and related intelligence activities with

2.3. DIRECTORS OF DEFENSE COUNTERINTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY
AGENCY; DEFENSE INFORMATION SYSTEMS AGENCY; DEFENSE
INTELLIGENCE AGENCY; DEFENSE THREAT REDUCTION AGENCY; NATIONAL
GEOSPATIAL-INTELLIGENCE AGENCY; NATIONAL RECONNAISSANCE
OFFICE; AND NATIONAL SECURITY AGENCY/CENTRAL SECURITY SERVICE.

Under the authority, direction, and control of their respective Principal Staff Assistants, the
Directors of the Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency; Defense Information Systems
Agency; Defense Intelligence Agency; Defense Threat Reduction Agency; National Geospatial-
Intelligence Agency; National Reconnaissance Office; and National Security Agency/Central
Security Service serve as the MIP Component Manager for their respective organization. Any
delegation of this responsibility must be transmitted to the USD(I&S).


2.4. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments:

    a. In coordination with the Military Service’s Senior Intelligence Officer, designates, and
transmits notice of such a designation in a memorandum to the USD(I&S) the individual Service
MIP Component Manager(s).

   b. Implement and oversee MIP management in accordance with Section 3.

    c. Prepare and submit annual program budget request submissions for CCMD and sub-
unified commands headquarters MIP-related resources and requirements in accordance with
DoD 7000.14-R and DoDD 5100.03.


2.5. COMMANDER, UNITED STATES CYBER COMMAND.

The Commander, United States Cyber Command:

   a. In coordination with their Senior Intelligence Officer, designates and transmits notice of
such a designation in a memorandum to the USD(I&S), United States Cyber Command’s MIP
Component Manager.

   b. Oversees the Command’s MIP Component Manager.

   c. Prepares programmatic guidance and oversees annual program budget development and
submissions for the MIP portion of United States Cyber Command capabilities and activities in
coordination with the Secretaries of the Military Departments and the other Combatant
Commanders in accordance with DoD 7000.14-R and DoDD 7045.14.

2.6. COMMANDER, UNITED STATES SPECIAL OPERATIONS COMMAND.

The Commander, United States Special Operations Command:

   a. In coordination with their Senior Intelligence Officer, designates and transmits notice of
such designation in a memorandum to the USD(I&S), United States Special Operations
Command’s MIP Component Manager.

   b. Oversees the Command’s MIP Component Manager.

   c. Prepares programmatic guidance and oversees annual program budget development and
submissions for the MIP portion of special operations-peculiar support within Major Force
Program 11 for United States Special Operations Command, Theater Special Operations
Command Headquarters, and other joint special operations in accordance with DoD 7000.14-R
and DoDD 7045.14.

                   SECTION 3: MIP COMPONENT MANAGERS
Within their respective Components, MIP Component Managers:

   a. Implement MIP policy and manage MIP processes.

   b. Respond to guidance from the MIP Executive.

   c. Identify, designate, account for, and report all MIP resources for intelligence,
counterintelligence, and intelligence-related activities in organizational databases to the MIP
Executive throughout all phases of the PPBE process in accordance with DoD 7000.14-R.

   d. Coordinate all proposed MIP resource realignments with the MIP Executive in accordance
with Paragraphs 2.1.e. and 2.1.i-k.

    e. Identify and recommend the addition or removal of MIP programs, functions, and
activities to the MIP Executive in accordance with Paragraph 2.1.h.

    f. Coordinate and submit to the MIP Executive annual MIP budget requests supporting
doctrine, organization, training, materiel, leadership and education, personnel and readiness, and
facilities-assigned tasks as they pertain to the MIP programs.

   g. Serve as members of the Defense Intelligence and Security Integration Council when
appointed by their MIP Component’s Senior Intelligence Officer.

TERM                   DEFINITION

major force program    An aggregation of program elements that reflects a force or support
                       mission for the DoD and contains the resources necessary to achieve
                       an objective or plan. The major force program is contained in the
                       context of the Future Years Defense Program. It reflects fiscal time-
                       phasing of mission objectives to be accomplished and the means
                       proposed for their accomplishment. Major Force Program 11 is
                       specific to DoD special operations forces and activities.

MIP                    Programs, projects, or activities that support the Secretary of
                       Defense’s intelligence, counterintelligence, and related intelligence
                       responsibilities. Includes those intelligence and counterintelligence
                       programs, project and activities that provide capabilities to meet
                       warfighters’ operational and tactical requirements more effectively.
                       Excludes capabilities associated with a weapon system whose
                       primary mission is not intelligence.

MIP Components         Organizations within the DoD to include OSD, the Military
                       Departments, CCMDs, Defense Agencies, utilizing MIP program
                       element codes and resources identified by Under Secretary of
                       Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department of
                       Defense special program codes 296, 355, 365, and 398.

NIP                    Defined in E.O.12333.

relevant elements of   Examples of authoritative program and fiscal guidance documents
guidance documents     informing PPBE decisions. Examples include: congressional
                       legislation and associated committee mark-ups or directed actions,
                       Office of Management and Budget Passback, DNI decision
                       documents, and OSD program decision memorandums or program
                       budget decisions.

special operations-    Equipment, material, supplies, and services required for special
peculiar               operations missions for which there is no Service-common
                       requirement.

                 SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance:

    a. Applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs
of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands (CCMDs), the Office of Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

   b. Applies to use of DoD transportation assets for PA purposes with other Federal
departments or agencies and with foreign governments.

   c. Does not apply to:

       (1) Use of DoD transportation assets for events or activities designed primarily to meet
Service recruiting or accession goals.

       (2) Defense support of civil authorities and qualifying entities pursuant to DoDD
3025.18 and related issuances, including DoD Instruction (DoDI) 3025.20.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. PA transportation, as defined in DoDI 4515.13, may be provided when such transportation
is determined to be primarily in the interest of the DoD to enable public understanding of DoD
activities, including:

        (1) PA travel in connection with public and media coverage of military operations and
exercises, military sponsored events, military installation visits, or other PA objectives that add
to the public understanding of DoD activities.

        (2) Transportation of non-Federal entity (NFE) passengers, a category that includes
media members, involved strictly in approved PA activities that are of primary interest to the
DoD in accordance with DoDD 5122.05. Activities may include community outreach programs,
tours, or other events in which a DoD Component is either a sponsor or a participant that
maintains lead for development (e.g., participant lists, agenda) and execution of any activity
using DoD transportation assets in accordance with Volumes 1 and 2 of DoDI 5410.19.

       (3) Transportation filmed as part of a DoD-sponsored entertainment media production in
accordance with DoDI 5410.16.

     (4) Transportation of individuals in support of approved PA activities arranged by any
DoD Component jointly with or at the request of another Federal agency or foreign government.

    b. PA air transportation must be in accordance with the air transportation guidelines in DoDI
4515.13. PA planners must confirm that commercial transportation is not available or is
inadequate to fulfill mission objectives. When NFE passengers travel on military aircraft, these
aircraft must be designed or configured for passenger transport. Exceptions are orientation
flights and flights in non-passenger aircraft that serve a direct PA purpose, such as NFE ride-
alongs, or tandem jumps with DoD jet demonstration or parachute teams in accordance with
DoDI 5410.19.

    c. PA transportation likely to garner national or international media interest must be
forwarded through command channels to the Office of the Assistant to the Secretary of Defense
for Public Affairs (ATSD(PA)) for awareness before the travel occurs.

   d. DoD may not contract commercial air transportation for media member travel.

   e. Records created or received pertaining to this issuance must be managed in accordance
with the applicable DoD Component Records Disposition Schedule.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ATSD(PA).

The ATSD(PA):

   a. Establishes policy and provides guidance regarding PA transportation.

   b. Reviews and approves PA transportation requests in accordance with DoDIs 4515.13 and
5410.16 and Volume 2 of DoDI 5410.19, for:

       (1) PA transportation arranged by any DoD Component with, or at the request of,
another Federal agency or foreign government.

         (2) Non-local PA transportation, unless delegated in accordance with Paragraphs 2.2,
2.3., 3.1.b.(1), and 3.1.b.(3)(b).

       (3) Intertheater PA transportation.


2.2. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND CJCS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the CJCS approve non-local PA transportation
that is pertinent to the mission and scope of their respective DoD Components in accordance
with Section 3.


2.3. COMBATANT COMMANDERS (CCDRS).

The CCDRs approve requests for PA transportation within their areas of responsibility (AORs)
in accordance with Section 3.

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. AUTHORITY.

   a. Local Travel.

        (1) Except when otherwise specified in this issuance or prohibited by applicable Military
Department policies, commanders at all levels are authorized to grant transportation approval for
local travel for PA purposes on DoD transportation assets under their operational control if that
travel is within the scope of the mission and responsibilities of their command, and all of the
following conditions apply:

           (a) The PA activity is not the responsibility of a higher authority.

            (b) The public interest in the PA activity is confined primarily to the mission or
vicinity of that command.

         (c) Transportation is provided for NFE passengers who are a part of an approved
component PA activity that is in the primary interest of the DoD.

        (2) Visits to ships at sea by NFE passengers for PA activities are considered local travel
for the purposes of this issuance if the visit is only to and from shore and ship. Commanders and
PA staffs must also adhere to any Service-specific guidance about embark and visit programs.

        (3) National Guard-sponsored NFE passenger travel to locations within a geographically
neighboring State that return to the point-of-origin or nearby location are considered local travel
for the purposes of this issuance if all of the following conditions are met:

            (a) The purpose of the travel is to demonstrate to NFE passengers the personnel,
aviation, facility, and other capabilities of the sponsoring and neighboring State National Guard
units.

           (b) The PA objective cannot be met without interstate travel.

         (c) Both States’ Adjutants General concur in the travel and the National Guard
Bureau PA office approves the travel.

      (4) Not all orientation flights for NFE passengers are considered PA activities.
DoDI 4515.13 must also be reviewed, as needed, for further guidance.

   b. Non-local Travel.

       (1) Delegated Approval Authority.

        The Secretaries of the Military Departments, the CJCS, and the CCDRs have the
authority to approve non-local PA transportation in accordance with Paragraphs 2.2. and 2.3.

            (a) Unless ATSD(PA) approval is required, the PA offices for each Military
Department may control and approve PA transportation by their respective units in accordance
with DoDI 4515.13. Military Departments that delegate approval authority for non-local PA
transportation, in accordance with Paragraphs 2.2. and 2.3., cannot delegate approval authority
lower than the major command level of the Military Service concerned.

            (b) The CJCS may delegate approval authority to the Director, Joint Staff.

            (c) The CCDRs cannot delegate approval authority below their Service component
heads.

         (2) Intratheater Travel.

        CCDRs or their designees are authorized to approve intratheater PA transportation in
accordance with Paragraph 2.3. and provide dedicated airlift or seats for NFE passengers. Both
of the following conditions must be met:

            (a) PA transportation is only provided aboard assets contracted or organic to U.S.
forces in the approving commander’s AOR. If a request involves transportation assets not
organic to the CCMD that are currently in the requesting CCMD’s AOR, the use of the asset
must be coordinated through and approved by the owning command even though the CCDR
approves the PA purpose. In accordance with Paragraph 1.2.d., the DoD may not contract
commercial air transportation assets for media member travel.

           (b) Dedicated DoD transportation assets are only provided when the CCDR
determines, on a case-by-case basis, that other means of transportation are not reasonably
available or adequate to allow timely media coverage of an operation or PA activity.

         (3) Retained Approval Authority for Intertheater Travel.

            (a) The ATSD(PA) approves intertheater PA transportation, except as noted in
Paragraph 3.1.b.(3)(b). The DoD Component sponsoring the intertheater travel coordinates
transportation with the United States Transportation Command, provided all of the following
conditions are met:

                1. The supported CCDR agrees.

                2. Media members have a PA escort.

               3. Documentation requirements prescribed by the DoD and, as applicable, the
countries involved, including valid identification and country clearances, are met. For more
information about DoD documentation requirements, see Paragraphs 3.7. and 4.8. of
DoDI 4515.13.

          (b) Travel involving more than one CCMD with a physical AOR will be approved by
the ATSD(PA). CCDRs may authorize PA transportation from the nation where their
headquarters is located to their AOR if their headquarters is located outside their AOR. For
example:

               1. United States Africa Command may authorize travel from Germany to Africa.

               2. United States Southern Command and United States Central Command may
authorize travel from the United States to their respective AORs.


3.2. REIMBURSEMENT.

    a. The approving authority may authorize PA transportation in accordance with Paragraphs
1.2.a.(1) through (4) on a non-reimbursable basis when:

       (1) Both of these conditions are met:

          (a) Commercial transportation is not available or is otherwise inadequate to fulfill
mission objectives, or the individual is in immediate danger of loss of life, limb, or sight.

           (b) Travel on DoD transportation assets is on a space-available, non-interference
basis on previously scheduled flights, to the maximum extent possible.

      (2) Use of DoD transportation assets is an integral part of media coverage or required to
accomplish a PA objective; or

      (3) The safety and security of DoD-accredited media member movement is unduly
compromised by not using DoD transportation assets.

    b. If the approving authority does not authorize PA transportation on a non-reimbursable
basis, then NFE passengers involved in approved PA activities are required to reimburse the
DoD for travel at the prevailing commercial rate (e.g., full coach fare) available to the public
between the day the travel was planned and the day the travel occurred.

       (1) The DoD Components must receive written agreement for reimbursement from
passengers before providing PA transportation.

       (2) Procedures for reimbursement are included in DoDD 4500.09 and Volumes 11A and
11B of DoD 7000.14-R.


3.3. AUTHORIZATION.

    a. DoD Components will not commit DoD transportation assets for PA transportation until
the request is coordinated and approved by the appropriate authority, in accordance with
Paragraphs 2.1.b.(1) through (3), 2.2., 2.3., 3.1.a., and 3.1.b., as applicable.

    b. All requests for PA transportation must be submitted to the appropriate approval authority
through the appropriate command channels. All Army and Air National Guard requests for non-
local PA transportation will be coordinated through National Guard Bureau PA before approval
by the applicable approval authority. In instances requiring ATSD(PA) approval, justification is
required to establish both the PA purpose to be served and the necessity for use of DoD
transportation assets in accordance with this issuance.

    c. Travel authorization (including invitational travel authorization, if applicable) is issued by
the sponsoring organization in accordance with Chapter 3 of the Joint Travel Regulations.

    d. When the approval authority determines passengers must provide reimbursement for
travel, the approval authority will also confirm passengers have required authorization and make
timely payment in accordance with the Joint Travel Regulations.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 AOR                       Defined in the DoD Dictionary of Military and Associated Terms.

 DoD transportation        Any form or type of transportation (i.e., land, sea, or air) that a DoD
 assets                    Component provides.

 Federal agency            Defined in Section 551 of Title 5, United States Code. This includes
                           departments and agencies such as the Departments of Homeland
                           Security, State, and Transportation, the Federal Bureau of
                           Investigation, and the Central Intelligence Agency.

 geographically            Typically, one of the 50 States of the United States that shares a
 neighboring State         border with another State. In some cases, two States that are near to
                           one another but do not share a border may be considered
                           geographically neighboring States for purposes of this issuance (e.g.,
                           Maine with Massachusetts or Vermont, and Virginia with
                           Pennsylvania).

 intertheater              Involving the AOR of more than one CCMD.

TERM                  DEFINITION

intertheater travel   Transportation linking theaters to the continental United States, as
                      well as the airlift between CCMDs. Such travel often involves long-
                      range assets assigned by United States Transportation Command.

intratheater          Within the AOR of a CCMD.

intratheater travel   Transportation within the AOR of a CCMD.

local travel          Transportation that occurs in the vicinity of the DoD Component or
                      installation concerned and/or is proximate to the scope of its mission,
                      and public interest in the PA activity involved is primarily confined
                      to the vicinity and/or mission of the DoD Component concerned.
                      May include PA orientation flights or point-to-point travel.

                      PA transportation may be considered local if travel involves visiting
                      installations overseen by the sponsoring military command or if a
                      unit conducts PA transportation while performing its mission as part
                      of a military operation or exercise away from its home installation.
                      However, local travel does not include the following, regardless of
                      the scope of the sponsoring command’s mission:

                         Intertheater travel;

                         Travel from one country to another; or

                          Travel to or from the continental United States, Alaska, or Hawaii
                      to or from U.S. territories.

major command         Military command that reports directly to the Service Headquarters
                      and is usually commanded by a U.S. military officer in the grade of
                      O-9 or above.

media member          An individual employed or contracted by a civilian radio or television
                      station, newspaper, newsmagazine, periodical, or news agency to
                      gather and report on a newsworthy event.

                      Also includes internal and U.S. or foreign PA-credentialed
                      journalists, broadcasters, producers, screenwriters, playwrights,
                      authors, photographers, videographers, and documentarians, as well
                      as PA-credentialed freelancers, bloggers, and social media
                      influencers. Their support staff (e.g., lighting technician, audio
                      technician, grip, researcher) may be considered “media” for the
                      purpose of support in accordance with this issuance.

TERM                     DEFINITION

NFE                      A self-sustaining non-Federal person or organization, established,
                         operated, and controlled by any individual(s) acting outside the scope
                         of any official capacity as officers, employees, or agents of the
                         Federal Government. It is an incorporated or unincorporated person
                         or organization that is not an agency or instrumentality of the Federal
                         Government. NFEs also include elements of State, interstate, Indian
                         tribal, or local government, as well as private organizations. Other
                         subsets of NFEs include for-profit businesses, business industry
                         organizations, veteran service organizations, nonprofit NFEs,
                         foundations, community-based or other non-governmental
                         organizations that are not affiliated with a local, State, or Federal
                         Government agency.

non-local travel         All transportation that cannot be considered local, including travel:

                            Covering large geographic distances within the United States;

                            From one country to another;

                            To or from the continental United States, Alaska, or Hawaii to or
                         from U.S. territories;

                           Conducted in support of a PA activity that affects more than one
                         DoD Component; or

                             Outside the geographic AOR of the command originating the
                         travel request.


PA                       Defined in DoD Dictionary of Military and Associated Terms.

PA orientation flights Continuous flights in DoD aircraft performed within the local flying
                       area and terminating at the point of origin or within proximity to the
                       point of origin as part of an approved component PA activity that is
                       developed, managed, and led through PA channels.

PA transportation        Defined in DoDI 4515.13.

Service component        The head of a Service component command of a CCMD that reports
head of a CCMD           directly to a CCDR.

SUBJECT:     Management of DoD Modeling and Simulation (M&S) Activities

References: See Enclosure 1


1. PURPOSE. This Instruction:

   a. Implements DoD M&S management activities of DoD Directive (DoDD) 5000.59
(Reference (a)), pursuant to DoD Directive 5134.01 (Reference (b)).

   b. Assigns responsibilities for the DoD Modeling and Simulation Steering Committee (M&S
SC) pursuant to the provisions of Reference (a).

    c. Establishes the Director, DoD Modeling and Simulation Coordination Office (M&SCO),
as the focal point for coordinating all matters related to DoD M&S, with the advice and
recommendation of the M&S SC, and as Secretariat for the M&S SC.

    d. Extends discovery metadata policy consistent with DoD Instruction 8320.02 (Reference
(c)) to those DoD M&S tools, data, services, data assets (as defined in Reference (c)), models,
and simulations (hereinafter collectively referred to as “M&S assets”) that are key.

   e. Authorizes the publication of the DoD M&S Glossary at http://www.msco.mil.

   f. Cancels DoD 5000.59-M (Reference (d)).


2. APPLICABILITY. This Instruction applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of
the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the
Office of the Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD
Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD (hereinafter referred to
collectively as the “DoD Components”).


3. DEFINITIONS. See Glossary.
                                                                     DoDI 5000.70, May 10, 2012



4. POLICY. It is DoD policy in accordance with Reference (a):

    a. That M&S assets shall be visible and accessible within and across the DoD Components.

       (1) M&S discovery metadata shall conform to the DoD Discovery Metadata
Specification (DDMS) (Reference (e)) and be discoverable, searchable, and retrievable using the
DoD M&S discovery metadata search capability and federated DoD discovery metadata search
capabilities.

      (2) Visibility and accessibility shall be facilitated by creating and associating discovery
metadata consistent with Reference (c) to key M&S assets.

         (3) When intellectual property considerations and restrictions apply to M&S assets, all
restrictions on use and reuse will be clearly noted in discovery metadata.

   b. To encourage efficient and effective development, use, and reuse of M&S, particularly
Government-owned, non-proprietary M&S assets.

    c. That the DoD M&S discovery metadata search capability shall be used in assessing
existing M&S assets for reuse.


5. RESPONSIBILITIES. See Enclosure 2.


6. PROCEDURES. See Enclosure 3.


7. RELEASABILITY. Cleared for public release. This Instruction is available on the
Directives Division Website at http://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.


8. SUMMARY OF CHANGE 3. This change reassigns the office of primary responsibility for
this instruction to the Under Secretary of Defense for Research and Engineering in accordance
with the July 13, 2018 Deputy Secretary of Defense Memorandum (Reference (f)).


8. EFFECTIVE DATE. This Instruction is effective May 10, 2012.

                                    RESPONSIBILITIES


1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION, TECHNOLOGY, AND
LOGISTICS (USD(AT&L)). The USD(AT&L) shall:

   a. In coordination with the M&S SC, develop and implement the DoD M&S discovery
metadata search capability.

      (1) Develop governance and configuration management for the DoD M&S discovery
metadata search capability and Reference (e).

       (2) Develop and implement minimum required discovery metadata for key M&S assets
consistent with Reference (e).

       (3) Develop and implement process rules and procedures to promote and to populate the
DoD M&S discovery metadata search capability with M&S asset discovery metadata consistent
with Reference (e).

      (4) Expand M&S asset discoverability by federating authoritative data repositories with
the DoD M&S discovery metadata search capability.

   b. Approve and publish the following plans and reports, consistent with Reference (a):

       (1) M&S Strategic Vision.

       (2) DoD M&S Enterprise Implementation Plan.

   c. Establish, manage the membership of, and chair the M&S SC, an executive-level DoD
committee established by Reference (a) and comprised of Senior Executive Service, flag or
general officers of the military, or officials of equivalent rank and precedence.

   d. In coordination with the Heads of the DoD Components, represent the DoD Test and
Evaluation Community on the M&S SC, and on all matters of M&S associated with the Testing
and Evaluation Community.

  e. In coordination with the Heads of the DoD Components, represent the DoD Acquisition
Community on the M&S SC, and on all matters of M&S associated with the Acquisition
Community.

   f. In coordination with the Heads of the DoD Components, develop and publish the
Acquisition Community and Test and Evaluation Community M&S Strategic Plans.

2. DIRECTOR, DoD M&SCO. The Director, DoD M&SCO, under the authority, direction, and
control of the USD(AT&L) and with the advice and recommendation of the DoD Components as
represented on the M&S SC, shall:

   a. Serve as the DoD focal point for coordinating all matters related to DoD M&S.

    b. Recommend policies, plans, and programs to the USD(AT&L) that support the M&S
Strategic Vision and DoD M&S priorities.

    c. Provide oversight and management for M&S projects and continuing activities, as
directed by the USD(AT&L).

    d. Act as DoD advocate for M&S matters and perform DoD M&S enterprise-level functions
that facilitate coordination among M&S activities DoD-wide and with industry, interagency,
academia, and international partners.

   e. Manage USD(AT&L) plans and investments in DoD M&S.

   f. Manage M&S enterprise-level standards and standardization activities.

        (1) Serve as the DoD lead standardization activity (LSA) for managing M&S standards
and methodologies (MSSM) in the Defense Standardization Program consistent with DoD
Instruction 4120.24 (Reference (g)).

      (2) Provide a representative to the Defense Information Systems Agency, Information
Technology Standards Committee, and chair the M&S Technical Working Group.

       (3) Coordinate M&S standards activities with national and international M&S standards
development and standards setting organizations.

    g. Provide technical expertise to the USD(AT&L) and the M&S SC on DoD M&S-related
topics, including architectures, software, computing, data, and other related enabling
technologies.

   h. Serve as the DoD focal point for coordinating international M&S activities consistent with
Reference (b).

   i. As directed by the M&S SC Chair, advise the USD(AT&L) on key M&S capabilities.

   j. Chair M&S working groups as directed by the M&S SC Chair.

   k. Provide the M&S SC Secretariat functions to facilitate the activities of the M&S SC. The
Secretariat shall:

       (1) Publish and maintain the M&S SC standard operating procedures.

                                       ENCLOSURE 3

                                           M&S SC


1. The M&S SC shall:

   a. Advise and assist the USD(AT&L) in all matters related to DoD M&S.

    b. Oversee the development and periodic review of DoD’s M&S Strategic Vision to guide
the M&S activities of the DoD Components and DoD Communities.

   c. Provide oversight and strategic governance of DoD M&S capabilities and resources.

   d. Develop, periodically review, and submit for USD(AT&L) approval the DoD M&S
Enterprise Implementation Plan.

    e. Oversee the development and implementation of policies, plans, procedures, and DoD
issuances to manage M&S and the implementation of best practices of how models and
simulations are effectively acquired, developed, managed, and used by DoD Components (e.g.,
verification, validation, and accreditation; standards; and protocols).

    f. Establish standard operating procedures, to include the establishment of working groups,
and other activities determined by the M&S SC as necessary to implement the responsibilities in
this Instruction.

  g. Advise and assist the USD(AT&L) in governance and configuration management for the
DoD M&S discovery metadata search capability.

   h. Meet at least semiannually, and additionally as determined by the M&S SC Chair.

2. Each member of the M&S SC shall represent the interests of the Head of their DoD
Component or their DoD Community.

                                    PART II. DEFINITIONS

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this Instruction.

architectures.The structure of components in a program or system, their interrelationships, and
the principles and guidelines governing their design and evolution over time.

data asset. Defined in Reference (c).

discovery metadata. A type of metadata that describes or summarizes key attributes and
concepts, and allows assets (e.g., a model, simulation, or data) to be found using enterprise-level
search capabilities.

DoD Community. Defined in Reference (a).

DoD Community M&S Strategic Plan. A high-level DoD plan created and published by a DoD
Community to facilitate and achieve the DoD M&S Strategic Vision, goals, and objectives for
that DoD Community.

DoD M&S discovery metadata search capability. An enterprise-level capability that enables the
discovery of metadata about M&S assets to facilitate visibility into the resources available across
the DoD and within other Federal and non-Federal agencies engaged in M&S, and federated with
DoD M&S. Sources of the M&S assets described by the discovery metadata are maintained by
DoD Components and other authoritative data and software repositories. Visibility into the

discovery metadata enables a user or developer of M&S assets to identify potential reuse
opportunities relative to their requirements.

Enterprise. An arbitrarily defined functional and administrative entity that exists to perform a
specific, integrated set of missions and achieve associated goals and objectives, encompassing all
of the primary functions necessary to perform those missions.

federated. The ability to search across multiple archives, data collections, or multiple content
aggregator services.

intellectual property. Intangible property that is the result of creativity, such as patents,
trademarks, or copyrights.

key M&S asset. An M&S tool, data set, or service, including models, simulations, or data assets,
that either exceeds $5M in annual expenditures, or is less than $5M but determined by the DoD
Component to be “key.” The total annual expenditure will be determined using standard
justification documentation for DoD appropriations, such as RDT&E (R-docs), Procurement (P-
docs), and O&M (O&M exhibits), which are provided to Congress pursuant to DoD 7000.14-R
(Reference (h)).

M&S. Defined in Reference (a).

M&S assets. M&S tools, data, and services, including models and simulations, and data assets.

M&SCO. A USD(AT&L) organization that serves as the DoD focal point for coordinating all
matters related to DoD M&S and performs key DoD M&S enterprise-level coordination
functions necessary to encourage cooperation, synergism, and cost-effectiveness among the
M&S activities of the DoD Components.

M&S data. Defined in Reference (a).

M&S Enterprise Implementation Plan. A DoD plan coordinated with the DoD Components and
published under the authority of the USD(AT&L) to achieve the vision, goals, and objectives for
corporate and crosscutting M&S within the DoD.

M&S SC. An executive-level DoD committee that advises and assists the USD(AT&L) in all
matters pertaining to DoD M&S.

M&S services. Defined in Reference (a). Examples include M&S standards development and
promulgation, interoperability, Verification, Validation, and Accreditation (VV&A) process
development, and workforce development.

M&S Strategic Vision. A high-level document, describing the strategic overview and goals for
DoD M&S.

M&S tools. Defined in Reference (a).

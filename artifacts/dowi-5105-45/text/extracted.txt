                SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to:

     a. OSW, the Military Departments (including the Coast Guard at all times, including when it
is a Service in the Department of Homeland Security by agreement with that Department), the
Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant
Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense
Agencies, the DoW Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoW
(referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoW Components”).

   b. The Commissioned Corps of the U.S. Public Health Service, under agreement with the
Department of Health and Human Services, and the Commissioned Corps of the National
Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, under agreement with the Department of Commerce.


1.2. POLICY.

The USU:

   a. Pursuant to Chapter 104, Title 10 U.S.C., conducts its mission as described in
Paragraph 4.1. of this issuance.

   b. When making admissions decisions, adheres to the principle of equal educational
opportunity for all. Pursuant to DoDD 1020.02E:

       (1) Individual consideration will be given to all applicants to USU programs.

        (2) Acceptance decisions will be made without unlawful discrimination based on race,
sex, sexual preference, color, creed, religion, national origin, marital status, or State of residence.

       (3) USU will establish focused, significant, and sustained programs to recruit and retain
students.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF WAR FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS
(USW(P&R)).

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.5., the USW(P&R) has overall responsibility for
operation and oversight of the USU and establishes salary schedules and other related benefits
for faculty and staff.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF WAR FOR HEALTH AFFAIRS (ASW(HA)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USW(P&R), the ASW(HA):

   a. Exercises authority, direction, and control over the President, USU; develops policies and
procedures for the operation and oversight of the USU; and implements such policies and
procedures pursuant to DoDD 5136.01.

   b. Exercises the authorities over the USU vested in the Secretary of War by Title 10, U.S.C.,
except:

       (1) The authority to appoint the President, USU, which resides with the Secretary of
War.

        (2) The authority to establish salary schedules and other related benefits for faculty and
staff described in Paragraph 2.1.

      (3) The authorities reserved to the Secretary of War, the USW(P&R), and the Director of
Administration and Management of the Department of War pursuant to DoDI 5105.04.

   c. Reviews and approves the USU annual budget submission and ensures its consideration
and integration in the Unified Medical Budget for the Defense Health Program (DHP)
appropriation. Reviews and approves the USU manpower document.

   d. Supports the Board of Regents, USU, in its capacity to provide independent advice to the
Secretary of War through the USW(P&R); the ASW(HA); and the President, USU on matters
concerning the operation of the USU, particularly academic affairs and management.

   e. Develops USU performance goals to assist in strategic planning and characterizes
accomplishment of the USU mission.

   f. Defines performance metrics to measure achievement of USU goals.

   g. Consults regularly with the Surgeons General of the uniformed services; the Director,
Defense Health Agency; and the Joint Staff Surgeon concerning USU goals and objectives and
progress in meeting them.

   h. Through the President, USU, and in coordination with the Director, Defense Threat
Reduction Agency, ensures the Director, Armed Forces Radiobiology Research Institute
(AFRRI) maintains three deployable medical radiobiology advisory teams pursuant to
DoDD 3150.08 and DoDI 3150.10, and in accordance with DoDI 5105.18.

   i. In coordination with the Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Secretary of
Health and Human Services, assesses statutory and budgetary limits on USU student admissions.


2.3. PRESIDENT, USU.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the ASW(HA), the President, USU:

    a. Offers educational programs leading to degrees in the health professions that meet the
standards of applicable and recognized accrediting, licensing, and certifying agencies, and
confers such degrees after considering the recommendations of faculty.

    b. Ensures that curriculum and training opportunities, to the maximum extent possible, are
aligned to critical wartime specialties, and that students are encouraged to pursue operationally
relevant assignments.

   c. Develops and administers policies and procedures on:

       (1) The academic affairs of:

           (a) The F. Edward Hébert School of Medicine (SOM).

           (b) The Daniel K. Inouye Graduate School of Nursing (GSN).

           (c) The Postgraduate Dental College (PDC).

           (d) The College of Allied Health Sciences (CAHS).

       (2) Activities of the AFRRI and other USU organizational elements and associated
programs, sponsored or accredited by the USU to accomplish the USU mission.

   d. Develops funding requirements for USU that will be resourced in the program objective
memorandum of the DHP for incorporation into the budget estimate submission and President’s
budget submissions.

       (1) Collaborates with the Defense Health Agency Deputy Assistant Director for
Financial Operations to ensure USU DHP financial reporting complies with all applicable
requirements of law and regulation.

        (2) Ensures USU internal controls are robust and working effectively to mitigate fraud,
waste, and abuse of DHP and other resources.

       (3) Promptly responds to requests for financial transaction information requested by the
Office of Inspector General of the Department of War, or any other entity appointed by the
ASW(HA) to investigate, audit, or otherwise review USU financial information.

       (4) Collaborates as requested by Defense Health Agency Deputy Assistant Director for
Financial Operations and the ASW(HA) in DHP Planning, Programming, Budgeting and
Execution efforts.

   e. Ensures accreditation requirements are met for USU to operate as an academic institution
with appropriate autonomy.

   f. Appoints a representative to any coordination, oversight, or assessment board or
committee established pursuant to DoDI 5105.18 and as directed by the ASW(HA).

   g. Selects an officer to serve as professor of military, naval, or air science pursuant to
Section 2113 (c)(3) of Title 10, U.S.C.

    h. Selects, after consultation with the Surgeons General of the respective uniformed services,
one or more military officer(s) to serve as professors, instructors, or administrative staff pursuant
to Section 2113(c)(1) of Title 10, U.S.C. Unless released earlier by the President, USU, the
selected military officer(s) will serve in the positions no less than 4 years.

   i. Consults regularly with the Surgeons General of the uniformed services; the Director,
Defense Health Agency; and the Joint Staff Surgeon concerning USU goals and objectives and
progress in meeting them.

    j. Through the Director, AFRRI, conducts activities to better understand the biological
effects of ionizing radiation, support the operational force, and engages in collaborative research
and development (R&D) of medical capabilities for exposure to ionizing radiation. These
activities will:

       (1) Reflect requirements identified by the DoW Components in support of current and
future military operations.

       (2) Evaluate the individual and collective performance of deployed Service members in
radiological and nuclear environments.

       (3) Consider present and projected risks to the United States and the Military Services,
including risks to operations, weapons, and defense systems.

       (4) Include advanced education and training in the field of radiobiology, including the
biological effects from nuclear and radiological weapons, to meet the internal requirements of
USU, the Military Departments, other DoW Components and, as necessary, other organizations.

       (5) Include analysis, study, and consultation on the impact of the biological effects of
ionizing radiation on the organizational efficiency of the Military Departments and their
members.

       (6) Include cooperative research with the Military Departments on aspects of operational
and medical support, considerations related to nuclear weapons effects, and the radiobiological

        (7) Include cooperative research and other enterprises with other federal agencies
involved in homeland security and emergency medical preparedness, consistent with the mission

   k. Develops an annual plan for AFRRI R&D activities.

   l. Develops and implements overall procedures for operation and oversight of USU

        (1) Coordinating with the Surgeons General of the uniformed services to determine the

       (3) Coordinating with the Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Department of

each uniformed service to approve USU waiver requests for waivers of Service accession
medical standards to the Service designated waiver authority to adjudicate USU waiver requests.
Final decisions on waiver requests are made by the uniformed service appointed medical officer.
Unless otherwise determined by their respective uniformed service, this medical officer will be

respective uniformed service, the medical student applicant will be assigned to the USU.
Students are held to retention standards upon matriculation and throughout their enrollment at
the USU.

        (5) Issuing, at least every other year, with addendums as necessary, the USU Bulletin
describing USU educational programs and their admissions requirements, application
procedures, and acceptance procedures, as well as summarizing federal-duty obligations as
specified in separate, individual contracts with the Military Departments, the U.S. Public Health

    m. Exercises authority to grant exceptions, consistent with accreditation requirements, to all

2.4. DIRECTOR, DEFENSE LEGAL SERVICES AGENCY.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the General Counsel of the Department of War, and
in addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.5., the Director, Defense Legal Services
Agency, provides legal advice and services for the USU in accordance with DoDD 5145.04.


2.5. OSW AND DOW COMPONENT HEADS.

The OSW and DoW Component heads will coordinate with the ASW(HA) on all matters relating
to the USU mission.


2.6. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments:

    a. In alignment with Service staffing levels, provide authorized military manpower to the
USU in accordance with approved individual Service manning documents that identify USU
billets and which describe the requirements of the Service member selected to fill the position in
terms of Service, Corps, grade, and specialty.

    b. Ensure periods of active duty served by medical students of USU are accurately
determined for purposes of computation of retirement pay pursuant to Sections 2114(b)(1) and
(c) and 2126 of Title 10, U.S.C.

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. DIRECTOR, AFRRI, NOMINATION AND APPOINTMENT.

The Director, AFRRI, is selected and appointed as follows:

    a. Candidates are individuals that are military officers (in grade O-6 or above) and hold an
earned academic doctoral degree in one of the life sciences.

    b. The President, USU, will request nominations from the Surgeons General of the Military
Departments who will nominate one candidate from their respective Departments with the proper
professional qualifications and demonstrated leadership ability.

   c. The President, USU, will appoint an individual from the Surgeons General-nominated list
of Military Department candidates to the position of the Director, AFRRI.


3.2. ADMISSIONS PROCEDURES.

Individuals seeking admission as medical students, graduate students, or students in other USU
degree-granting programs are advised to apply following instructions periodically published by
the respective schools in the USU Bulletin, available online at
https://www.usuhs.edu/admissions.

                   SECTION 4: MISSION AND ORGANIZATION

4.1. MISSION.

The USU mission is to:

    a. Educate, train, and comprehensively prepare uniformed services health professionals,
officers, scientists, and leaders to directly support the Military and Public Health Systems, the
National Security and National Defense Strategies of the United States, and the readiness of the
uniformed services.

   b. Provide high quality educational programs in health sciences and grant applicable degrees
pursuant to Sections 2113(e) and 2113(f) of Title 10, U.S.C.

    c. Ensure consistent development of sound qualities (including professionalism, ethics,
leadership, and commitment) in USU students to prepare them for exemplary careers as
operationally oriented health professionals and leaders, thus providing continuity in the health
care for the DoW, uniformed services, and others.

    d. Conduct health readiness training and continuing health education for members of the
uniformed services and others in the health professions.

   e. Conduct health sciences research consistent with the academic mission of the USU.

   f. Conduct R&D activities related to the biological effects of ionizing radiation and other
specified functions through the AFRRI.

  g. Provide Global Health Engagement–related support to the ASW(HA), OSW, Joint Staff,
Combatant Commands, Services, and other DoW Components, in accordance with
DoDI 2000.30, through the Center for Global Health Engagement, by:

       (1) Conducting professional development, research, knowledge management,
assessment, monitoring, evaluation, and operational support.

        (2) Helping to ensure DoW activities are effective in improving health readiness;
fostering partner nation interoperability; and contributing to the achievement of Combatant
Command, DoW, and U.S. national security strategic objectives.

      (3) Supporting DoW's Global Health Security Agenda–related efforts, helping to ensure
DoW fulfills its responsibilities under the U.S. Global Health Security Strategy.

       (4) Supporting DoW efforts to promote military-civilian collaboration and advance
regional health security.

       (5) Supporting ASW(HA) participation in the Global Health Security Agenda
Interagency Review Council.

    h. Conduct additional activities as directed by the ASW(HA) or higher authorities to further
the DoW health mission.


4.2. ORGANIZATION.

USU is an organizational entity of the DoW. The USU consists of:

  a. A President, who is the chief executive officer of the USU, appointed by the Secretary of
War pursuant to Section 2113 (b) of Title 10, U.S.C.

   b. A Board of Regents:

      (1) Established by Section 2113a of Title 10, U.S.C., and operated pursuant to
DoDI 5105.04.

       (2) Consisting of appointed members pursuant to Section 2113a (b) of Title 10, U.S.C.

       (3) Vested with the authority to advise, under procedures authorized by DoWI 5105.04,
the Secretary of War; the USW(P&R); the ASW(HA); and the President, USU on matters
concerning the operation of the USU, particularly academic affairs and management.

   c. Four schools and one institute:

       (1) The SOM. The SOM admits students (medical officer candidates) to the Doctor of
Medicine program and military and civilian graduate students to non- Doctor of Medicine
degree-granting graduate programs.

        (2) The GSN. The GSN admits registered nurses into one of several advanced nursing
practice tracks and to a research doctoral program in nursing science.

         (3) The PDC. The PDC selects and admits students to the Master of Science in oral
biology program from the cohort of officers previously selected for residency training in USU-
affiliated graduate dental education programs by their respective uniformed service.

        (4) The CAHS. The CAHS admits students to undergraduate and graduate programs of
study in the field of allied health sciences with a goal of enhancing their performance on military
health multidisciplinary teams.

        (5) The AFRRI. The AFRRI conducts R&D activities and provides education, training,
and advisory assistance to the DoW related to the health effects of radiation exposure, especially
as related to the support of military operations.

    d. Other schools, institutes, or subordinate organizational elements and associated programs
as directed by the ASW(HA) and established by the President, USU, within assigned resources.

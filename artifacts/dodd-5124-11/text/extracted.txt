                               DOD DIRECTIVE 5124.11
        ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR READINESS
                         (ASD(R))

Originating Component:       Office of the Chief Management Officer

Effective:                   September 6, 2019

Releasability:               Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                             at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

Approved by:                 David L. Norquist, Deputy Secretary of Defense


Purpose: Pursuant to the authority vested in the Secretary of Defense by Sections 113 and 138 of
Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.), this issuance:

• Assigns the responsibilities and functions, relationships, and authorities of the ASD(R), under the
authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness
(USD(P&R)).
• Establishes the ASD(R) as the successor in part to the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Reserve
Affairs (ASD(RA)) with respect to the responsibilities and functions identified in Section 2 of this
issuance.
• Establishes the ASD(R) as the successor in part to the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Readiness
and Force Management (ASD(R&FM)) with respect to the responsibilities and functions identified in
Section 2 of this issuance.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

APPLICABILITY. This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the
Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office
of the Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field
Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this
issuance as the “DoD Components”).

                SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES AND FUNCTIONS

ASD(R). The ASD(R) is the principal advisor to the Secretary of Defense and the USD(P&R)
on all matters related to the readiness of the Total Force. In this capacity, the ASD(R):

   a. Develops policies and plans, provides advice, and makes recommendations for:

       (1) Total Force Readiness programs, reporting, and assessments of readiness to execute
the National Defense Strategy, as well as operating and refining the Defense Readiness
Reporting System-Strategic, in accordance with DoD Directive (DoDD) 7730.65.

       (2) Oversight of global force management, including participation in the Secretary of
Defense Orders Book, Global Force Management Board, Global Force Management Allocation
Plan, Directed Readiness Tables, and associated programs and policies to recover and sustain the
readiness of the Total Force.

        (3) Institutional, collective, individual, and joint military training and its enablers,
including common and interoperable training capabilities, Live-Synthetic systems, and live
training ranges for the Total Force, in accordance with DoDDs 1322.18 and 3200.15.

        (4) Implementing Reserve Component (RC) mobilization policy and overseeing full RC
participation in the global force management process, consistent with Title 10, U.S.C.; Title 32,
U.S.C.; and DoD Instruction (DoDI) 1235.12.

        (5) Establishing resourcing requirements for readiness programming and assessment
matters pertaining to the Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution process, Total Force
readiness resource management, and equipment and facilities readiness. Serves as the DoD focal
point for integration of Total Force readiness resources.

       (6) Professional military education to include alignment to the National Defense Strategy
and National Military Strategy and talent management and utilization.

        (7) Voluntary education programs, other than graduate medical education, and
partnerships to include military participation in off-duty education and tuition assistance
consistent with Section 2007 and 2006a of Title 10, U.S.C., and in accordance with
DoDD 1322.08E, and DoDIs 1322.19 and 1322.25.

        (8) The Troops to Teachers Program to include military and veteran participation in
counseling, assessment, and referral to facilitate employment of eligible members of the Armed
Forces as teachers in public elementary or secondary schools, consistent with Section 1154 of
Title 10, U.S.C.

       (9) DoD language, regional expertise, and culture capability management and skill
tracking, in accordance with DoDD 5160.41E. This includes the National Security Education
Program, pursuant to Chapter 37 of Title 50, U.S.C. and in accordance with DoDI 1025.02.

        (10) The accession, training, and utilization of foreign area officers, in accordance with
DoDD 1315.17, to perform strategic-level military-diplomatic roles, fill key security cooperation
positions, and support Combatant Commanders with uniformed members possessing in-depth
regional knowledge, cultural expertise, and advanced foreign language proficiency.

        (11) Safety assessment and reporting; trend analysis; mishap and accident reduction and
mitigation activities; operational mishap safety to include aviation, ground, motor vehicle, afloat,
and space; and occupational safety and health.

        (12) Promoting and sustaining Service member financial readiness. Oversees related
financial literacy training, and counseling programs and assesses financial literacy and
preparedness, in accordance with Sections 987 and 992 of Title 10, U.S.C.

       (13) The Office of the Secretary of Defense Strategic Thinkers Program in accordance
with Directive-type Memorandum-19-001.

       (14) The Combatant Commanders Exercise Engagement and Training Transformation
program to include developing program goals and objectives, co-chairing the annual Combatant
Commanders Exercise Engagement and Training Transformation Program Objective
Memorandum panel, and assessing the overall performance of the program through the Joint
Assessment and Enabling Capability Office.

       (15) The implementation of timely and efficient implementation, fielding and
sustainment of training capabilities to support live and virtual ranges and constructive
capabilities.

        (16) Innovative and interoperable standards for education and training to include the
development, management, and evaluation of distributed learning policy and the use of
electronic distributed learning in accordance with DoDI 1215.21.

   b. Reports the readiness of the DoD to execute the National Defense Strategy to the
Congress quarterly, in accordance with Section 482 of Title 10, U.S.C. Revises and updates the
Readiness Recovery Framework at least semi-annually.

   c. Serves as the U.S. representative to the North Atlantic Treaty Organization Training
Group.

  d. Serves as the DoD Designated Agency Safety and Health Official and supports the
USD(P&R) in his or her role as the Principal Enterprise Safety Official.

    e. Fosters and participates in interagency and international partnerships to enhance training
and education interoperability, support effective integrated operations, and strengthen DoD’s
relationships with allies and partners.

   f. Initiates analyses and studies that support DoD’s readiness, education, military training,
and crisis planning and response functions.

    g. Provides administrative support and funding associated with the Secretary of Defense
Executive Fellows, in accordance with DoDI 1322.23, including information systems, staffing,
travel and per-diem, training, contract services, logistics, and security support.

   h. Chairs the Executive Readiness Management Group.

    i. Participates in Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution activities that relate to
assigned responsibilities and functions, to include:

       (1) Reviewing readiness, military training, and force management policies and programs
to improve standards of performance, economy, and efficiency.

       (2) Using data modeling, research, and exploratory techniques to develop and identify

parameters and validates relevant metrics.

   j. Uses existing systems, facilities, and services of the DoD and other federal agencies, to

   k. Performs such other duties as the Secretary of Defense and the USD(P&R) may prescribe.

                             SECTION 3: RELATIONSHIPS

3.1. ASD(R). In performing his or her assigned responsibilities and functions, the ASD(R):

    a. Reports directly to the USD(P&R) and the Deputy USD(P&R), who exercise authority,
direction, and control over the ASD(R).

    b. Coordinates with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve Affairs
on all matters impacting the RC and Total Force Management, such as manpower, allocation,
structure, and workforce mix.

   c. Coordinates with the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Health Affairs on all matters
impacting medical readiness of the Total Force.

   d. Provides input to the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Sustainment on expected training
impacts during offshore mission capability assessments in accordance with DoDI 4180.02.

   e. Coordinates with the Director, Joint Staff, on matters related to:

       (1) Joint force readiness.

       (2) Global force management.

       (3) Apportionment data.

       (4) Directed Readiness Tables.

       (5) Readiness resourcing and programmatic issues.

        (6) Joint forces development policy governing officer and enlisted joint professional
military education.

       (7) RC mobilization execution.

   f. Coordinates and exchanges information with other OSD officials, the DoD Component
heads, and federal, State, or local officials having collateral or related responsibilities and
functions.


3.2. OSD PRINCIPAL STAFF ASSISTANTS AND DOD COMPONENT HEADS. The
OSD Principal Staff Assistants and DoD Component heads coordinate with the ASD(R) on
matters, under their respective purviews, related to the responsibilities and functions and
authorities assigned in this issuance.

                               SECTION 4: AUTHORITIES

The ASD(R) is delegated authority to:

    a. Exercise the powers of the USD(P&R) on all matters arising from the responsibilities and
functions identified in this issuance that are not otherwise specifically set forth in this section,
except in those areas where delegation of the USD(P&R) authority is restricted by higher
authority or prohibited by law.

    b. Serve as successor in part to the ASD(RA) for the functional areas identified in Section 2
of this issuance and, for those functional areas, exercise authorities delegated to the ASD(RA) in
other issuances or memoranda.

   c. Serve as successor in part to the ASD(R&FM) for the functional areas identified in
Section 2 of this issuance and, for those functional areas, exercise authorities delegated to the
ASD(R&FM) in other issuances or memoranda.

    d. Approve and sign DoD manuals and directive-type memorandums, in accordance with
DoDI 5025.01, that implement policy approved by the Secretary of Defense or the USD(P&R) in
the ASD(R)’s assigned areas of responsibility and function. The ASD(R) will:

       (1) Assign responsibilities in DoD issuances for the Military Departments to the
Secretaries of those Departments.

       (2) Coordinate with the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff when assigning
responsibilities in DoD issuances to the Combatant Commanders.

    e. Communicate directly with other OSD officials and the DoD Component heads, as
necessary, to carry out assigned responsibilities and functions, including transmitting requests for
advice and assistance. Communications to the Military Departments must be transmitted
through the Secretaries of the Military Departments, as otherwise provided in law, or as the
Secretary of Defense directs in other DoD issuances. Communications to the Combatant
Commanders must be in accordance with DoDD 5100.01.

    f. Communicate with other government officials, members of the public, and representatives
of foreign governments, as appropriate, in carrying out assigned responsibilities and functions.
Communications with representatives and members of the legislative branch must be conducted
through the Office of the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Legislative Affairs, except for
communications with the Defense Appropriations Committees, which must be coordinated with
the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense (Comptroller)/Chief Financial Officer, Department
of Defense.

   g. Obtain reports and information, as necessary, to perform assigned responsibilities and
functions, in accordance with DoDI 8910.01.

  h. Arrange for DoD participation in those non-defense governmental programs for which the
ASD(R) has been assigned primary responsibility.

G.2. DEFINITIONS. These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

RC. Refers collectively to the Army National Guard of the United States, the Army Reserve, the
Navy Reserve, the Marine Corps Reserve, the Air National Guard of the United States, the Air
Force Reserve, and the Coast Guard Reserve when the Coast Guard is operating as a Service in
the Navy.

Total Force. The organizations, units, and manpower used to meet the requirements associated
with the DoD missions. It includes Active Component and RC military personnel, DoD civilian
personnel (including foreign national direct- and indirect-hires), as well as non-appropriated fund
employees, host nation support personnel, and contracted services.

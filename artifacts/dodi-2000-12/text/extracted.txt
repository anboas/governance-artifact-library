                 SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance:

    a. Applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs
of Staff (CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands (CCMDs), the Office of Inspector
General of the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all
other organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).

    b. Applies to the following (referred to collectively in this issuance as “DoD-affiliated
personnel”):

       (1) DoD military and civilian personnel (referred to collectively in this issuance as “DoD
personnel”).

       (2) DoD personnel assigned to the United Nations or other multinational peace
operations.

       (3) Dependents of DoD military personnel, as defined in Section 1072 of Title 10,
United States Code (U.S.C.).

       (4) Family members of DoD civilian employees, as defined in Section 040 of
Department of State (DOS) Standardized Regulations, residing with the DoD civilian employee
outside the United States.

       (5) Defense contractor personnel, as defined in Section 158.3 of Title 32, Code of
Federal Regulations, to the extent incorporated in and consistent with the terms of their
applicable contracts.

   c. Does not apply to DoD-affiliated personnel under the security responsibility of DOS
pursuant to the December 16, 1997 Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between the DOS
and DoD (referred to in this issuance as the “1997 DOS-DoD MOU”) and DoD Instruction
(DoDI) 5210.84.

    d. Does not expand or replace the functions, responsibilities, or authorities of the Principal
Staff Assistants or those of the DoD Component heads prescribed by law or other DoD guidance.

    e. Does not implement Section 2672 of Title 10, U.S.C., the implementation of which is
prescribed in DoDD 5525.21.

1.2. POLICY.

   a. AT is one of multiple FP enablers. AT and FP are not synonymous.

   b. The DoD AT Program:

       (1) Includes defensive measures used to reduce the vulnerability of individuals and
property to terrorist and other hostile acts.

       (2) Contributes to FP by deterring and mitigating the effects of violent or coercive action
intended to undermine U.S. military power through immediate or incremental impact.

           (a) To support FP and in coordination with other protection-related programs, the
DoD AT Program mitigates against Level I and II threats to the force, operational readiness, and
force projection, as described in Joint Publication 3-10.

           (b) The requirements in this issuance support local application of AT program
standards in Volume 1 of DoDI O-2000.16 and the threat-driven implementation of Force
Protection Condition (FPCON) System measures in Volume 2 of DoDI O-2000.16.

           (c) Commanders of assigned forces maintain flexibility to adjust AT priorities to
support FP based on threat posture and immediate or anticipated operational requirements.

           (d) Commanders and civilian equivalents maintain flexibility to integrate AT with
other programs and functions based on mission requirements and threat assessments.

   c. AT contributes to mission assurance by preventing and mitigating the effects of hostile
and coercive actions perpetrated by adversaries.

    d. AT response planning is consistent to the maximum extent possible with the Department
of Homeland Security (DHS)’s National Response Framework and National Incident
Management System and synchronized with incident response plans pursuant to DoDI 6055.17.

   e. DoD complies with the No Double Standard Policy as prescribed in Paragraph 3.1.
regarding the dissemination of threat information.

    f. In addition to appropriate restrictions on military personnel, the DoD Component heads
with proper authority may impose narrowly tailored restrictions, reasonably related to the
identified DoD interest, on non-official foreign travel, after appropriate consultation with the
relevant Chief of Mission (COM) if time permits, for:

       (1) Individuals briefed and indoctrinated for sensitive compartmented information,
pursuant to Intelligence Community Directive 703.

      (2) Defense contractor personnel, if they are required by the terms of their contracts to
comply with AT policies of CCMDs with physical areas of responsibility (AORs).

    g. DoD-affiliated personnel comply with the theater, country, and special area clearance

HEMISPHERIC AFFAIRS (ASD(HD&HA)).

2.14. DOD COMPONENT HEADS.

The DoD Component heads:

   a. Coordinate with necessary stakeholders to avoid conflicts between:

       (1) CCMD and other Component AT plans and policies.

      (2) FP measures and defense readiness condition or other protection condition system
measures.

     b. Integrate AT with other FP and protection-related programs to deter and mitigate hostile
actions against DoD personnel; resources, including defense critical infrastructure; facilities; and
critical information.

    c. As part of the PPBE process and to support FP, use risk analysis to identify resource
requirements necessary to implement and maintain AT-related programs, functions, and facility
standards, including countermeasures for identified vulnerabilities.

       (1) Submit AT resource requirements to the Secretary of Defense and provide an
information copy to the ASD(HD&HA) and CJCS.

       (2) Submit emergent or emergency AT resource requirements that cannot be funded
through other means through the appropriate CCDR to the CJCS for CCIF consideration.

        (3) Implement accounting procedures to enable precise reporting of data in accordance
with current DoD Fiscal Year Budget Estimates, including the number and cost of personnel
directly supporting DoD AT activities.

   d. Establish procedures to:

       (1) Comply with threat-based standards for facilities and spaces.

       (2) Ensure that appropriate AT personnel participate in facility planning projects.

        (3) Approve levels of protection for facility projects based on the Unified Facilities
Criteria (UFC) risk management process.

       (4) Ensure non-DoD tenants comply with DoD AT requirements, including compliance
of non-DoD tenant facilities on DoD installations with applicable UFC standards.

        (5) Ensure commanders and civilian equivalents responsible for DoD elements
occupying leased facility space or space in any non-DoD facility not located on DoD
installations comply with the applicable Federal Interagency Security Committee standards
incorporated into the UFC.

   e. In accordance with Volume 3 of DoD Manual 5200.08, evaluate requests for deviations
from minimum physical security standards and coordinate with the CCDR of the physical AOR
concerned for approval of deviations that may impact FP posture.

2.16. CJCS.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.14., the CJCS:

    a. Serves as the principal military adviser to the Secretary of Defense for all DoD AT issues
to support FP.

   b. Assists the ASD(HD&HA) in developing and maintaining AT standards and procedures.

       (1) Provides results of comprehensive AT program reviews to the ASD(HD&HA).

        (2) Reviews DoD Component doctrine, standards, and procedures for consistency with
joint doctrine and compliance with this issuance.

    c. Collaborates with the ASD(HD&HA) to identify AT intelligence, counterintelligence, and

2.17. CCDRS WITH PHYSICAL AORS.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.14., CCDRs with physical AORs:

   a. Exercise TACON for FP of covered forces, as defined and described in Paragraph 3.2.,
within their physical AORs.

    b. Establish AT policies and programs to protect all DoD-affiliated personnel within their
AORs except DoD personnel under diplomatic security responsibility of COMs as designated by
the 1997 DOS-DoD MOU and associated MOAs.

   c. Are authorized to modify mandatory DoD FPCON measures conflicting with elevated
defense readiness condition or other protection condition system measures.

   d. Are authorized to restrict all DoD civilian employees from engaging in official travel to or
within their AORs.

    e. Develop and maintain country-level MOAs in accordance with the 1997 DOS-DoD MOU
to specify security responsibility and security services for DoD personnel based on COM and
CCDR capacity and capability.

    f. Serve as the DoD point of contact with host nation officials on matters involving AT
policies and FP measures.

    g. Establish command relationships and policies for subordinate commands, including joint
task forces, to maintain an FP posture commensurate with the threat.

    h. Provide sufficient threat information to COMs, subordinate commanders, and DoD
civilian managers within their AORs to shape effective FP measures and reduce differences in
FP standards.

    i. Coordinate with the Secretaries of the Military Departments to identify and address gaps in
information sharing.

2.18. CCDRS WITH TRANSREGIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES.

                              SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. NO DOUBLE STANDARD POLICY.

    a. Outside the United States, commanders and civilian equivalents pass information
regarding threats directed solely against the DoD through the chain of command to the lowest
level with direct liaison with DOS or the appropriate U.S. embassies (or for non-CCDR-assigned
forces, and the SDOs/DATTs).

    b. Within the United States, including its territories and possessions, commanders and
civilian equivalents pass threat information through the chain of command to the lowest level
that has direct liaison with DHS, the Department of Justice, and other Federal law enforcement
agencies.

   c. Except when immediate notice is critical to the security of DoD-affiliated personnel,
commanders and DoD civilian equivalents will inform the DOS and U.S. embassies or DHS, the
Department of Justice, and other Federal law enforcement agencies of the threat information
before release to DoD-affiliated personnel.

   d. When immediate notice is critical to the security of DoD-affiliated personnel,
commanders and civilian equivalents may immediately disseminate the information to, and
implement appropriate protective measures for, DoD-affiliated personnel and inform the DOS
and U.S. embassies or DHS, the Department of Justice, and other Federal law enforcement
agencies as soon as possible.

   e. Commanders and civilian equivalents will, as soon as possible, inform DOS and U.S.
embassies or DHS of changes to FPCON levels or security posture that significantly affect the
host nation or U.S. public.

    f. When FPCONs are changed based on received threat information, commanders and
civilian equivalents will pass both the threat information and notice of the changed FPCON
through the chain of command to the lowest level with direct liaison with the DOS and the U.S.
embassies (or for non-CCMD-assigned forces, and the SDOs/DATTs), or DHS, the Department
of Justice, and other Federal law enforcement agencies. The purpose of coordination and
cooperation with DOS and U.S. embassies or DHS in these cases is to inform the COM or
Secretary of Homeland Security of the DoD response to an identified threat. Providing this
information is not a request for concurrence.


3.2. TACON FOR FP.

   a. TACON for FP includes:

       (1) Preventive measures taken to detect, deter, and mitigate hostile actions against DoD
personnel, resources, facilities, and critical information, including FPCON system measures and
temporary relocation and departure.

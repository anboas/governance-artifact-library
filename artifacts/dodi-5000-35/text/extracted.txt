               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

DoD will maintain an organized system for the development and control of procurement and
contracting policies and procedures. The DARS develops and maintains acquisition rules and
guidance for the contracting workforce as they acquire the goods and services DoD requires.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR ACQUISITION AND SUSTAINMENT
(USD(A&S)).

The USD(A&S) establishes policy, assigns responsibilities for the management and operations of
the DARS, and delegates all responsibilities pertaining to DoD procurement to the Principal
Director, Defense Pricing, Contracting, and Acquisition Policy (DPCAP).


2.2. PRINCIPAL DIRECTOR, DPCAP.

Under the authority and direction of the USD(A&S) and subject to his or her authority, direction,
and control, and in accordance with statutes, regulations, DoD issuances and policies, the
Principal Director, DPCAP is delegated the authority to exercise, within assigned responsibilities
and functions, all DoD procurement authority, except where specifically limited by statute to the
USD(A&S). The Principal Director, DPCAP is specifically delegated the responsibility to:

   a. Serve as the DoD representative on the Federal Acquisition Regulatory Council and attend
Council meetings in accordance with Section 1302 of Title 41, United States Code (U.S.C.).
This authority cannot be redelegated.

   b. Issue and maintain the Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) jointly with the
administrators of the General Services Administration (GSA) and the National Aeronautics and
Space Administration (NASA) in accordance with Section 1303 of Title 41, U.S.C.

    c. Develop, coordinate, issue, and maintain any regulations issued by the DoD to supplement
the FAR (also known and referred to in this issuance as the Defense Federal Acquisition
Regulation Supplement (DFARS)) in accordance with Section 1303 of Title 41, U.S.C. through
establishment of the DAR and appointment of the DAR Council Director.

   d. Review and approve revisions to the FAR in coordination with the other members of the
Federal Acquisition Regulatory Council, the Civilian Acquisition Agency Council, and industry
where appropriate pursuant to Section 1303 of Title 41, U.S.C.

   e. Ensure that DoD procurement and contracting regulations are consistent with the FAR,
and any that are redundant with the FAR or are unnecessary are eliminated in accordance with
Section 1303 of Title 41, U.S.C.

   f. Ensure transparency and efficiency in DoD procurement and contracting rulemaking in
accordance with Section 1303 of Title 41, U.S.C.

  g. Review and approve revisions to the DFARS in accordance with Subpart 201.304 of the
DFARS.

   h. Approve or disapprove regulations relating to procurement proposed to be issued by the
DoD Components pursuant to Section 1303 of Title 41, U.S.C. This authority may only be
redelegated with the approval of the USD(A&S).

   i. Approve any individual or class waivers or deviations from the FAR or DFARS that are
required to be approved above the level of a DoD Component in accordance with Subparts
201.402(1) and 201.404(b)(i) of the DFARS. This authority may only be redelegated with the
approval of the USD(A&S).

    j. Review and approve DoD procurement and contracting policies, procedures, clauses, and
forms that have a significant effect beyond DoD internal operating procedures or have a
significant cost or administrative impact on contractors or offerors in accordance with Subpart
201.304(1)(i) of the DFARS.

    k. Review and approve DoD Component plans for the control of clauses, other than those
prescribed by the FAR or DFARS as specified in Subpart 201.304(4) of the DFARS.

    l. Ensure compliance with the publication requirements of Section 1707 of Title 41, U.S.C.
for DoD procurement and contracting regulations, or waive these requirements in urgent and
compelling circumstances.

    m. Carry out DoD’s responsibilities set forth in Chapter 35 of Title 44, U.S.C. for each
information collection request that relates to procurement rules and regulations pursuant to
Section 1303 of Title 41, U.S.C.

   n. Act as the head of agency or agency head for DoD Components other than the Military
Departments for functions that are required by the FAR or DFARS to be performed by the
USD(A&S) or another official within OSD.

   o. Act for the USD(A&S) in making any determination and approving any waiver,
exception, or exemption as authorized by statute that pertains to the conduct of DoD
procurement. This authority may only be redelegated with the approval of the USD(A&S).

    p. Ensure uniform administration of DoD contract financing, including DoD contract
financing policies in accordance with Subpart 232.070(a) of the DFARS.

   q. Redelegate responsibilities that are vested in the USD(A&S) by statute or by delegation
from the Secretary of Defense or Deputy Secretary of Defense that require implementation in the
FAR or DFARS, except to the extent that further redelegation is not permitted.

                             SECTION 3: DAR COUNCIL

3.1. DAR COUNCIL.

The DAR Council is responsible for developing fully coordinated recommendations for revisions
to the FAR, DFARS, and DFARS Procedures, Guidance, and Information (PGI) that are
responsive to overall DoD needs. The DAR Council consists of:

   a. The DAR Council Director, who serves under the authority and direction of the Principal
Director, DPCAP.

   b. The DAR Council Deputy Director, who serves under the direction of the DAR Council
Director.

   c. A policy representative and a legal representative from each Military Department, DLA,
and DCMA.

        (1) These representatives are nominated by the contracting principal or the appropriate
legal principal for their DoD Component and are approved by the Principal Director, DPCAP.

      (2) These representatives will have at least a SECRET clearance and will serve as the
permanent representative for their agency on the DAR Council for not less than 2 years.

       (3) On behalf of their respective DoD Components, they will be empowered to develop
and present fully coordinated positions on all matters brought before the DAR Council.


3.2. DAR COUNCIL DIRECTOR.

The DAR Council Director will direct the activities of the DAR Council, the DARS Directorate
within DPCAP, and the DFARS drafting teams. In conjunction with the administrators of GSA
and NASA, the DAR Council Director promulgates government-wide acquisition regulations
with the FAR System. Additionally, the DAR Council Director will:

    a. Establish operating procedures for the DARS to facilitate development and processing of
procurement and contracting policies, procedures, clauses, and forms for approval by the
Principal Director, DPCAP.

   b. Determine what matters are appropriate for consideration by the DAR Council.

   c. Act as the DoD point of contact for all proposed FAR, DFARS, and DFARS PGI cases
submitted from:

       (1) Senior officials responsible for DoD acquisition matters.

     (2) Senior officials from other Federal agencies designated to represent their agency on
FAR matters.

       (3) Authorized representatives of private sector entities.

   d. Determine the need to open or close a DFARS or DFARS PGI case and, in conjunction
with GSA and NASA, the need to open or close a FAR case.

  e. Establish schedules based on the perceived urgency of the case for the completion of each
DFARS or DFARS PGI case and, in conjunction with GSA and NASA, each FAR case.

   f. Determine the appropriate scope of industry input to the development of DFARS
regulations and, in conjunction with GSA and NASA, FAR regulations.

   g. When appropriate, establish ad hoc drafting teams to work unusual, special interest, or
high priority cases. The DAR Council Director may request participation of representatives from
OSD staff elements, the Military Departments, the Defense Agencies and, in coordination with
GSA and NASA, the civilian agencies.

    h. Refer to the Principal Director, DPCAP for resolution of any matters or issues where a
consensus of DAR Council policy members is not achieved after a reasonable period of debate or
where the DAR Council consensus appears contrary to established DoD policy. The decision of
the Principal Director, DPCAP, consistent with any applicable guidance from the Secretary of
Defense, the Deputy Secretary of Defense, or the USD(A&S), will be adopted as the DoD
position without further coordination.

    i. In conjunction with GSA, NASA, and the Office of Federal Procurement Policy, establish
permanent interagency FAR drafting teams to consider proposed FAR revisions within specific
functional areas. The Military Departments, DLA, and DCMA will provide members to serve on
those teams as needed.

   j. Establish permanent DFARS drafting teams to consider proposed DFARS and DFARS
PGI revisions within specific functional areas. The Military Departments, DLA, and DCMA will
provide members to serve on those teams as needed.

   k. Provide information on FAR cases and DFARS cases for the biannual Unified Agenda of
Regulatory and Deregulatory Actions and annual Regulatory Plan in accordance with Section 4,
Paragraphs (b) and (c) of Executive Order 12866.

   l. Maintain permanent records of DAR Council activities.

   m. Maintain electronic official case files and perform case management functions.

    n. Refer matters for appropriate coordination within OSD and present the views of OSD on
all matters discussed by the DAR Council.

   o. Publish DFARS and DFARS PGI and notices of any changes thereto.

   p. Serve as liaison with the Civilian Agency Acquisition Council, the FAR Secretariat, the
Federal Register, and the Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs.

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

These terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 case                      A recommended revision to the FAR, the DFARS, or DFARS PGI
                           that has been formally accepted by the DAR Council Director for
                           consideration by the DAR Council.

 clause                    A contract or contract solicitation term, condition, or provision, of the
                           type found in Part 52 of the FAR or Part 252 of the DFARS.

 DARS                      A directorate within DPCAP responsible for developing and
                           maintaining the FAR and DFARS.

 regulation                The FAR, DFARS, and any DoD Component supplements to the
                           FAR or DFARS, including any contracting policy letters, clause
                           books, automated systems, written instructions, or similar
                           instruments.

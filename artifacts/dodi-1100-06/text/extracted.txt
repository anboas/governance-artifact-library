               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD (referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD
Components”).


1.2. POLICY.

It is DoD policy that as part of the manpower mobilization base for national security, a unit or
units of the NDER program may be established and maintained to confirm that the DoD is
capable of responding to all threats to the national security of the United States.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS
(USD(P&R)).

The USD(P&R), in accordance with the NDER Policies and Procedures Manual:

   a. Establishes the procedures for, and administration of, the DoD NDER program.

   b. Represents the DoD on the Interagency NDER Committee.

    c. Provides the Secretary of Homeland Security any information required regarding DoD
units established under the NDER program.


2.2. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND DIRECTORS OF
THE DEFENSE AGENCIES AND DOD FIELD ACTIVITIES.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Directors of the Defense Agencies and DoD
Field Activities with independent appointing authority on behalf of themselves and their serviced
populations:

   a. In accordance with the NDER Policies and Procedures Manual:

       (1) Recruit and train NDER unit members.

       (2) Designate, redesignate, and deactivate members of the NDER unit.

       (3) Require compliance with all Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) and
Office of Personnel Management policies and regulations pertaining to the NDER program.

        (4) Establish appropriate qualification levels and standards for NDER member
recruitment and selection for successful performance of their planned emergency assignment.

       (5) Submit appropriate documentation to FEMA when deactivating NDER units.

       (6) Submit reports as required by the USD(P&R).

    b. Determine the level of security clearance necessary for each NDER unit member under
existing security position sensitivity designation standards for positions within the Federal
service and verify that the proper security clearance is obtained before a person is designated an
NDER member.

   c. Conduct, within funding limitations, appropriate annual training for each NDER unit.

   d. Prescribe additional operating procedures consistent with this issuance and any applicable
FEMA issuances that are necessary for the efficient establishment and operation of the NDER
program.

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES
3.1. In accordance with E.O. 13603, the Secretary of Defense may employ civilian personnel
when activating all or a part of its NDER unit. The exercise of this authority is subjected to the
provisions of E.O. 13603, which includes that the Secretary of Defense may not delegate this
authority.

3.2. The following procedures will be used for the NDER program:

   a. The membership of individual NDER units will be limited to the number of executive
positions that cannot be filled appropriately during a national emergency by regular full-time
Federal employees.

   b. In the interest of efficiency, existing facilities, materials, and information channels will be
used as much as possible in operating the NDER program.

    c. Persons assigned to the Ready Reserve of the Military Services will not be designated as
members of the NDER. State and local officials and persons campaigning for elective local
office who have (or would have, if elected) emergency responsibilities because of their positions
will not be considered for membership.

    d. NDER members are not considered officers or employees of the Federal Government
during times in which they receive training for mobilization assignments under the NDER
program. At such times, they will not act or advise on any matter pending before a DoD
Component.

    e. During periods of national emergency, NDER members expected to work no more than
130 days in any consecutive 365-day period are subject to the provisions of Section 202(a) of
Title 18, United States Code regarding special Government employees. Section 202 sets forth
whether and to what extent the ethics laws apply to special Government employees. To avoid
conflicts of interest with the NDER member’s personal financial interests, each NDER member
must submit an Office of Government Ethics Form 450, “Confidential Financial Disclosure
Report” or Office of Government Ethics Form 278, “Public Financial Disclosure Report,” as
appropriate, before each appointment or re-appointment. An NDER member must submit the
required financial disclosure report(s) for each calendar year in which the member is activated.

    f. In accordance with the NDER Policies and Procedures Manual promulgated by FEMA,
regarding conflict of interests provisions, NDER appointees are limited to advising appropriate
full-time salaried Federal Government officials who are responsible for making policy decisions.
In accordance with the NDER Policies and Procedures Manual, NDER appointees, although
subject to conflict-of-interest laws, may continue to receive compensation from their private
employer during the period they are performing services for the Federal Government.

    g. Annual NDER unit member training will include general orientations and provision of
up-to-date information on the actions planned in the event of mobilization and on the associated
responsibilities of NDER members. Training will include ethics training. NDER members will

G.2. DEFINITIONS.

Unless otherwise noted, these terms and their definitions are for the purpose of this issuance.

 TERM                      DEFINITION

 executive position        A position in an agency that is classified above General Schedule 15
                           or equivalent position that is not required to be filled by an
                           appointment by the President by and with the advice and consent of
                           the Senate, and in which an employee:
                              Directs the work of an organizational unit;
                              Is held accountable for the success of one or more specific
                           programs or projects;
                              Monitors progress toward organizational goals and periodically
                           evaluates and makes appropriate adjustments to such goals;
                              Supervises the work of employees other than personal assistants;
                           and
                              Otherwise exercises important policy-making, policy-determining,
                           or other executive function.

 NDER                      Defined in Section 501 of E.O. 13603.

 NDER unit                 A nucleus of executive reserve personnel comprised of persons with
                           recognized expertise from various segments of the private sector and,
                           to a limited extent, from the Federal service, for the purpose of
                           performing functions during an emergency. The NDER unit is a
                           select group of individuals who are qualified on the basis of
                           managerial, professional, and technical skills, and highly expert in
                           their fields.

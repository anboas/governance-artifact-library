                             DOD INSTRUCTION 1330.04
      MILITARY SERVICES PARTICIPATION IN NATIONAL AND
             INTERNATIONAL SPORTS ACTIVITIES

Originating Component:        Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and Readiness

Effective:                    July 20, 2022

Releasability:                Cleared for public release. Available on the Directives Division Website
                              at https://www.esd.whs.mil/DD/.

                              Readiness


Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02, this issuance:
• Establishes policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for the management and
participation of Service members in Military Service sports competitions and in civilian national and
international amateur sports competitions.
•   Establishes:
    o The Senior Military Sports Advisor (SMSA),
    o The Armed Forces Sports Council (AFSC), which will manage the Armed Forces Sports
      Program.
    o The AFSC Secretariat.
    o The AFSC Working Group.

                 SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance:

    a. Applies to OSD, the Military Departments (including the Coast Guard at all times,
including when it is a Service in the Department of Homeland Security by agreement with that
Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the
Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the Department of Defense, the
Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other organizational entities within the DoD
(referred to collectively in this issuance as the “DoD Components”).

    b. Does not apply to Service member participation in sporting activities for community
outreach in accordance with Volume 1 of DoD Instruction 5410.19.

   c. Does not apply to Service member participation in professional sporting activities in
accordance with DoD Instruction 1322.22 and Directive-type Memorandum 19-011.


1.2. POLICY.

   a. The Military Services will be appropriately represented at the International Military Sports
Council, also known and referred to in this issuance as the Conseil International du Sport
Militaire (CISM), activities and in civilian national and international amateur sports
competitions.

    b. Pursuant to Section 717 of Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.), Service members may
train for, attend, and participate in amateur competitions including the Pan American Games, the
Parapan American Games, the Olympic Games, the Paralympic Games, and qualifying events
and preparatory competitions for those games. Service members may also train for, attend, and
participate in other international amateur sports competitions, including qualifying events and
preparations for those competitions, if the Secretary of State determines participation in those
competitions will serve the interests of the United States.

    c. Only sporting events conducted or sanctioned by the AFSC may use the Armed Forces
Sports logo and call the event an Armed Forces event (e.g., Armed Forces Basketball
Championship). DoD and OSD Components are not authorized to use the phrase “Armed
Forces” for any event that is related to sports or physical competition without the expressed
written consent from the AFSC. The Armed Forces Sports logo and wordmark are registered
trademarks with the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office. Other non-DoD and OSD events should
request written consent of the AFSC before titling or describing a sporting event as an Armed
Forces event and use a non-endorsement disclaimer.

   d. When selected to represent the United States in non-military national or international
amateur sports competitions as part of a team that includes civilians, Service members, to the
maximum extent feasible, will be equipped, transported, and subsisted by the sponsoring agency

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS (ASD(M&RA)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, the ASD(M&RA) provides guidance for and oversees the participation of Service
members in Armed Forces, national, and international amateur sports competitions.


2.2. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR POLICY (USD(P)).

Pursuant to Section 717 of Title 10, U.S.C., the USD(P) consults with the Department of State
(DOS) as to whether the interests of the United States will be served through participation by
Service members in any international athletic sports competition other than those mentioned in
Paragraph 1.2.b.


2.3. ASSISTANT TO THE SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PUBLIC AFFAIRS
(ATSD(PA)).

The ATSD(PA):

   a. Helps the AFSC facilitate a free flow of news and information to the news media, the
general public, and the DoD concerning the participation of Service members in Armed Forces
Sports competitions and in civilian national and international amateur sports competitions.

    b. Coordinates with the AFSC when non-DoD organizations are planning sporting events
that may include the phrase “Armed Forces” in their title.


2.4. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND THE
COMMANDANT OF THE UNITED STATES COAST GUARD.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Commandant of the United States Coast
Guard:

   a. Develop sports programs that provide the opportunity for qualified Service members to
voluntarily prepare for and compete in authorized national and international amateur sports
competitions based on specific needs and mission requirements.

   b. Designate their respective Service morale, welfare, and recreation (MWR) directors, or
another representative, as a member of the AFSC.

    c. Through the AFSC and the USD(P), request DOS determination on participation in
international sports competitions not already approved by DOS and on necessary travel
clearances.

    d. Consistent with military missions, may independently provide opportunities for qualified
Service members to prepare for and compete in authorized national and international amateur
sports competitions either on a voluntary basis or through organized programs such as a Military
Service-based world class athlete program. Expenses incurred for such purposes may not exceed
the limitation established in Section 717 of Title 10, U.S.C.


2.5. SECRETARY OF THE ARMY.

In addition to the responsibilities in Paragraph 2.4., the Secretary of the Army:

    a. Provides administrative and logistical support to the AFSC Secretariat. This includes
programming through the Program Objective Memorandum request process for the staffing of
the AFSC Secretariat office, budgeting, funding, personnel and security administration, facilities,
supplies, and other administrative provisions and services required in support of the operation
and administration of the Armed Forces Sports Program.

   b. Requires civilian and military personnel assigned to the AFSC Secretariat office to
comply with applicable personnel regulations.

   c. Exercises no independent program supervision in the execution of the operation and
administration of the Armed Forces Sports Program, outside of their role as a member of the
AFSC.

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. SMSA.

The SMSA will:

    a. Be the Chief of Personnel for a Military Service, or their designated general officer, flag
officer, or senior executive service representative assigned to a 4-year term (ending at the
completion of each Summer Olympiad) on a rotating basis in the following order:

       (1) Marine Corps.

       (2) Army.

       (3) Air Force.

       (4) Navy.

    b. Make recommendations on requests from the AFSC to the USD(P) for participation in
international sports competitions and other international travel matters relating to the sports
competitions requiring action from OSD.

   c. Approve the selection of the U.S. Armed Forces Chief of Delegation to CISM.

   d. Refer all matters requiring OSD review and approval to the ASD(M&RA).

    e. Submit comments to the ASD(M&RA) and the ATSD(PA) on legislative matters affecting
military sports activities that are of interest to the ATSD(PA) and the respective Military
Services.

  f. Review AFSC minutes to ensure compliance with DoD policies before submission to the
ASD(M&RA).


3.2. AFSC.

The AFSC will:

    a. Consist of the MWR directors of each of the Military Services or their designated
representatives. The Chair of the AFSC will rotate every 2 years on October 1 between the
Military Service MWR directors or their designated representatives in the following order:

       (1) Navy.

       (2) Air Force.

       (3) Marine Corps.

       (4) Army.

   b. Recommend for approval by the ASD(M&RA) the quadrennial apportionment among the
Military Services for funding Service member participation in civilian national and international
amateur sports competitions pursuant to Section 717 of Title 10, U.S.C., and advise the
ASD(M&RA) of the apportionment and any changes to it.

    c. Initiate requests through the SMSA to the USD(P) for DOS determinations as to whether
participation in international sports competitions not specifically listed in Paragraph 1.2.b. is in
the interests of the United States.

    d. Maintain frequent contact with the ATSD(PA) concerning Service member participation
in the Armed Forces Sports Program and provide appropriate information to that office for
traditional and social media release.

   e. Advise and act upon, as appropriate, matters pertaining to sports events involving multiple
Military Services.

    f. Recommend for approval by the ASD(M&RA) the annual Armed Forces Sports
championship events schedule and monitor all Military Service efforts in support of national and
international sports activities. The host Service plans and conducts assigned Armed Forces
Sports championship events.

   g. Coordinate and maintain liaison with the appropriate sports organizations, federations,
and NGBs on matters of mutual interest to the Military Departments and nominate Military
Service representatives to those organizations, federations, and NGBs in accordance with
DoD 5500.07-R. Representatives may not participate in the day-to-day operations of the entity.
Appointed Military Service representatives will:

        (1) Serve as DoD liaisons or representatives to the U.S. Olympic & Paralympic
Committee, NGBs, and other civilian national and international sports organizations that the
AFSC routinely engages. In cases where not all of the Military Services are represented or when
representation is limited by the civilian national or international sports organization, the AFSC
will select a representative from among those recommended by the Military Services. This
provision does not preclude individual Military Service membership in civilian national or
international sports organizations in support of the Services’ respective programs; however, prior
AFSC approval is required.

       (2) Serve as the Military Services Chief of Delegation to CISM. The length of the
appointment will be at the AFSC’s discretion.

       (3) Serve on CISM committees and commissions, or in other CISM positions.

   h. Meet quarterly or when requested by the Chair.

   i. Recommend for approval by the ASD(M&RA) funding support for Armed Forces Sports
events by:

        (1) Specifying the maximum funding each Military Service can provide to support
participation in international sports activities pursuant to Section 717 of Title 10, U.S.C. The
AFSC may allocate part of such funding for joint specialized training and participation in
international competitions.

       (2) Developing nonappropriated fund requirements for supplementary support to be
considered by the Military Services.

    j. Establish annual standard operating procedures that outline the operation and execution of
the Armed Forces Sports Program.

    k. Develop and implement performance measures for the Armed Forces Sports Program as
part of development of the annual standard operating procedures. Performance measures will, at
a minimum:

       (1) Demonstrate linkage to the program’s mission, goals, or objectives.

       (2) Have a measurable target.

       (3) Include a baseline that can be used to demonstrate program performance.

   l. Review and track performance metrics annually and update program priorities and
operating guidance as needed.


3.3. OFFICE OF THE AFSC SECRETARIAT.

The Office of the AFSC Secretariat will:

    a. Report directly to and receive direction from the AFSC in the discharge of AFSC
responsibilities, primarily as staff administrator and coordinator.

   b. Serve as the executive office for the AFSC.

   c. Serve as the U.S. liaison to CISM.

    d. Process travel clearances through the Aircraft and Personnel Automated Clearance
System to obtain country, theater, and special area clearances for Service members participating
in CISM events.

    e. Coordinate with DOS, other governmental agencies, and civilian national sports
organizations regarding sports tours and clinics in foreign countries that involve Service member
participation.

   f. Serve as the point of contact for the DoD Components on matters pertaining to the Armed
Forces Sports Program.

  g. Refer AFSC matters requiring OSD review and approval to the ASD(M&RA) through the
SMSA.

    h. Submit comments on legislative matters affecting military sports activities that are of
interest to the DoD to the ASD(M&RA) through the SMSA.

   i. Publicize Service member participation in sports programs, including the Olympic and
Paralympic Games, by providing informational material to the ATSD(PA) and the respective
Military Services.

   j. Provide agenda, meeting minutes, reports, and other administrative support as required.

    k. Transmit AFSC meeting minutes to the SMSA for review to ensure compliance with DoD

3.4. AFSC WORKING GROUP.

   a. Each Military Service will designate a representative to the AFSC Working Group.

   b. The AFSC Working Group will perform duties as directed by the AFSC.

   c. The Chair of the AFSC Working Group will be from the same Military Service as the
Chair of AFSC and will serve the same 2-year term of office as the AFSC Chair.

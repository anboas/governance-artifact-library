SUBJECT:     Geospatial-Intelligence (GEOINT) Training

References: See Enclosure 1


1. PURPOSE. This Instruction:

    a. Reissues DoD Instruction 3305.10 (Reference (a)) to establish policy and standards,
assign responsibilities, and provide procedures for the conduct of GEOINT training in
accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5105.60, DoD Directive 5143.01, and DoD
Instruction 3115.11 (References (b), (c), and (d)).

   b. Designates the National Geospatial-Intelligence College (NGC) as the GEOINT training
and educational institution of the National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA).

   c. Establishes the Community Geospatial-Intelligence Training Council (CGTC) as an
advisory body on GEOINT training.


2. APPLICABILITY. This Instruction applies to OSD, the Military Departments (including the
Coast Guard at all times, including when it is a Service in the Department of Homeland Security
by agreement with that Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff
(CJCS) and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of the Inspector General of the
Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizations entities within the DoD (hereinafter referred to collectively as the “DoD
Components”).


3. DEFINITIONS. See Glossary.


4. POLICY. It is DoD policy that the DoD shall provide GEOINT training, which shall:

   a. Be technically sound and conform to DoD GEOINT training standards.

   b. Support DoD missions.

   c. Be adequately resourced to support GEOINT mission requirements in accordance with

                                        ENCLOSURE 2

                                      RESPONSIBILITIES


1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR INTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY
(USD(I&S)). The USD(I&S), in accordance with Reference (c), shall:

    a. Establish policy and provide guidance and oversight for GEOINT training to the DoD
Intelligence Components and assign DoD GEOINT training responsibilities as necessary in
accordance with Reference (d).

    b. Review DoD GEOINT training programs and provide recommendations to the Heads of
the DoD Components.

    c. Promulgate GEOINT training guidance through appropriate issuances or memorandums to
ensure that GEOINT training issues are addressed while the program and budget are built and
during the development of supplemental resource requests. Review resource requests from
Director, NGA, and provide any additional guidance as needed.

    d. Receive and evaluate recommendations from the CGTC on policies, standards,
responsibilities, or related GEOINT matters for use in appropriate policy, oversight, and
guidance.


2. DIRECTOR, NGA. The Director, NGA, under the authority, direction, and control of the
USD(I&S), shall:

   a. Issue guidance and prescribe standards for GEOINT training.

    b. Submit GEOINT training resource requirements to the USD(I&S) for inclusion in the
various defense planning documents for the Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution
System. Provide guidance recommendations to the DoD Components during the preparations of
their budget submissions.

   c. Oversee the Director, NGC, as the principal executive for GEOINT-related training
matters and as the chair of the CGTC, as described in Enclosure 3.

   d. Coordinate with the USD(I&S) and Military Services to determine Component-specific
requirements and limitations on training and certifications based on unique mission areas.


3. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND COMMANDANT OF THE
U.S. COAST GUARD. The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Commandant of the
U.S. Coast Guard shall:

                                         ENCLOSURE 3

                   GEOINT-RELATED TRAINING AND CGTC MATTERS


1. GEOINT-RELATED TRAINING. As the NGA’s principal executive for GEOINT training,
the Director, NGC, shall:

   a. Establish:

       (1) Standards for training aligned to GEOINT tradecraft competencies.

       (2) Programs and courses for imagery analysts, geospatial analysts, cartographers, and
personnel performing imagery tasking, in coordination and collaboration with DoD Components.

       (3) Standards for geospatial information collection.

       (4) Standards for GEOINT and geospatial information processing, exploitation, and
dissemination functions.

       (5) Standards for GEOINT-training related functional management.

       (6) Standards for related fields as prescribed by Reference (c), as appropriate.

    b. Advocate, through the DoD Intelligence resource framework, GEOINT training resource
allocation to support GEOINT mission that broadens and deepens analytic expertise.

    c. In cooperation with the Secretaries of the Military Departments, Combatant Commands, and
the Commandant of the U.S. Coast Guard, establish objectives, types, and sources, of training to
include reciprocal agreements, to satisfy enterprise requirements for GEOINT training.

   d. Monitor and evaluate the performance of GEOINT curriculums within the DoD Components.

   e. Identify new GEOINT training requirements and major changes to joint GEOINT training
programs. Advise the USD(I&S) on recommended adjustments to assignments of DoD
GEOINT training responsibilities, as necessary.

  f. Maintain and operate the NGC to provide the Department of Defense, the Intelligence
Community, and other federal agencies with GEOINT training.

    g. Provide for effective collaboration and sharing of training materials and associated
software among the DoD Components.

   h. Establish and maintain American Council on Education academic accreditation for NGC
courses, as appropriate.

2. CGTC MATTERS

   a. The CGTC will:

       (1) Meet at least annually to provide a forum for GEOINT training collaboration in areas such as:

           (a) Policy.

           (b) Standards.

           (c) Responsibilities.

           (d) Emerging GEOINT training requirements.

           (e) Other related topics, as appropriate.

       (2) Provide recommendations to the USD(I&S) on GEOINT matters discussed.

   b. CGTC members will be from the DoD Components with GEOINT responsibilities and
others, as determined by the chair, including but not limited to. the National System for
Geospatial-Intelligence and the Allied System for Geospatial-Intelligence.

   c. As chair of the CGTC, the Director, NGC, shall:

       (1) Hold a meeting of the CGTC at least annually.

       (2) Provide a CGTC staff and coordinate the efforts of the CGTC.

       (3) Develop CGTC-coordinated recommendations.

       (4) Conduct periodic collaborative training site visits to DoD Components to:

           (a) Assess the GEOINT enterprise training environment.

           (b) Develop a summary of the status of GEOINT training.

           (c) Research potential improvements to the GEOINT training framework.

     (5) Recommend realignment of GEOINT training responsibilities to improve access to
GEOINT training, which may consolidate redundant activities.

       (6) Prepare an annual CGTC activities report for inclusion in the USD(I&S) Annual
Report of Intelligence and Security Training, Education, and Certification. The report shall
include a review and assessment of GEOINT enterprise training curriculums with enhancement
recommendations from CGTC outcomes and current year assessment visits.

                                       PART II. DEFINITIONS

GEOINT training. Training involving the exploitation and analysis of imagery, imagery
intelligence, and geospatial information to describe, assess, and visually depict physical features
and geographically referenced activities on the earth.

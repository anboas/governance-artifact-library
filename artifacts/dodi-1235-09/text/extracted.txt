               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments (including the United States Coast
Guard (USCG) at all times, including when it is a Service in the Department of Homeland
Security by agreement with that Department), the Office of the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of
Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of the
Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD.


1.2. POLICY.

Service members assigned to the Standby Reserve are liable for active duty pursuant to
Sections 12301 and 12306 of Title 10, United States Code (U.S.C.), and will be managed
pursuant to criteria prescribed in this issuance in accordance with Sections 10150, 10151, 10152,
10153, 12306, and 12735 of Title 10, U.S.C.

                           SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR MANPOWER AND RESERVE
AFFAIRS.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel and
Readiness, the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Manpower and Reserve Affairs:

   a. Develops policy, procedures, and guidance for the management of the Standby Reserve.

   b. Oversees DoD implementation of this issuance.

    c. Adjudicates requests from the Military Services for exceptions to the requirements in this
issuance.


2.2. SECRETARIES OF THE MILITARY DEPARTMENTS AND THE
COMMANDANT OF THE USCG.

The Secretaries of the Military Departments and the Commandant of the USCG:

    a. Prepare plans, policies, and procedures for management and mobilization of the Standby
Reserve in accordance with this issuance, including policies and procedures governing the
transfer between:

       (1) Active and inactive status in the Standby Reserve.

       (2) The Ready Reserve and the Standby Reserve.

       (3) The Standby Reserve and the Retired Reserve.

   b. Report up-to-date personnel information for members of the Standby Reserve as required
by DoD Instruction (DoDI) 7730.68, including ensuring members are classified with the correct
Reserve Component (RC) training and retirement category codes.

    c. Maintain credentialing records, including certification and licensure, for all healthcare
professionals who possess critical wartime medical skills in accordance with DoDI 6025.13 and
Volume 4 of Defense Health Agency Procedures Manual 6025.13.

    d. Maintain an active status list (ASL) and inactive status list (ISL) on Service members
assigned to the Standby Reserve.

   e. Inform members of the Standby Reserve of their service obligations, including potential
duty requirements in time of war or national emergency.

    f. Develop and maintain a list of RC Service members assigned to the Standby Reserve who
possess critical military skills that may be required in time of war or national emergency and that
will not be readily available from the Ready Reserve or the Retired Reserve.

                               SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. STANDBY RESERVE CATEGORIES.

The Standby Reserve is a category of the RC that consists of those individuals who have a
temporary disability or hardship, individuals who hold key positions in their civilian jobs, and
certain other Service members. While in the Standby Reserve, RC Service members are not
required to participate in military training and are subject to involuntary activation pursuant to
Sections 12301 and 12306 of Title 10, U.S.C. Service members placed into the Standby Reserve
will be actively managed in one of two categories:

   a. The ASL of the Standby Reserve; or

   b. The ISL of the Standby Reserve.


3.2. ASL MANAGEMENT.

   a. The ASL is composed of Service members in an active status with mobilization potential
and includes:

      (1) Service members who have been transferred from the Ready Reserve because of
temporary hardship, disability, or other clear reason with intent to return to the Ready Reserve.

       (2) Key employees in Federal Government or those designated as key employees in non-
Federal public or private employment who have been transferred from the Ready Reserve to:

           (a) Ensure the continuity of the Federal Government; or

            (b) Prevent conflicts between emergency manpower needs of civilian activities and
the military during mobilization in accordance with Section 10149 of Title 10, U.S.C., Part 44 of
Title 32, Code of Federal Regulations, and DoDI 1200.07.

        (3) Pursuant to Section 12317 of Title 10, U.S.C., theology students transferred to the
Standby Reserve for the duration of their civilian ministerial studies at accredited theological or
divinity schools.

       (4) RC officers who have completed at least 18 but fewer than 20 years of service in
accordance with Section 12646(a) and 12646(b) of Title 10, U.S.C.

        (5) Service members not listed in Paragraphs 3.2.a.(1) through 3.2.a.(4) whose retention
in the Standby Reserve is determined, by the Secretary of the Military Department concerned or
Commandant of the USCG, to be in the best interest of their Military Service. Retention on the
ASL for these Service members will be for no more than 2 years.

    b. A member of the ASL may perform military duty in accordance with Sections 12301(b)
or 12301(d) of Title 10, U.S.C., for the purpose of individual skill training in an active duty
training category as required by DoDI 1215.06.

     (1) When performing active duty for purposes of individual skill training, Service
members are entitled to retirement points and are not authorized pay or allowances.

        (2) Duties performed other than those pursuant to Section 12306 of Title 10, U.S.C., will
not be performed in an imminent danger area.

   c. A member of the ASL may be ordered to active duty in time of war or national emergency
pursuant to Section 12306 of Title 10, U.S.C.

    d. A member of the ASL in receipt of separation pay in accordance with Section 1174 of
Title 10, U.S.C., or the special separation benefits or voluntary separation incentive in
accordance with Sections 1174a and 1175 of Title 10, U.S.C., will not have those benefits
affected by their status in the Standby Reserve.

   e. A member of the ASL will not have their benefits affected by their status in the Standby
Reserve if they are in receipt of:

      (1) Involuntary separation pay in accordance with Section 1174 of Title 10, U.S.C., and
DoDI 1332.29; or

      (2) Voluntary separation pay in accordance with Section 1175a of Title 10, U.S.C., and
DoDI 1332.43.

    f. A key employee who is member of the ASL receiving separation pay, involuntary
separation pay, special separation benefits, or voluntary separation incentive benefits who
changes civilian employment status and is no longer designated as a key employee will be
retained in the Standby Reserve or transferred to the Ready Reserve, as appropriate, to fulfill any
remaining service obligation.

   g. A member of the ASL may be considered for promotion and promoted if selected.


3.3. ISL MANAGEMENT.

  a. The ISL is composed of Service members in an inactive status with no current
mobilization potential and includes:

        (1) Service members not required by law or regulation to remain in an active Reserve
status who possess requisite military skills as determined by the Secretary of the Military
Department concerned or the Commandant of the USCG that may be required for active duty in
accordance with Section 12306 of Title 10, U.S.C.

       (2) RC officers either not qualified for retention in the Ready Reserve or who fail to
conform to prescribed standards and qualifications of Section 12641 of Title 10, U.S.C.

       (3) Service members who:

            (a) Have failed to attain 50 points in any yearly period after achieving 20 qualifying
years of service for a non-regular retirement and did not apply for transfer to the Retired
Reserve.

            (b) Fail to conform to prescribed standards and qualifications of Section 12642 of
Title 10, U.S.C.

            (c) Requested transfer to the Standby Reserve in accordance with Section 12735 of
Title 10, U.S.C.

        (4) Service members with at least 20 qualifying years of service for a non-regular
retirement who have been determined to have a disability rated at less than 30 percent, and who
have been transferred to the ISL instead of separated for that disability, pursuant to Section 1209
of Title 10, U.S.C.

       (5) Service members moved from the ASL, or any Service member who is eligible for
ASL transfer and placed on the ISL pursuant to regulations prescribed by the Secretary of the
Military Department concerned or Commandant of the USCG when such action will prevent an
inequity with regard to an individual’s pay, promotion, or retirement points.

   b. An ISL member may not train for pay, receive retirement points, be eligible for
promotion, nor accrue credit for years of service, pursuant to Section 10153 of Title 10, U.S.C.

   c. An ISL member may be ordered to active duty in time of war or national emergency
declared by Congress in accordance with Section 12306 of Title 10, U.S.C., only if:

       (1) The Secretary of the Military Department concerned or Commandant of the USCG
determines that not enough qualified Service members in the Ready Reserve are available in the
required reserve categories.

       (2) The Secretary of Defense approves that determination.


3.4. STANDBY RESERVE TRANSFER.

   a. The following conditions regarding transfer to and from the Ready Reserve pertain to
Service members of the Standby Reserve.

        (1) A Standby Reserve member may be transferred back to the Ready Reserve when the
reason for transfer to the Standby Reserve no longer exists, and the Service member is otherwise
qualified.

        (2) A member of the Army National Guard of the United States or the Air National
Guard of the United States may be transferred to the Standby Reserve only with the consent of
the governor or other appropriate authority of the State, commonwealth, or territory concerned
(including the District of Columbia) pursuant to Section 10146 of Title 10, U.S.C.

3.5. MILITARY SERVICE OBLIGATION (MSO).

The Standby Reserve member is subject to the following conditions regarding fulfillment of the
MSO.

    a. In accordance with Section 10150 of Title 10, U.S.C., a Service member with an MSO,
temporarily placed in the Standby Reserve, will be transferred back to the Ready Reserve as soon
as possible if the reason for transfer to the Standby Reserve no longer exists before fulfillment of
the MSO.

    b. An RC officer assigned to the Standby Reserve who has fulfilled their MSO and has not
taken action to remain in the Ready or Standby Reserve will be advised of the requirement to
remove them from military service. The Secretary of the Military Department concerned or
Commandant of the USCG must remove an officer from service within 2 years after fulfillment
of the officer’s MSO unless the officer elects to remain in the Ready Reserve or the Standby
Reserve past their MSO.

    c. An enlisted Standby Reserve member will be discharged upon completion of their MSO
unless they elect to enter into a contractual agreement to remain in the Standby Reserve.

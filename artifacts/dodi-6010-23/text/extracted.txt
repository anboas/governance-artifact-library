Purpose: In accordance with the authority in DoD Directive 5124.02, this issuance establishes policy,
assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for:
• Implementing Section 8111 of Title 38, United States Code (U.S.C.), and Section 1104 of Title 10,
U.S.C.
• The development and operation of DoD and Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) healthcare
resource sharing agreements (hereinafter referred to as “sharing agreements”).
• Entry into agreements and contracts for the mutually beneficial coordination, use, or exchange of use
of the healthcare resources of DoD and VA with the goal of improving the access to, and quality and
cost effectiveness of, the healthcare provided by the Veterans Health Administration and the Military
Health System to the beneficiaries of both departments.

               SECTION 1: GENERAL ISSUANCE INFORMATION

1.1. APPLICABILITY.

This issuance applies to OSD, the Military Departments, the Office of the Chairman of the Joint
Chiefs of Staff and the Joint Staff, the Combatant Commands, the Office of Inspector General of
the Department of Defense, the Defense Agencies, the DoD Field Activities, and all other
organizational entities within the DoD entering into sharing agreements with the VA.


1.2. POLICY.

    a. DoD may enter into direct care sharing agreements with the VA for mutually beneficial
coordination, use, and exchange of healthcare resources that are consistent with the healthcare
responsibilities of DoD pursuant to Chapter 55 of Title 10, U.S.C.

    b. Sharing agreements must not adversely affect the range of services, the quality of care, the
established priorities for care, or the readiness and deployment capabilities of the DoD.


1.3. INFORMATION COLLECTION REQUIREMENTS.

The DoD/VA Health Care Resource Sharing Program reporting requirement referred to in
Paragraph 3.4.a. is submitted to Congress in accordance with Section 8111(f) of Title 38, U.S.C.,
and in coordination with the Office of the Assistant Secretary of Defense for Legislative Affairs.

                          SECTION 2: RESPONSIBILITIES

2.1. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR PERSONNEL AND READINESS
(USD(P&R)).

The USD(P&R):

   a. Co-chairs the DoD/VA Joint Executive Committee with the Deputy Secretary of Veterans
Affairs.

    b. Oversees development and implementation of DoD activities under the DoD/VA Joint
Strategic Plan (JSP).

   c. Oversees DoD activities in relation to the Health Executive Committee (HEC).

   d. Identifies opportunities to enhance mutually beneficial services and resources.

    e. Submits the VA-DoD Joint Executive Committee Annual Joint Report, including progress
of the JSP, to the Secretary of Defense and, in coordination with the Secretary of Veterans
Affairs.


2.2. ASSISTANT SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR HEALTH AFFAIRS (ASD(HA)).

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R), the ASD(HA):

  a. Co-chairs the DoD/VA HEC with the Under Secretary for Health, Veterans Health
Administration, in accordance with the procedures in Section 3.

   b. Oversees the cooperative efforts of DoD and VA healthcare organizations and all
committees or work groups designated by the HEC.

    c. Oversees the development and implementation of the healthcare initiatives consistent with
the DoD/VA JSP.

    d. Oversees the development, implementation, and operation of the Joint Incentives Program
DoD/VA Health Care Sharing Incentive Fund (hereinafter referred to as the Joint Incentive Fund
(JIF)) established pursuant to Section 8111 of Title 38, U.S.C.

   e. Issues guidance and oversees the DoD/VA Health Care Resource Sharing Program in
accordance with the April 20, 2020 memorandum of understanding (MOU) between the VA and
DoD.

  f. Recommends to the Secretaries of the Military Departments through the Joint Executive
Committee:

       (1) The strategic direction for policy development and implementation initiatives.

       (2) The joint coordination and sharing efforts between and within the two healthcare
organizations.

    g. Provides DoD/VA resource sharing strategic direction and policy development support to
the Director, Defense Health Agency (DHA).

    h. Assigns policy and guidance development to the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense

       (1) Monitoring the effectiveness and compliance with this issuance.

      (2) Reviewing and recommending approval or disapproval of proposed JIF program

      (3) Reviewing and recommending approval of DHA Uniform Business Office billing
   methodologies respective to the DoD/VA Health Care Resource Sharing Program.


2.3. DIRECTOR, DHA.

Under the authority, direction, and control of the USD(P&R), through the ASD(HA), the
Director, DHA:

   a. Implements the DoD/VA Health Care Resource Sharing Program while overseeing the
availability of information management and other support systems determined necessary for
program implementation and operation.

    b. Publishes resource sharing implementing procedures that direct the DHA market
organizations, standalone DoD military medical treatment facilities (MTFs), and non-MTF DHA
components entering into resource sharing agreements with the VA within 120 calendar days
following the publication of this issuance.

     c. Develops and approves departmental or interagency level agreements through the HEC or
its subordinate councils, steering committees, and work groups in accordance with DoD
Instruction (DoDI) 4000.19.

   d. Complies with the safety and occupational health requirements of joint-use facilities
specified in Paragraph 2.e of Enclosure 3 of DoDI 6055.01.


2.4. UNDER SECRETARY OF DEFENSE FOR INTELLIGENCE AND SECURITY.

The Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security:

   a. Provides overall direction and supervision for policy, program planning and execution,
and use of resources, for the protection of controlled unclassified information (CUI).

                              SECTION 3: PROCEDURES

3.1. HEC.

   a. The HEC:

        (1) Works to institutionalize DoD and VA sharing collaboration efforts to ensure the
effective and efficient use of health care services and resources.

       (2) Meets on a regular basis. The DoD HEC co-chair may call additional meetings and
executive sessions as required.

   b. DoD HEC membership includes:

       (1) ASD(HA) (Co-chair).

       (2) Principal Deputy ASD(HA).

       (3) Deputy Assistant Secretaries of Defense for Health Affairs.

       (4) Director, DHA.

       (5) Chief Information Officer, DHA.

       (6) Military Department Surgeons General.


3.2. SHARING AGREEMENTS.

    a. All newly developed, renewed or amended DoD-VA clinical resources sharing
agreements will comply with the standard billing and payment rate structure methodologies
established by the DHA Uniform Business Office VA-DoD Resource Sharing Inpatient
Calculator and Outpatient Billing Guide references as updated, and the April 20, 2020 MOU
between VA and DoD.

    b. DoD-VA resource sharing agreements that are non-clinical (e.g., administrative services,
facility space planning and usage as outlined in DoDI 6015.17, or human capital resources) are
developed in accordance with negotiated reimbursement rates between the local DoD-VA
sharing partners.

    c. Funds received from the VA pursuant to an approved resources sharing agreement will be
credited to the funds allotted for operation and maintenance of the DoD MTF involved in the
sharing agreement to the extent allowed under applicable law.

    d. VA medical facilities are designated as TRICARE network providers pursuant to Chapter
1 of Section 8.1. of the TRICARE Policy Manual 6010.60-M.

    e. All agreements between DoD and VA related to this issuance must address CUI
safeguarding requirements and information system security standards contained in
DoDI 5200.48.


3.3. NETWORK PROVIDER AGREEMENTS.

   a. In accordance with Chapter 1 of Section 8.1 of TRICARE Policy Manual 6010.60-M, VA
healthcare facilities may enter into network provider agreements directly with the managed care
support contractor (MCSC).

    b. Pursuant to network provider agreements, the VA facility is established as an authorized
participating provider. The MCSC may reimburse the VA facility for care provided to DoD
beneficiaries according to the standardized negotiated TRICARE reimbursement rate discount
established by DoD and VA.

    c. Under network provider agreements, the MCSC manages the referral process to the VA
facility for eligible DoD beneficiaries in accordance with Sections 1079 and 1086 of Chapter 55
of Title 10, U.S.C.


3.4. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.

   a. Annual Report.

   Pursuant to Section 8111(f) of Title 38, U.S.C., a report on the DoD/VA Health Care
Resource Sharing Program is due annually to Congress to coincide with the submission of the
President’s budget.

   b. External Audits.

    All DoD/VA Health Care Resource Sharing program audits conducted by external agencies,
such as the Government Accountability Office, may be coordinated through Washington
Headquarters Services’ DoD Audit Management Division in accordance with DoDI 7650.02, to
the Office of the ASD(HA) and DHA through the DHA Inspector General office. Audits
requiring participation from the Military Departments may be coordinated between Washington
Headquarters Services’ DoD Audit Management Division, through the respective Service audit
organization, and the designated external audit liaison point of contact within the Military
Departments.

   c. Other External Information Requests.

   All other requests for Resource Sharing program information from external agencies may be
coordinated through the Office of the ASD(HA).
